Best Budget Earbuds 2024: Top Wireless Earbuds & Headphones Reviewed for Sound, Battery & Value

In the crowded world of wireless audio, you don't need to spend a fortune to get great sound. The market for budget earbuds has exploded, offering features like active noise cancellation (ANC), long battery life, and impressive sound quality at a fraction of the price of premium brands. Whether you're a commuter, a gym-goer, or just looking for a reliable pair for daily use, finding the right balance of performance and cost is key.

I’ve spent weeks testing the top contenders under $100. This review compares budget earbuds and headphones across four critical areas: features, sound quality, battery life, and overall value. Let’s dive into the best options that prove you can have your music and your savings too.

Top Budget Earbuds: Feature Comparison

When shopping for budget earbuds, you’ll encounter a mix of compromises. Some prioritize battery life, others focus on comfort or water resistance. Here are the standout models in the sub-$100 category.

1. Soundcore Space A40 by Anker

Best Overall Budget Earbuds

  • Features: Adaptive ANC, multipoint connection, wireless charging, IPX4 water resistance, HearID sound personalization.
  • Sound Quality: Detailed, balanced with a slight bass boost. Clear mids and crisp highs. Excellent for pop, rock, and podcasts.
  • Battery Life: 10 hours (with ANC off) / 8 hours (with ANC on). Case provides an additional 40 hours.
  • Value: Outstanding. You get premium features like multipoint and adaptive ANC for under $80.

2. JBL Tune 230NC TWS

Best for Bass Lovers

  • Features: Active Noise Cancelling, Smart Ambient mode, JBL Pure Bass Sound, IPX4, voice assistant support.
  • Sound Quality: Punchy, aggressive bass that doesn't muddy the mids. Great for EDM, hip-hop, and rock.
  • Battery Life: 8 hours (ANC off) / 6 hours (ANC on). Case adds 24 hours.
  • Value: Good. Bass performance rivals earbuds twice the price, though ANC is average.

3. Tozo Golden X1

Best Sound Quality Under $100

  • Features: Dual drivers (dynamic + balanced armature), LDAC support, 10-band EQ, wireless charging, IPX6 water resistance.
  • Sound Quality: Exceptional clarity and detail. The balanced armature delivers crisp highs, while the dynamic driver provides deep, controlled bass. Audiophile-grade.
  • Battery Life: 7 hours (ANC off) / 5.5 hours (ANC on). Case provides 28 hours.
  • Value: Excellent for audiophiles on a budget. The LDAC codec is rare at this price point.

4. EarFun Air Pro 3

Best for Battery & Multipoint

  • Features: ANC, Bluetooth 5.3, multipoint connection, wireless charging, IPX5, QuietSmart 2.0 noise cancellation.
  • Sound Quality: Warm, spacious soundstage. Good for vocals and acoustic music. Bass is present but not overwhelming.
  • Battery Life: 9 hours (ANC on) / 12 hours (ANC off). Case adds 45 hours – best in class.
  • Value: Superb. Long battery life and solid ANC make it a top contender for travelers.

Budget Wireless Headphones: An Alternative Choice

If over-ear comfort is your priority, these budget earbuds alternatives (headphones) offer different strengths.

5. Soundcore Life Q30 by Anker

Best Budget Over-Ear Headphones

  • Features: Hybrid ANC, 3 noise-cancelling modes, foldable design, 40mm drivers, custom EQ via app.
  • Sound Quality: Well-balanced with good bass. The soundstage is wide for closed-back headphones.
  • Battery Life: 40 hours (ANC on) / 60 hours (ANC off). Incredible endurance.
  • Value: Unbeatable. ANC performance is 80% of what Sony or Bose offer at 1/5th the price.

6. Sony WH-CH720N

Best for Lightweight Comfort

  • Features: Sony's Integrated Processor V1 for ANC, DSEE upscaling, multipoint, 30mm drivers.
  • Sound Quality: Clean, natural sound with good detail. Less bass-heavy than JBL, more neutral.
  • Battery Life: 35 hours (ANC on) / 50 hours (ANC off).
  • Value: Good. Sony's ANC algorithm is top-tier, but the build feels slightly plastic.

Sound Quality Showdown: Which Budget Earbud Sounds Best?

Sound is subjective, but here’s a quick hierarchy based on pure audio performance:

  • Tozo Golden X1 – Best clarity, soundstage, and detail. The dual-driver design is a game-changer for budget earbuds.
  • Soundcore Space A40 – Best all-rounder. Balanced, fun, and customizable via HearID.
  • JBL Tune 230NC – Best for bassheads. If you love thumping low-end, this is your pick.
  • EarFun Air Pro 3 – Best for vocals and acoustic genres. Warm and non-fatiguing.

Practical Tip: Use an equalizer (EQ) app to fine-tune your budget earbuds. Most of these models have companion apps (Soundcore, Tozo, EarFun) that let you boost treble or add more bass.

Battery Life: The Real Winner

Battery life is a major differentiator. Here’s how they stack up:

  • Soundcore Life Q30 (headphones): 60 hours (ANC off) – perfect for long-haul flights.
  • EarFun Air Pro 3 (earbuds): 12 hours (ANC off) – beats most competitors by 2-3 hours.
  • Soundcore Space A40 (earbuds): 10 hours (ANC off) – solid for daily commuting.
  • Tozo Golden X1 (earbuds): 7 hours – lower, but justified by superior sound hardware.

Actionable Advice: Always check battery life with ANC on vs. off. Many budget earbuds advertise "up to 10 hours," but that's often with ANC disabled. For real-world use, expect 6-8 hours with ANC active.

Features That Matter Most for Budget Buyers

Not all features are created equal. Here’s what to prioritize:

  • Active Noise Cancellation (ANC): Worth it if you commute or work in noisy environments. The Soundcore Space A40 and EarFun Air Pro 3 have the best ANC in this price range.
  • Multipoint Connection: Connects to two devices (phone + laptop) simultaneously. A must for productivity. Available on Space A40, Air Pro 3, and Sony WH-CH720N.
  • Water Resistance: IPX4 (sweat-resistant) is fine for gym use. IPX5/IPX6 (Tozo Golden X1) is better for heavy workouts or rain.
  • Wireless Charging: Convenient but not essential. Found on Space A40, Tozo X1, and EarFun Air Pro 3.
