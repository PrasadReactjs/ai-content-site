The 7 Best Online Form Builders in 2025: Expert Picks, Pricing & Features

In today’s digital landscape, online forms are the backbone of lead generation, customer feedback, event registration, and payment collection. Whether you’re a small business owner, a marketer, or a developer, choosing the right form builder can save you hours of work and significantly boost your conversion rates. But with dozens of options on the market, how do you pick the best one for your needs?

We’ve tested and compared the top form builders based on ease of use, features, pricing, and integrations. In this article, we’ll break down the pros and cons of each tool, share practical tips for creating high-converting forms, and help you make an informed decision. Let’s dive in.

Why You Need a Dedicated Online Form Builder

While you could manually code a form or use a basic Google Form, dedicated form builders offer powerful advantages:

  • Drag-and-drop editors – No coding required.
  • Conditional logic – Show or hide fields based on user answers.
  • Payment integrations – Collect payments via Stripe, PayPal, or Square.
  • Advanced analytics – Track drop-off rates and conversion metrics.
  • Pre-built templates – Start faster with proven designs.

Using the right online forms tool can increase your form completion rate by 20–40% and automate your workflow.

Top 7 Online Form Builders Compared

1. JotForm

Best for: Beginners and small businesses who need a feature-rich, no-code solution.

JotForm is one of the most popular form builders, offering over 10,000 templates and a powerful drag-and-drop editor. Its standout feature is the extensive integration library (over 100 apps) and the ability to create multi-page forms with ease.

  • Key Features: Conditional logic, file uploads, payment gateways, signature fields, PDF forms, and mobile-friendly designs.
  • Pricing: Free plan (up to 5 forms, 100 submissions/month). Paid plans start at $34/month (Bronze) for 25 forms and 1,000 submissions. Gold plan at $49/month offers 100 forms and 10,000 submissions.
ProsCons
Huge template libraryFree plan is limited
Excellent integrationsCan be slow with complex forms
Built-in form analyticsAdvanced features require higher tiers

Recommendation: Choose JotForm if you need a versatile, beginner-friendly tool with lots of templates and integrations.

2. Typeform

Best for: Creating visually stunning, conversational forms that boost engagement.

Typeform revolutionized online forms with its one-question-at-a-time interface. It’s ideal for surveys, quizzes, and lead-generation forms where user experience matters most.

  • Key Features: Customizable themes, logic jumps, calculations, video backgrounds, and native integrations with Slack, Google Sheets, and Mailchimp.
  • Pricing: Free plan (10 questions, 10 responses/month). Essentials plan at $25/month (unlimited forms, 1,000 responses). Pro plan at $50/month (unlimited questions, 5,000 responses).
ProsCons
Beautiful, modern designLimited free plan
High conversion ratesNot ideal for long forms
Easy to embedAdvanced logic is complex

Recommendation: Use Typeform when design and user experience are your top priority.

3. Gravity Forms

Best for: WordPress users who need a powerful, self-hosted form plugin.

Gravity Forms is a premium WordPress plugin that gives you complete control over your forms. It’s widely used by developers for its extensive hooks, filters, and add-ons.

  • Key Features: Conditional logic, multi-page forms, file uploads, entry management, spam protection, and payment integrations (Stripe, PayPal).
  • Pricing: License starts at $59/year (Basic) for 1 site. Pro at $159/year includes multi-file uploads and conditional logic. Elite at $259/year adds priority support and more add-ons.
ProsCons
Deep WordPress integrationRequires WordPress site
Highly customizableNo free version
Developer-friendlySteeper learning curve

Recommendation: Ideal for WordPress site owners who want a robust, self-hosted solution with full control.

4. Wufoo

Best for: Small teams and freelancers who want a simple, reliable form builder with good reporting.

Wufoo, owned by SurveyMonkey, is known for its clean interface and strong reporting features. It’s a solid middle-ground option.

  • Key Features: Drag-and-drop builder, pre-built templates, file uploads, payment collection, and built-in reporting (charts, graphs).
  • Pricing: Free plan (5 forms, 100 entries/month). Starter at $14/month (10 forms, 500 entries). Professional at $29/month (25 forms, 2,000 entries).
ProsCons
Excellent reporting toolsLimited design customization
User-friendly interfaceFewer integrations than competitors
Affordable entry-level plansNo conditional logic on lower tiers

Recommendation: Choose Wufoo if reporting and simplicity matter more than advanced customization.

5. Cognito Forms

Best for: Businesses that need advanced conditional logic and data management.

Cognito Forms is a powerful tool that excels at complex forms with conditional calculations, data validation, and workflow automation.

  • Key Features: Unlimited fields, conditional logic, calculations, payment integrations, HIPAA compliance, and custom email notifications.
  • Pricing: Free plan (unlimited forms, 500 submissions/month). Basic at $15/month (2,500 submissions). Pro at $50/month (10,000 submissions, HIPAA).
ProsCons
Very generous free planSteeper learning curve for advanced features
Strong calculation engineInterface feels a bit dated
HIPAA compliance availableFewer design templates

Recommendation: Great for creating complex, data-heavy forms like order forms, applications, or healthcare intake forms.
