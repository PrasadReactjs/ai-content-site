The Best Online Form Builders in 2025: Features, Pricing, and Honest Reviews

Whether you're collecting leads, processing orders, or gathering feedback, online forms are the backbone of modern digital interaction. But with dozens of form builders on the market, choosing the right one can be overwhelming. In this comprehensive guide, we'll break down the top form builders based on features, pricing, ease of use, and real-world performance. By the end, you'll know exactly which tool best fits your needs—and budget.

1. Typeform: Best for Engaging, Conversational Forms

Typeform is famous for making online forms feel like a conversation. Instead of a static list of fields, users see one question at a time, which dramatically improves completion rates.

Key Features

  • Drag-and-drop builder with 100+ templates
  • Conditional logic (show/hide questions based on answers)
  • Built-in payment integrations (Stripe, PayPal)
  • Custom branding and themes
  • Advanced reporting and analytics
  • Mobile-responsive design

Pricing

  • Free: Up to 10 responses/month
  • Plus: $25/month (100 responses)
  • Business: $50/month (1,000 responses)
  • Enterprise: Custom pricing

Pros

  • Exceptional user experience—high completion rates
  • Beautiful, modern design out of the box
  • Strong logic and branching capabilities

Cons

  • Free tier is very limited (10 responses/month)
  • Can be expensive for high-volume users
  • Less suitable for complex, multi-page surveys

Best For

Marketers, sales teams, and anyone who wants to create online forms that feel personal and engaging. Typeform excels when user experience is your top priority.

2. Jotform: Best for Versatility and Templates

With over 10,000 templates, Jotform is the Swiss Army knife of form builders. It can handle everything from simple contact forms to complex order forms with payment processing.

Key Features

  • 10,000+ pre-built templates
  • Drag-and-drop form builder with 100+ widgets
  • Conditional logic and calculations
  • File uploads (up to 1 GB per file on paid plans)
  • Integration with 100+ apps (Google Sheets, Slack, Mailchimp)
  • PDF form converter
  • HIPAA compliance available

Pricing

  • Free: Up to 5 forms, 100 submissions/month
  • Bronze: $34/month (1,000 submissions)
  • Silver: $49/month (2,500 submissions)
  • Gold: $99/month (10,000 submissions)

Pros

  • Massive template library saves time
  • Very affordable for small businesses
  • Excellent file upload capabilities

Cons

  • Interface can feel cluttered
  • Some templates look dated
  • Customer support response times vary

Best For

Small to medium businesses that need a wide variety of online forms quickly. Jotform is ideal if you need to create forms for multiple use cases without learning a new tool each time.

3. Google Forms: Best Free Option

Google Forms is the most accessible form builder on the market—it's completely free with a Google account. While it lacks advanced features, it's perfect for basic data collection.

Key Features

  • Unlimited forms and responses
  • Simple question types (multiple choice, checkboxes, dropdowns)
  • Automatic response storage in Google Sheets
  • Basic conditional logic
  • Collaboration features (multiple editors)
  • Free with any Google account

Pricing

Free for all users (with a Google account). Workspace plans add branding control and advanced settings.

Pros

  • 100% free with no submission limits
  • Seamless integration with Google Workspace
  • Easy to share and collaborate

Cons

  • Very limited customization and branding
  • No payment integrations
  • Basic design—not ideal for professional use
  • No file uploads

Best For

Teachers, small teams, and anyone who needs a quick, no-cost solution for online forms. Google Forms is perfect for internal surveys, RSVPs, and simple data collection.

4. Gravity Forms: Best for WordPress Users

If you run a WordPress site, Gravity Forms is the gold standard for online forms. It's a premium plugin that offers unmatched flexibility and power.

Key Features

  • Advanced conditional logic (multi-page, multi-step)
  • File uploads with size limits
  • Payment integrations (Stripe, PayPal, Square)
  • Spam protection (reCAPTCHA, honeypot)
  • Partial entries (save and resume)
  • Post creation (create WordPress posts from form submissions)
  • Add-ons for Mailchimp, Salesforce, Zapier, and more

Pricing

  • Basic: $59/year (single site)
  • Pro: $159/year (3 sites)
  • Elite: $259/year (unlimited sites)

Pros

  • Deep WordPress integration
  • Extremely powerful conditional logic
  • Excellent developer documentation

Cons

  • Requires WordPress (not standalone)
  • Steeper learning curve for beginners
  • No free tier (but 30-day money-back guarantee)

Best For

WordPress site owners who need advanced online forms with payment processing, file uploads, and complex workflows. Gravity Forms is the go-to for membership sites and e-commerce.

5. Wufoo: Best for Simplicity and Reporting

Wufoo, owned by SurveyMonkey, focuses on making form creation and data analysis simple. Its reporting features are among the best in the industry.

Key Features

  • Drag-and-drop builder with 400+ templates
  • Customizable themes and CSS
  • Advanced reporting and charts
  • File uploads (up to 250 MB per file)
  • Payment integrations (Stripe, PayPal)
  • Conditional logic
  • API access

Pricing

  • Free: Up to 5 forms, 100 entries/month
  • Starter: $14/month (500 entries)
  • Professional: $29/month (1,000 entries)
  • Business: $79/month (5,000 entries)

Pros

  • Clean, intuitive interface
