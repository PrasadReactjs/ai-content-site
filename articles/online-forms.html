The Best Online Form Builders in 2025: Create Stunning Forms That Convert

In today's digital landscape, online forms are the backbone of lead generation, customer feedback, and data collection. Whether you're a small business owner collecting email signups, a marketer running surveys, or a developer embedding complex registration forms, choosing the right form builder can make or break your workflow. But with dozens of tools on the market, which one truly delivers the best value?

I've tested the top contenders—evaluating them on ease of use, features, pricing, and integrations. In this comprehensive guide, I'll break down the best online forms builders for 2025, complete with pros, cons, and pricing so you can pick the perfect tool for your needs.

1. Typeform – Best for Conversational Forms

Typeform revolutionized online forms by making them feel like a conversation. Instead of a static list of fields, users answer one question at a time, which dramatically improves completion rates.

Key Features

  • Drag-and-drop builder with 60+ templates
  • Conditional logic (show/hide questions based on answers)
  • Built-in payment integration (Stripe, PayPal)
  • Custom branding and themes
  • Analytics dashboard with completion rates

Pricing

  • Free: Up to 10 forms, 100 responses/month
  • Basic: $29/month (1,000 responses)
  • Plus: $59/month (10,000 responses)
  • Business: $99/month (50,000 responses)

Pros

  • High engagement – forms feel personal and interactive
  • Beautiful, modern design out of the box
  • Excellent mobile experience

Cons

  • Expensive at higher tiers
  • Limited advanced logic compared to JotForm
  • No native CRM integration on free plan

Best For

Brands that prioritize user experience and want online forms that look like a premium product. Ideal for surveys, quizzes, and lead gen.

2. JotForm – Best for Power Users

JotForm is the Swiss Army knife of online forms. With 10,000+ templates and an insane number of widgets, it can handle almost any use case—from simple contact forms to complex multi-page registrations with payment processing.

Key Features

  • 10,000+ free form templates
  • Advanced conditional logic (nested conditions)
  • 100+ integrations (Slack, Mailchimp, Google Sheets, Salesforce)
  • Form builder with drag-and-drop, CSS customization
  • HIPAA compliance (paid plans)
  • Mobile form builder app

Pricing

  • Free: 5 forms, 100 submissions/month
  • Bronze: $34/month (1,000 submissions)
  • Silver: $49/month (10,000 submissions)
  • Gold: $99/month (100,000 submissions)

Pros

  • Unmatched template library – start in seconds
  • Powerful logic and calculation widgets
  • Great for complex workflows (e.g., order forms with inventory)

Cons

  • Interface can feel cluttered
  • Free plan has JotForm branding
  • Some advanced features require coding

Best For

Businesses that need flexible, feature-rich online forms for everything from event registration to payment collection.

3. Google Forms – Best Free Option

Google Forms is the no-brainer choice for anyone who needs a simple, reliable online forms tool with zero cost. It integrates seamlessly with Google Workspace and is perfect for basic surveys, quizzes, and internal data collection.

Key Features

  • Unlimited forms and responses
  • Real-time collaboration (multiple editors)
  • Automatic response collection into Google Sheets
  • Basic conditional logic (section jumps)
  • Built-in quiz grading

Pricing

  • Free: Unlimited forms, unlimited responses
  • Google Workspace: $6/user/month (for advanced features like custom branding)

Pros

  • Completely free for most users
  • Simple, intuitive interface
  • Excellent for team collaboration

Cons

  • Limited design customization
  • No payment processing
  • Basic analytics (no funnels or drop-off reports)

Best For

Budget-conscious users, educators, and teams that need quick online forms without complexity.

4. Wufoo – Best for Ease of Use

Wufoo prides itself on being the easiest form builder to use. Its clean interface and "form builder" approach make it a favorite for non-technical users who want professional online forms fast.

Key Features

  • Simple drag-and-drop builder
  • 400+ templates
  • Built-in payment processing (Stripe, Square)
  • Conditional logic (limited)
  • Email notifications and auto-responders
  • Data export to CSV, Excel, PDF

Pricing

  • Free: 5 forms, 100 entries/month
  • Starter: $14.08/month (500 entries)
  • Professional: $29.08/month (1,000 entries)
  • Business: $82.08/month (10,000 entries)

Pros

  • Very beginner-friendly
  • Clean, professional output
  • Good value for low-volume users

Cons

  • Limited design flexibility
  • No advanced logic (e.g., calculations)
  • Fewer integrations than JotForm or Typeform

Best For

Freelancers, small businesses, and anyone who wants online forms without a learning curve.

5. Cognito Forms – Best for Complex Workflows

Cognito Forms is the hidden gem for power users who need advanced calculations, conditional logic, and even database-like features. It's ideal for insurance applications, medical intake forms, and multi-step processes.

Key Features

  • Advanced math and conditional logic
  • Repeating sections (add multiple items dynamically)
  • Role-based access control
  • Payment processing (Stripe, Square, PayPal)
  • PDF generation from form data
  • HIPAA and GDPR compliance

Pricing

  • Free: 500 entries/month, 10 forms
  • Basic: $15/month (1,000 entries)
  • Professional: $30/month (5,000 entries)
  • Enterprise: Custom pricing

Pros

  • Extremely powerful logic and calculations
  • Excellent for data-heavy applications
