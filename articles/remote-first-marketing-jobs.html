The Ultimate Guide to Finding Remote First Marketing Jobs in 2024

In a world where "remote" can mean anything from a co-working space to a mountain cabin, a new standard has emerged for digital nomads and homebodies alike: remote-first marketing jobs. Unlike traditional companies that simply allow telework as a perk, remote-first organizations build their entire culture, workflows, and hiring practices around a distributed workforce. For marketers, this is the golden ticket to true location independence, better work-life integration, and often, higher pay. If you are searching for remote first marketing jobs, this guide will show you exactly where to look, how to stand out, and what to expect.

Whether you are a content strategist, SEO specialist, social media manager, or growth marketer, the demand for talent in remote-first companies is skyrocketing. Let's dive into the curated job boards, insider tips, and actionable strategies to land your next role.

Why Remote-First Marketing Jobs Are Different

It is crucial to understand the distinction between "remote-friendly" and "remote-first." A remote-friendly company might let you work from home on Wednesdays, but their decision-making, meetings, and promotions are still centered around a physical office. In contrast, a remote-first company treats remote employees as the default. Every process—from onboarding to performance reviews—is designed for a distributed team.

For marketing professionals, this means you are judged on output, not face time. You have the freedom to work during your peak productivity hours, and you can collaborate with global teams without the friction of time zones being an afterthought. However, it also demands exceptional written communication skills, self-discipline, and the ability to build relationships through a screen.

Top Job Boards for Remote First Marketing Jobs

Finding legitimate remote first marketing jobs requires knowing the right platforms. General job boards are flooded with "remote" listings that are actually hybrid or require occasional office visits. The following boards are curated specifically for remote-first companies.

1. We Work Remotely

This is the largest remote work community on the web. Their marketing category is consistently updated with roles from companies like Buffer, Zapier, and GitLab—all pioneers of the remote-first model. You will find positions for digital marketing managers, content creators, and SEO leads. The site filters out non-remote listings, so you can apply with confidence.

2. Remote OK

Remote OK aggregates jobs from across the web but manually verifies that companies are truly remote-first. Use the "marketing" tag to narrow your search. A unique feature is the "salary filter," which lets you see only jobs that meet your compensation expectations. Many listings include equity and benefits details upfront.

3. FlexJobs

While FlexJobs requires a subscription, it is worth the investment for serious job seekers. Their team screens every listing to eliminate scams and part-time "gigs." They have a dedicated "Remote-First Company" tag, and their marketing category spans from entry-level to executive roles. The site also offers career coaching and resume reviews.

4. AngelList Talent

Startups are often remote-first by necessity, and AngelList is the hub for startup marketing roles. You can filter by "remote" and "marketing" to find positions at fast-growing companies. Many listings include salary ranges and equity percentages, making it easier to compare offers.

5. Dynamite Jobs

This board focuses on high-quality remote roles with salary transparency. Their marketing section includes roles from companies like Hotjar, Toggl, and Doist. Dynamite Jobs also hosts live hiring events where you can interview directly with hiring managers.

How to Spot a Genuine Remote-First Company

Not every company that advertises a remote job truly embraces the remote-first philosophy. Here are telltale signs of a genuine remote-first organization:

  • Asynchronous communication is the norm. They use tools like Slack, Notion, and Loom, but they don't expect instant replies. Meetings are recorded, and decisions are documented.
  • Hiring is global. They don't restrict candidates to a specific time zone or country unless the role requires it.
  • They invest in remote culture. Look for virtual team retreats, co-working stipends, and a budget for home office equipment.
  • Job descriptions emphasize outcomes over hours. Phrases like "self-starter," "results-driven," and "autonomous" are common.

If a job listing says "remote" but requires you to be in a specific city for quarterly meetings or mentions "core hours" that align with a single time zone, it may be remote-friendly rather than remote-first.

Actionable Tips for Landing Remote First Marketing Jobs

Competition for remote-first roles is fierce because the lifestyle is so desirable. To stand out, you need to tailor your approach.

Build a Digital-First Portfolio

Your resume is not enough. Create a personal website or a well-organized portfolio on platforms like Notion or Contently. Include case studies that showcase your impact—metrics like "increased organic traffic by 150% in six months" or "reduced customer acquisition cost by 30%." Remote-first companies love data-driven marketers.

Master Asynchronous Communication

During interviews, demonstrate that you can communicate clearly in writing. Send a follow-up email that summarizes your understanding of the role and the company's challenges. Use bullet points and keep it concise. This shows you can operate without constant hand-holding.

Network in Remote Communities

Join communities like Remotive's Slack group, the Remote Work subreddit, or the Women in Remote Work Facebook group. Many remote-first companies post jobs in these communities before listing them publicly. Engage genuinely—share insights, ask questions, and offer help. You'll build relationships that can lead to referrals.

Optimize Your LinkedIn Profile

Use the "Open to Work" feature but set it to "Remote" only. Add keywords like "remote-first," "distributed team," and "asynchronous" to your headline and summary. Recruiters often search for these terms. Also, request recommendations from past colleagues that highlight your ability to work independently.

Skills That Are in High Demand

To land the best remote first marketing jobs, certain skills are especially valuable:

  • SEO and Content Marketing: Remote-first companies rely heavily on organic traffic. Knowledge of tools like Ahrefs, SEMrush, and Surfer SEO is a major plus.
  • Growth Hacking: The ability to run experiments, analyze data, and scale channels without a big budget is gold for startups.
  • Automation and CRM: Familiarity with HubSpot, Marketo, or ActiveCampaign shows you can manage leads without a physical sales floor.
  • Video and Visual Content: Asynchronous communication often relies on Loom videos and recorded demos. Basic video editing skills set you apart.

Common Pitfalls to Avoid

Even experienced marketers make mistakes when applying for remote-first roles. Here are the most common:

  • Applying without research: Read the company's blog, check their remote policy page, and understand their culture. Mention something specific in your cover letter.
  • Ignoring time zone requirements: Some roles require overlap with a specific team. Be honest about your availability.
  • Overlooking the "test project": Many remote-first companies ask candidates to complete a small project. Treat it like a real client deliverable—it's often the deciding factor.

Conclusion: Your Next Step

The landscape of work has shifted permanently, and remote first marketing jobs are at the forefront of this transformation. These roles offer not just flexibility, but a genuine opportunity to build a career on your own terms. By targeting the right job boards, honing your skills, and presenting yourself as a self-sufficient communicator, you can secure a position at a company that values your output over your location.

Your call to action: Start today by updating your portfolio with one recent project that demonstrates measurable results. Then, visit the job boards listed above and apply to three remote-first marketing positions this week. With persistence and the right strategy, your next role is just a click away.
