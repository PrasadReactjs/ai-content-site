Your Ultimate Guide to Remote First Marketing Jobs: Where to Find Them & How to Land One

The shift to remote work has fundamentally changed the marketing landscape. But there’s a critical distinction between a company that allows remote work and a company that is remote-first. For marketers seeking true flexibility, autonomy, and a culture built around distributed teams, targeting remote first marketing jobs is the smartest career move you can make. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll curate the best job boards, share actionable tips for your search, and explain why a remote-first company is often a better fit than a remote-friendly one.

What Does "Remote First" Actually Mean?

Before diving into the listings, it’s essential to understand the philosophy. A remote-first company designs its entire operations around a distributed workforce. This isn’t a perk—it’s the core operating model. Key characteristics include:

  • Asynchronous communication: Meetings are recorded, decisions are documented, and work happens across time zones without pressure to be "online" 9-5.
  • Digital headquarters: There is no central office. Everything from onboarding to team retreats is built for remote collaboration.
  • Intentional culture-building: They invest in virtual team bonding, transparent communication tools, and global team meetups.
  • Equal opportunity: Remote-first companies hire talent from anywhere, not just near a physical office. This opens up a world of opportunity for marketers.

When you search for remote first marketing jobs, you are looking for roles where you won’t be an afterthought or feel isolated from the core team. You’ll be a fully integrated member of a distributed workforce.

Top Job Boards for Remote First Marketing Jobs

Not all job boards are created equal. Many "remote" listings are actually hybrid or time-zone restricted. The following platforms are curated specifically for remote-first and fully distributed companies. Bookmark these for your daily search.

1. We Work Remotely (WWR)

WWR is one of the largest and most trusted remote job boards. They have a dedicated "Marketing" category that is updated daily. Companies like Automattic, Buffer, and Zapier (all remote-first) frequently post here. Filter by "Marketing & Sales" to find roles in content marketing, SEO, growth, and brand management. This board is strict about only listing fully remote positions, making it a goldmine for remote first marketing jobs.

2. Remote OK

Remote OK aggregates jobs from across the web but manually reviews each listing to ensure it’s truly remote. Their marketing section is robust, with roles ranging from junior copywriters to VP of Marketing. Use the "No Timezone" filter to find roles that are truly asynchronous and remote-first, not just "remote for now."

3. FlexJobs

FlexJobs is a premium service (paid subscription) that vets every listing for legitimacy and remote-first policies. They have a dedicated "Marketing & Communications" category. The subscription cost is worth it because you avoid scams and time-wasting "remote-friendly" positions that later require office visits. They also offer career coaching and resume reviews tailored to remote job seekers.

4. Remotive

Remotive is a community-driven platform with a strong emphasis on remote-first culture. Their job board is curated by humans, and they offer a "Marketing" filter. Beyond listings, Remotive provides a Slack community where you can network with hiring managers and other remote marketers. This is an excellent resource for learning about company culture before applying.

5. LinkedIn (with Advanced Filters)

LinkedIn is a massive database, but you need to use it strategically. Use the "Remote" filter under location, but also add the keyword "remote-first" in your search bar. Look for job descriptions that mention "asynchronous," "distributed team," or "global team." Follow companies known for remote-first cultures like GitLab, Doist, and Toggl.

How to Identify a Genuine Remote-First Marketing Role

Not every job labeled "remote" is remote-first. Here’s how to spot the difference during your search:

  • Check the job description for time zone requirements. If it says "must work in EST hours," it’s remote-friendly, not remote-first. True remote-first roles offer flexibility.
  • Look for "async" culture. If the JD mentions "asynchronous communication," "written documentation," or "no mandatory meetings," it’s a strong sign.
  • Read the "About Us" section. Remote-first companies often have a page dedicated to their remote philosophy, including tools they use (Slack, Notion, Loom) and how they handle global collaboration.
  • Ask in the interview. "How does your company ensure remote employees feel included?" and "What percentage of your team works remotely?" are great questions.

Actionable Tips for Landing Remote First Marketing Jobs

Finding the listing is only half the battle. Here’s how to stand out as a candidate:

Optimize Your Resume for Remote Roles

Highlight your ability to work independently. Use phrases like "self-starter," "managed projects across time zones," and "proficient with async tools (Slack, Trello, Notion)." Include metrics that show you can drive results without hand-holding.

Build a Digital Portfolio

In marketing, your work speaks louder than your resume. Create a simple website or Notion page showcasing your best campaigns, writing samples, or growth metrics. Link to it in your application. Remote-first companies love seeing tangible proof of your skills.

Network in Remote Communities

Join remote work communities like Remote Work Hub, Women in Remote Work, or the Remotive Slack channel. Many remote-first marketing jobs are never posted publicly; they’re shared within these communities first. Engage genuinely, offer help, and you’ll hear about openings before they hit job boards.

Tailor Your Cover Letter

Write a brief cover letter (3-4 sentences) explaining why you specifically want a remote-first role. Mention your experience with async communication, your home office setup, and your ability to manage your own schedule. This shows you understand and value the remote-first ethos.

Prepare for Async Interviews

Many remote-first companies use asynchronous interviews (e.g., recording a video response via Loom or answering written questions). Practice being concise and clear in writing. Record yourself on Loom to ensure you come across as professional and engaging.

Examples of Remote First Marketing Jobs to Look For

Here are some common roles you’ll find when searching for remote first marketing jobs:

  • Content Marketing Manager: Create blog posts, ebooks, and social content. Often requires strong writing skills and SEO knowledge.
  • Growth Marketer: Focus on acquisition, A/B testing, and funnel optimization. Data-driven roles are very common in remote-first companies.
  • SEO Specialist: Optimize websites for search engines. This role is naturally remote-friendly because it’s largely independent.
  • Brand Strategist: Define brand voice and positioning for global audiences. Requires strong communication and cultural awareness.
  • Marketing Operations Manager: Manage tools, workflows, and analytics. A key role in keeping remote teams efficient.
  • Social Media Manager: Run campaigns across platforms. Often requires flexibility to post across time zones.
