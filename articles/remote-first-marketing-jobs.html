Your Ultimate Guide to Remote First Marketing Jobs: Where to Find Them and How to Land One

In the rapidly evolving landscape of modern employment, "remote first" has emerged as more than just a buzzword—it's a fundamental shift in how companies operate. For marketing professionals, the appeal of remote first marketing jobs is undeniable: the freedom to work from anywhere, the elimination of daily commutes, and the opportunity to collaborate with global teams. But what exactly makes a company "remote first," and how can you find these coveted roles? This comprehensive guide will walk you through everything you need to know about curating and securing remote first marketing jobs.

What Does "Remote First" Really Mean?

Before diving into the job search, it's crucial to understand the distinction between "remote-friendly" and "remote first." A remote-first company designs its entire culture, processes, and tools around a distributed workforce. Unlike companies that merely allow occasional work-from-home days, remote-first organizations treat remote work as the default. This means all meetings are conducted virtually, documentation is prioritized over verbal communication, and career advancement is equally accessible regardless of location.

For marketing professionals, this translates to a truly flexible environment where your output matters more than your physical presence. You'll find that remote first marketing jobs often come with asynchronous communication norms, generous home office stipends, and a strong emphasis on written clarity—skills that are directly transferable to marketing itself.

Why Target Remote First Marketing Jobs?

The marketing industry was among the first to embrace remote work, but not all remote roles are created equal. Here's why you should specifically seek out remote-first companies:

  • True flexibility: You won't be penalized for not being in an office, and you can structure your day around peak creative hours.
  • Global collaboration: Work alongside marketers from different time zones and cultures, gaining diverse perspectives that strengthen your campaigns.
  • Career growth: Remote-first companies invest heavily in professional development, knowing that connection must be intentional.
  • Better compensation: Many remote-first companies adjust salaries based on market rates rather than physical location, often leading to higher pay.

Top Job Boards for Remote First Marketing Jobs

While general job boards like LinkedIn and Indeed list some remote positions, dedicated platforms offer a more curated experience. Here are the best resources for finding remote first marketing jobs:

1. Remote-First Specific Platforms

  • RemoteOK – A highly curated board with a strong marketing category. Companies pay to list, ensuring quality over quantity.
  • We Work Remotely – One of the largest remote job boards, with a dedicated "Marketing" section updated daily.
  • FlexJobs – A paid service that vets every listing, ensuring you avoid scams and find legitimate remote-first opportunities.

2. Niche Marketing Communities

How to Curate Your Own Remote First Marketing Job Search

Rather than passively applying to every "remote" listing, take a strategic approach to curating opportunities that truly align with remote-first values:

Step 1: Define Your "Remote First" Criteria

Not all remote roles are remote-first. Look for these indicators in job descriptions and company websites:

  • Mentions of "asynchronous work" or "async-first culture"
  • No requirement for specific time zone overlap (unless client-facing)
  • Explicit budget for home office equipment or coworking spaces
  • Transparent salary ranges regardless of location
  • Team photos showing people in different settings, not just an office

Step 2: Use Advanced Search Techniques

When using general job boards, refine your search with these keywords:

  • "remote first marketing"
  • "distributed marketing team"
  • "work from anywhere marketing"
  • "async marketing"

Boolean search strings can also help: "remote" AND ("marketing" OR "growth") AND ("first" OR "native" OR "distributed")

Step 3: Network in Remote-First Communities

Many remote first marketing jobs are filled through referrals. Join these communities:

  • Remote Work & Digital Nomad Facebook groups – Look for groups with active job boards.
  • Nomad List – A paid community for remote workers, with job listings and city guides.
  • Remote Women in Marketing – A supportive Slack community with job shares.
  • LinkedIn groups – Search for "remote first marketing" and join active conversations.

Crafting an Application That Stands Out

Applying for remote first marketing jobs requires a slightly different approach than traditional roles. Here's how to tailor your application:

Highlight Your Remote-Ready Skills

  • Asynchronous communication: Show examples of how you've managed projects without real-time meetings.
  • Self-motivation: Provide metrics that demonstrate your ability to work independently (e.g., "Managed 10+ campaigns with zero supervision").
  • Tech proficiency: Mention tools like Slack, Notion, Asana, Zoom, and Loom—the staples of remote-first teams.

Address the "Remote" Elephant in the Room

In your cover letter, proactively address how you'll succeed in a remote-first environment. For example:

"I thrive in asynchronous environments. In my last role, I coordinated a product launch across three time zones using Loom videos for updates and Notion for documentation. I believe clear written communication is the backbone of successful remote marketing."

Prepare for Remote-Specific Interviews

Many remote-first companies use unique interview methods:

  • Asynchronous video interviews – You'll record answers to prompts.
  • Paid test projects – A short, paid assignment to demonstrate your skills.
  • Written assessments – Tests of your ability to communicate clearly in writing.

Red Flags to Avoid

Not every "remote" job is a good fit. Watch out for these warning signs:

  • Vague language like "remote-friendly but prefer local"
  • No mention of remote work policies or tools
  • Expectation of 9-5 hours in a specific time zone
  • No budget for equipment or professional development
  • High turnover in the marketing department

Actionable Tips for Landing Remote First Marketing Jobs

Based on insights from hiring managers at remote-first companies, here are practical strategies:

  • Build a digital portfolio: Use a tool like Carrd or Notion to create a simple portfolio showcasing campaigns, metrics, and your remote workflow.
  • Get certified in remote tools: Free certifications from HubSpot, Google Analytics, and Asana can boost your credibility.
  • Write a "Remote Work Manifesto": A short document outlining your remote work philosophy can be a powerful differentiator.
  • Apply during their hiring windows: Many remote-first companies batch hiring in specific months. Follow their blogs and social media for announcements.
