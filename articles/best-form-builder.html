The Ultimate Guide to Finding the Best Form Builder in 2024 (Pricing, Features & Honest Reviews)

In today’s digital-first world, forms are the backbone of lead generation, customer feedback, event registration, and countless other business processes. Whether you're a solopreneur building your email list or a marketing team managing complex workflows, choosing the best form builder can make or break your conversion rates. With dozens of tools on the market, each boasting unique features, it’s easy to get overwhelmed.

That’s where this guide comes in. We’ve tested the top contenders, analyzed their pricing, features, and user experiences, and narrowed down the choices to help you find the best form builder for your specific needs. Let’s dive into the details so you can make an informed decision—and start capturing more leads today.

What Makes a Great Form Builder? Key Features to Look For

Before we compare specific tools, let’s establish the criteria that define the best form builder for most users. A top-tier form builder should offer:

  • Drag-and-drop editor: No coding required. You should be able to create beautiful forms in minutes.
  • Customizable templates: Pre-designed templates for common use cases (contact forms, surveys, order forms, etc.) save time.
  • Conditional logic: Show or hide fields based on user responses to create dynamic, user-friendly forms.
  • Integration capabilities: Seamless connections with email marketing platforms (Mailchimp, ConvertKit), CRMs (Salesforce, HubSpot), payment gateways (Stripe, PayPal), and analytics tools (Google Analytics).
  • Mobile responsiveness: Forms must look and work perfectly on smartphones and tablets.
  • Data security & compliance: GDPR, CCPA, and HIPAA compliance (if needed) are non-negotiable for many businesses.
  • Analytics & reporting: Track form views, submissions, and conversion rates to optimize performance.

With these criteria in mind, let’s review the top contenders for the title of best form builder.

Top 5 Best Form Builders Compared

1. Typeform – Best for User Experience & Engagement

Typeform is widely regarded as the best form builder for creating conversational, visually stunning forms. Its one-question-at-a-time format keeps users engaged and boosts completion rates.

Pricing:

  • Free plan: Up to 10 forms, 100 responses/month, basic features.
  • Plus plan: $25/month (billed annually) – 1 user, unlimited forms, 1,000 responses/month, remove Typeform branding.
  • Business plan: $50/month – 3 users, 10,000 responses/month, integrations, custom subdomain.
  • Enterprise: Custom pricing for large teams with advanced security.

Pros:

  • Beautiful, modern design that stands out.
  • High engagement rates due to conversational flow.
  • Strong integration library (Slack, Google Sheets, HubSpot, etc.).
  • Excellent mobile experience.

Cons:

  • Limited form logic on lower-tier plans.
  • Can be expensive for high-volume submissions.
  • Not ideal for complex, multi-page forms.

Best for: Marketers, content creators, and businesses focused on brand experience and high conversion rates.

2. Jotform – Best for Power Users & Advanced Features

If you need robust functionality without breaking the bank, Jotform is a strong candidate for the best form builder. It offers a massive template library and powerful conditional logic.

Pricing:

  • Free plan: 5 forms, 100 submissions/month, 100 MB storage.
  • Bronze plan: $34/month – 25 forms, 1,000 submissions/month, 10 GB storage.
  • Silver plan: $49/month – 50 forms, 10,000 submissions/month, 100 GB storage.
  • Gold plan: $99/month – 100 forms, 100,000 submissions/month, 1 TB storage.
  • Enterprise: Custom pricing.

Pros:

  • Over 10,000 templates for every use case.
  • Advanced conditional logic and widgets (e.g., signature fields, file uploads).
  • Integration with 100+ apps via native connectors and Zapier.
  • HIPAA compliance available on higher tiers.

Cons:

  • Interface can feel cluttered compared to Typeform.
  • Free plan is very limiting.
  • Some users report slower load times for complex forms.

Best for: Small to medium businesses, agencies, and users who need extensive customization and integrations.

3. Gravity Forms – Best for WordPress Users

For anyone running a WordPress site, Gravity Forms is often considered the best form builder plugin. It’s a premium plugin that integrates deeply with the WordPress ecosystem.

Pricing: (Annual licenses for one site)

  • Basic: $59/year – 1 site, 25 form fields, basic add-ons.
  • Pro: $159/year – 1 site, unlimited fields, 20+ add-ons (including Mailchimp, Stripe).
  • Elite: $259/year – 1 site, all add-ons, priority support.

Pros:

  • Seamless WordPress integration (works with WooCommerce, Membership plugins).
  • Highly extensible with hooks, filters, and third-party add-ons.
  • Excellent conditional logic and multi-page forms.
  • No monthly fees—just annual renewal.

Cons:

  • Requires WordPress (not a standalone solution).
  • Steeper learning curve for non-developers.
  • No free plan (but has a 30-day money-back guarantee).

Best for: WordPress site owners who need powerful, customizable forms and are comfortable with a bit of technical setup.

4. Google Forms – Best Free Option

If your budget is $0 and your needs are basic, Google Forms is the best form builder for simplicity and collaboration. It’s part of Google Workspace and completely free.

Pricing: Free with a Google account (no paid tiers).

Pros:

  • 100% free with unlimited forms and responses.
  • Easy to use—no learning curve.
  • Real-time collaboration with team members.
  • Automatic integration with Google Sheets for data analysis.

Cons:

  • Very limited customization and design options.
  • No conditional logic beyond basic “go to section.”
  • No payment collection or advanced integrations.
  • Google branding is always visible.

Best for: Internal surveys, simple RSVPs, educational purposes, and users with no design or integration needs.
