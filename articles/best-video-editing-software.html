The Ultimate Guide to Finding the Best Video Editing Software in 2024

Whether you're a budding YouTuber, a professional filmmaker, or someone who just wants to create polished home videos, finding the best video editing software can feel overwhelming. With dozens of options ranging from free tools to professional suites, choosing the right one depends on your skill level, budget, and specific needs.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll break down the top video editing software on the market, compare their pricing and features, and help you decide which one is right for you. By the end, you'll have all the information you need to make an informed purchase—and we’ve included affiliate links so you can get started right away.

What to Look for in the Best Video Editing Software

Before diving into specific tools, it’s important to understand what makes a video editor "the best" for you. Here are the key factors to consider:

  • Ease of use: A steep learning curve can kill your motivation. Look for intuitive interfaces, especially if you're a beginner.
  • Features & tools: Multi-track editing, color grading, audio effects, transitions, and support for 4K/8K video are essential for professional results.
  • Performance: The software should run smoothly on your computer without constant crashes or lag.
  • Export options: Direct upload to YouTube, Vimeo, or social media platforms saves time.
  • Pricing: Whether you prefer a one-time purchase or a subscription, the cost should fit your budget.
  • Platform compatibility: Mac, Windows, or both? Some tools even work on Linux or mobile devices.

Top 7 Best Video Editing Software (Ranked & Reviewed)

1. Adobe Premiere Pro – The Industry Standard

Best for: Professional editors, advanced users, and teams.

Pricing: Subscription only – $22.99/month (individual) or $54.99/month (Creative Cloud All Apps).

Key Features:

  • Multi-cam editing for interviews and events
  • Advanced color grading with Lumetri Color tools
  • Seamless integration with After Effects, Audition, and other Adobe apps
  • Support for VR, 360-degree video, and HDR
  • Team collaboration via shared projects

Pros: Incredibly powerful, huge community support, constant updates, and endless third-party plugins.

Cons: Subscription-only pricing can be expensive over time; steep learning curve for beginners.

Verdict: If you're serious about video editing as a career or business, Premiere Pro is the best video editing software for its versatility and industry adoption. Check the latest price on Adobe’s site.

2. Final Cut Pro X – The Mac Powerhouse

Best for: Mac users who want professional results without a subscription.

Pricing: One-time purchase – $299.99 (free trial available).

Key Features:

  • Magnetic Timeline for easy clip management
  • Optimized for Apple Silicon (M1/M2) for blazing-fast performance
  • Advanced color grading, motion graphics, and audio editing
  • Built-in proxy workflow for 4K/8K
  • Compressor and Motion apps sold separately for added functionality

Pros: One-time payment, incredibly fast rendering, intuitive interface for Mac users, excellent for 4K editing.

Cons: Mac-only; limited collaboration features compared to Adobe.

Verdict: For Mac users, this is arguably the best video editing software in terms of value and performance. Get Final Cut Pro X now.

DaVinci Resolve – Best Free Option (With a Pro Upgrade)

Best for: Colorists, intermediate to advanced editors, and budget-conscious creators.

Pricing: Free version (DaVinci Resolve) or $295 one-time (DaVinci Resolve Studio).

Key Features:

  • Industry-leading color grading tools (the best in the business)
  • Full editing, visual effects, motion graphics, and audio post-production in one app
  • Free version includes almost all features (only a few advanced effects are locked)
  • Support for up to 120fps and 4K (free) or 8K (Studio)
  • Multi-user collaboration

Pros: Free version is incredibly generous; professional-grade color grading; no subscription.

Cons: Steep learning curve; requires a powerful computer; interface can be cluttered.

Verdict: If you're on a budget but want Hollywood-level color tools, DaVinci Resolve is the best video editing software for the price. Download the free version or upgrade to Studio.

4. iMovie – Best Free Software for Beginners (Mac/iOS)

Best for: Complete beginners, Mac and iOS users, quick projects.

Pricing: Free (included with Apple devices).

Key Features:

  • Drag-and-drop interface with pre-built templates
  • Trailers and movie themes
  • 4K support
  • Easy sharing to YouTube and social media

Pros: Completely free, extremely easy to learn, great for quick edits.

Cons: Very limited features; no advanced color grading or audio tools; only works on Apple devices.

Verdict: Perfect for beginners dipping their toes into video editing, but not the best video editing software for serious projects. Learn more about iMovie.

5. Filmora – Best for Beginners & Intermediate Users on Windows/Mac

Best for: Content creators, educators, and social media managers who want a balance of ease and features.

Pricing: $49.99/year or $79.99 lifetime (one-time purchase). Free trial with watermark.

Key Features:

  • Intuitive drag-and-drop timeline
  • Built-in effects, transitions, and titles (hundreds of options)
  • Audio ducking, color tuning, and speed control
  • Screen recording and split-screen options
  • Export to multiple formats and direct social media upload

Pros: Very user-friendly, affordable lifetime license, regular updates, good for beginners.

Cons: Advanced features are limited compared to Premiere or DaVinci; some users report slower performance with large projects.

Verdict: One of the best video editing software options for beginners who want more than basic tools without breaking the bank. Try Filmora free or buy the lifetime license.
