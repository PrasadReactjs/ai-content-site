The Ultimate Guide to the Best Video Editing Software in 2024

Whether you're a content creator, a budding filmmaker, or a business owner looking to polish your marketing videos, finding the best video editing software can make or break your workflow. With dozens of options on the market—from free tools to professional suites—it's easy to feel overwhelmed. In this comprehensive guide, we break down the top contenders, compare their features and pricing, and help you choose the perfect tool for your skill level and budget.

What Makes the Best Video Editing Software?

Before diving into specific programs, it's important to understand what separates a good editor from a great one. The best video editing software should offer:

  • Intuitive interface: A clean, customizable workspace that doesn't bog you down.
  • Performance: Smooth playback and fast rendering, even with 4K or 8K footage.
  • Feature set: Essential tools (trimming, transitions, audio editing) plus advanced options (color grading, motion graphics, multi-cam editing).
  • Export flexibility: Support for various formats and direct uploads to platforms like YouTube, Vimeo, and social media.
  • Value for money: Pricing that matches the features and support provided.

Top 5 Best Video Editing Software: Detailed Reviews

1. Adobe Premiere Pro

Best for: Professionals and advanced creators who need a complete ecosystem.

Adobe Premiere Pro remains the industry standard for film, TV, and web content. It integrates seamlessly with other Adobe apps like After Effects, Photoshop, and Audition, making it a powerhouse for complex projects.

  • Pricing: $22.99/month (single app) or $59.99/month (Creative Cloud suite). No free version, but a 7-day free trial is available.
  • Key Features: Multi-camera editing, advanced color grading (Lumetri), AI-powered auto-reframe, motion graphics templates, and extensive third-party plugin support.
  • Pros: Unmatched flexibility; huge community and tutorials; regular updates with new features.
  • Cons: Subscription-only model; steep learning curve for beginners; can be resource-heavy on older computers.

Recommendation: If you're a serious content creator or work in a professional environment, Premiere Pro is worth the investment. Pair it with a powerful PC or Mac for the best experience.

2. DaVinci Resolve

Best for: Colorists and budget-conscious creators who want professional-grade tools for free.

DaVinci Resolve by Blackmagic Design is a rare gem: a fully-featured video editor that offers a robust free version. It's particularly famous for its color grading capabilities—the same tools used in Hollywood blockbusters.

  • Pricing: Free (DaVinci Resolve) or $295 (DaVinci Resolve Studio). One-time purchase for the Studio version.
  • Key Features: Advanced color wheels and curves, node-based color grading, Fairlight audio post-production, Fusion visual effects, and support for 8K and HDR.
  • Pros: Best color grading tool on the market; free version is incredibly capable; no watermark; excellent audio editing built-in.
  • Cons: Steep learning curve (especially for color grading); interface can feel cluttered; free version lacks some AI features and noise reduction.

Recommendation: Ideal for YouTubers, indie filmmakers, and anyone who wants to learn professional color grading without spending a dime. The Studio version is a one-time purchase that's cheaper than a year of Premiere Pro.

Apple's Final Cut Pro is a favorite among Mac-based editors for its blazing-fast performance and magnetic timeline. It uses Metal and Core Image to take full advantage of Apple hardware.

  • Pricing: $299.99 (one-time purchase from the Mac App Store). Free 90-day trial available.
  • Key Features: Magnetic Timeline 2, 360° VR editing, HDR support, advanced color grading, and built-in motion graphics (Motion app sold separately).
  • Pros: Extremely fast rendering and playback; intuitive interface for Mac users; no subscription; excellent for 4K/8K projects.
  • Cons: Mac-only; limited third-party plugin ecosystem compared to Premiere; fewer collaboration features for teams.

Recommendation: If you're a Mac user and don't need cross-platform compatibility, Final Cut Pro offers the best performance per dollar. It's especially great for solo creators and small studios.

4. Filmora

Best for: Beginners and social media content creators who want simplicity and effects.

Wondershare Filmora is designed for ease of use. It's perfect for those who want to create polished videos quickly without a steep learning curve. It includes a library of built-in effects, transitions, and titles.

  • Pricing: $49.99/year (annual plan) or $79.99 (lifetime license). Free version with a watermark.
  • Key Features: Drag-and-drop interface, keyframing, split-screen, motion tracking, audio ducking, and over 1,000 effects.
  • Pros: Very beginner-friendly; large library of built-in assets; affordable pricing; regular updates.
  • Cons: Limited advanced features (no advanced color grading, fewer audio controls); export times can be slow; free version adds a watermark.

Recommendation: Great for vloggers, social media managers, and anyone who needs quick, attractive videos. The lifetime license is a solid value if you plan to use it long-term.

5. iMovie

Best for: Absolute beginners and Mac/iOS users who need a free, simple editor.

iMovie comes free on every Mac and iOS device. While it's basic, it's surprisingly capable for simple projects like home videos, school projects, or quick social media clips.

  • Pricing: Free (included with Apple devices).
  • Key Features: Drag-and-drop timeline, built-in themes and transitions, basic color correction, and direct sharing to YouTube and Vimeo.
  • Pros: Completely free; no learning curve; works seamlessly with Apple ecosystem; good for 4K video.
  • Cons: Very limited features (no multi-cam, no advanced color grading, no motion tracking); only available on Apple devices.

Recommendation: A great starting point for complete beginners. Once you outgrow it, you can upgrade to Final Cut Pro or another advanced tool.

How to Choose the Best Video Editing Software for You

Still unsure? Here's a quick decision guide based on your primary needs:

  • You're a professional filmmaker or editor: Choose Adobe Premiere Pro for its ecosystem and industry acceptance, or DaVinci Resolve Studio for superior color grading.
  • You're a YouTuber or content creator on a budget: Start with DaVinci Resolve (free) or Filmora for its ease of use.
  • You're a Mac user who values speed: Final Cut Pro is the fastest and most optimized option.
  • You're a complete beginner: Use iMovie to learn the basics, then graduate to Filmora or DaVinci Resolve.
