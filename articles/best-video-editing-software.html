The Ultimate Guide to the Best Video Editing Software in 2024

Whether you're a budding YouTuber, a professional filmmaker, or a social media content creator, finding the best video editing software can make or break your workflow. With countless options on the market—ranging from free tools to professional suites—choosing the right one can feel overwhelming. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll break down the top video editing software based on pricing, features, ease of use, and performance. By the end, you’ll have a clear winner for your specific needs.

What Makes the Best Video Editing Software?

Before diving into reviews, let’s define what “best” means. The ideal video editor should balance powerful features with an intuitive interface. Key factors include:

  • Performance: Smooth playback and fast rendering, even with 4K or 8K footage.
  • Features: Multi-track editing, color grading, special effects, audio tools, and export options.
  • Pricing: Value for money—whether one-time purchase, subscription, or free.
  • Support: Tutorials, community forums, and customer service.
  • System Compatibility: Windows, macOS, or Linux support.

The Top 5 Best Video Editing Software in 2024

1. Adobe Premiere Pro – Best Overall for Professionals

Pricing: $22.99/month (Creative Cloud subscription) | Free 7-day trial available.

Key Features:

  • Industry-standard timeline editing with unlimited tracks
  • Advanced color grading with Lumetri Color tools
  • Seamless integration with After Effects, Photoshop, and Audition
  • AI-powered features like Auto Reframe and Speech to Text
  • Supports all major formats (4K, 8K, VR, 360° video)

Pros:

  • Extremely powerful and customizable
  • Huge library of plugins and third-party extensions
  • Regular updates with new features

Cons:

  • Subscription-only model can be expensive long-term
  • Steep learning curve for beginners
  • Requires a powerful computer for smooth performance

Best for: Professional editors, film studios, and advanced content creators who need a full-fledged suite.

2. Final Cut Pro – Best for Mac Users

Pricing: $299.99 (one-time purchase) | Free 90-day trial available.

Key Features:

  • Magnetic Timeline for easy clip organization
  • Optimized for Apple Silicon (M1/M2/M3) for blazing-fast performance
  • Advanced color grading, motion graphics, and multicam editing
  • Built-in 360° video editing and HDR support
  • Works seamlessly with Motion and Compressor

Pros:

  • One-time purchase with no subscription fees
  • Extremely fast rendering and smooth playback
  • Intuitive interface for Mac users

Cons:

  • Only available on macOS
  • Less third-party plugin support compared to Premiere Pro
  • Limited collaboration features

Best for: Mac-based creators, YouTubers, and indie filmmakers who want a powerful, one-time investment.

3. DaVinci Resolve – Best Free Option with Professional Color Grading

Pricing: Free (DaVinci Resolve) or $295 (DaVinci Resolve Studio one-time purchase).

Key Features:

  • Industry-leading color correction and grading tools
  • Professional editing, visual effects, and audio post-production (Fairlight)
  • Supports up to 4K on free version; 8K and 120fps on Studio version
  • Multi-user collaboration tools (Studio only)
  • Node-based workflow for advanced compositing

Pros:

  • Free version is incredibly powerful and not crippled
  • Best color grading tools in its class
  • All-in-one solution (edit, color, audio, VFX)

Cons:

  • Steep learning curve, especially for color grading
  • Requires a powerful GPU for smooth performance
  • Free version lacks some advanced features (noise reduction, HDR)

Best for: Colorists, filmmakers on a budget, and anyone who wants professional-grade tools for free.

4. Wondershare Filmora – Best for Beginners and Social Media Creators

Pricing: $49.99/year (annual plan) or $79.99 (lifetime license) | Free trial with watermark.

Key Features:

  • Drag-and-drop interface with pre-built templates and effects
  • AI-powered tools like Auto Beat Sync, motion tracking, and background removal
  • Built-in stock media library (audio, video, stickers)
  • Direct export to YouTube, Vimeo, and social media platforms
  • Support for 4K editing and screen recording

Pros:

  • Very beginner-friendly with minimal learning curve
  • Affordable pricing with lifetime option
  • Large library of effects and transitions

Cons:

  • Limited advanced features for professional work
  • Free version adds a watermark to exports
  • Performance can lag with complex projects

Best for: Beginners, social media influencers, and casual editors who want quick, polished results.

5. CyberLink PowerDirector – Best for Windows Users on a Budget

Pricing: $49.99 (Ultra) to $99.99 (Ultimate) one-time purchase | Free trial available.

Key Features:

  • Fast rendering with TrueVelocity engine
  • AI-powered tools: motion tracking, color correction, and style transfer
  • Multi-cam editing and 360° video support
  • Built-in screen recorder and video capture
  • Extensive library of effects and transitions

Pros:

  • One-time purchase with no subscription
  • Excellent performance on mid-range PCs
  • Feature-rich for the price

Cons:

  • Interface can feel cluttered
  • Less third-party plugin support
  • Customer support can be slow

Best for: Windows users who want a powerful, affordable editor without ongoing costs.

How to Choose the Best Video Editing Software for Your Needs

With so many options, here’s a quick decision guide:

  • For professionals: Go with Adobe Premiere Pro for its industry-standard features and integration.
  • For Mac users: Final Cut Pro offers unbeatable performance and a one-time price.
  • For budget-conscious creators: DaVinci Resolve (free) is unbeatable for color grading and editing.
  • For beginners: Wondershare Filmora is the easiest to learn and use.
  • For Windows users: CyberLink PowerDirector provides excellent value for the price.
