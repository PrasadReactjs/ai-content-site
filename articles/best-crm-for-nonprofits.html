The Best CRM for Nonprofits: Top Solutions Compared (2025 Pricing & Features)

Managing donor relationships, tracking volunteers, and streamlining fundraising efforts are critical for any nonprofit. But without the right tools, these tasks can become overwhelming. A dedicated best CRM for nonprofits can transform how your organization operates, helping you build stronger connections and maximize your impact.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll compare the top CRM platforms built specifically for nonprofits. We’ll break down pricing, features, pros and cons, and give you clear recommendations so you can choose the best CRM for your organization’s unique needs.

Why Nonprofits Need a Specialized CRM

Generic CRMs like Salesforce or HubSpot are powerful, but they’re often overkill—or worse, missing key features nonprofits rely on. A dedicated best CRM for nonprofits includes:

  • Donor management and segmentation
  • Fundraising and campaign tracking
  • Volunteer management tools
  • Grant management capabilities
  • Nonprofit-specific pricing (often discounted or free)
  • Integration with donation platforms like PayPal, Stripe, or GiveWP

Using the wrong CRM can waste time, money, and donor goodwill. Let’s explore the top contenders.

Top 5 Best CRM for Nonprofits in 2025

1. Bloomerang – Best for Donor Retention

Best for: Mid-sized nonprofits focused on donor retention and engagement.

Pricing: Starts at $99/month (billed annually) for up to 1,000 records. Discounts available for nonprofits with 501(c)(3) status.

Key Features:

  • Donor retention analytics and scoring
  • Automated email marketing and drip campaigns
  • Volunteer management module
  • Gift and pledge tracking
  • Mobile app for on-the-go access
  • Integration with QuickBooks, Mailchimp, and 1,000+ apps via Zapier

Pros:

  • Intuitive, user-friendly interface
  • Excellent donor retention tools
  • Strong reporting and analytics
  • Great customer support

Cons:

  • Limited advanced automation compared to enterprise CRMs
  • No built-in grant management
  • Higher cost for larger databases

Recommendation: Bloomerang is our top pick for the best CRM for nonprofits focused on building long-term donor relationships. It’s especially strong for organizations that rely on recurring donations and need to reduce churn.

2. Salesforce Nonprofit Cloud – Best for Scalability

Best for: Large nonprofits with complex needs and dedicated IT support.

Pricing: Free for up to 10 users with the Nonprofit Success Pack (NPSP). Paid plans start at $25/user/month for additional features.

Key Features:

  • Complete donor and constituent management
  • Program and case management
  • Volunteer and grant tracking
  • Powerful automation with Flow Builder
  • Extensive app marketplace (AppExchange)
  • AI-powered insights with Einstein Analytics

Pros:

  • Highly customizable and scalable
  • Free tier for small teams
  • Massive ecosystem of integrations
  • Industry-leading security

Cons:

  • Steep learning curve
  • Requires dedicated admin or consultant
  • Can become expensive with add-ons
  • Overkill for small nonprofits

Recommendation: If you have a dedicated IT team and need a platform that grows with you, Salesforce Nonprofit Cloud is the best CRM for nonprofits at scale. Start with the free NPSP and upgrade as needed.

3. Neon CRM – Best All-in-One Solution

Best for: Small to mid-sized nonprofits wanting an all-in-one fundraising and CRM platform.

Pricing: Starts at $99/month for up to 1,000 contacts. Includes fundraising tools, event management, and website integration.

Key Features:

  • Built-in donation forms and event registration
  • Membership management
  • Email marketing with templates
  • Peer-to-peer fundraising
  • Customizable dashboards and reports
  • Integrated payment processing

Pros:

  • All-in-one platform (no need for separate tools)
  • User-friendly setup
  • Strong event management features
  • Good value for the price

Cons:

  • Limited customization compared to Salesforce
  • Reporting can be less flexible
  • Some users report occasional performance issues

Recommendation: For nonprofits that want to replace multiple tools with one platform, Neon CRM is a strong contender for the best CRM for nonprofits seeking simplicity and integration.

4. Little Green Light – Best for Budget-Conscious Nonprofits

Best for: Small nonprofits with tight budgets and straightforward needs.

Pricing: Starts at $49/month for up to 1,000 records. No long-term contract required.

Key Features:

  • Donor and gift tracking
  • Basic email marketing (via Mailchimp integration)
  • Event management
  • Custom fields and tags
  • Simple reporting
  • Mobile-friendly interface

Pros:

  • Affordable entry point
  • Easy to learn and use
  • No setup fees
  • Good for organizations with limited staff

Cons:

  • Limited automation and advanced features
  • No built-in email marketing (requires integration)
  • Less suitable for large databases
  • Reporting is basic

Recommendation: If you’re a small nonprofit with a limited budget, Little Green Light is the best CRM for nonprofits that need a no-frills, reliable system. It’s perfect for getting started without breaking the bank.

5. DonorPerfect – Best for Fundraising Campaigns

Best for: Nonprofits of all sizes that prioritize fundraising and campaign management.

Pricing: Starts at $99/month for up to 1,000 records. Custom pricing for larger organizations.

Key Features:

  • Advanced donor segmentation
  • Campaign tracking and ROI analysis
  • Online donation forms and peer-to-peer fundraising
  • Volunteer management
  • Grant management module
  • Integration with major payment gateways

Pros:

  • Powerful fundraising tools
  • Excellent campaign reporting
  • Good customer support and training
  • Scalable for growing organizations

Cons:

  • Interface can feel dated
  • Some features require add-on costs
  • Learning curve for advanced features

Recommendation: For nonprofits that run multiple campaigns and need detailed ROI tracking, DonorPerfect is a top choice for the best CRM for nonprofits focused on fundraising performance.

1. Define Your Needs

List your must-have features: donor management, email marketing, event registration, volunteer tracking, grant management, or reporting. Rank them by priority.

2. Consider Your Budget

Factor in not just monthly fees but also setup costs, training, and potential add-ons. Many CRMs offer nonprofit discounts—always ask.
