The Ultimate Guide to Finding the Best CRM for Nonprofits in 2025

Running a nonprofit is a mission-driven endeavor, but it also requires managing donor relationships, tracking volunteers, coordinating campaigns, and processing donations—all while keeping overhead low. The right Customer Relationship Management (CRM) system can transform your operations, helping you build stronger connections and maximize your impact. But with so many options on the market, what is the best CRM for nonprofits in 2025?

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll break down the top contenders, compare pricing and features, and weigh the pros and cons of each. Whether you’re a small grassroots organization or a large international foundation, we’ll help you find the perfect fit for your budget and needs.

Why Nonprofits Need a Dedicated CRM

Before diving into specific software, it’s important to understand why a generic business CRM often falls short for nonprofits. Nonprofit CRMs are built with features like:

  • Donor management — Track giving history, pledge amounts, and recurring donations.
  • Volunteer coordination — Schedule shifts, track hours, and communicate with teams.
  • Grant management — Monitor application deadlines, reporting requirements, and funder relationships.
  • Segmentation & reporting — Create targeted campaigns based on donor behavior and demographics.
  • Nonprofit-specific pricing — Many vendors offer discounted or free tiers for qualifying organizations.

Using a specialized system ensures you’re not paying for features you don’t need, while gaining tools that directly support your fundraising and outreach goals.

Top 5 Best CRMs for Nonprofits (2025)

After evaluating dozens of platforms, here are our top picks for the best CRM for nonprofits based on features, affordability, and user feedback.

1. Salesforce Nonprofit Cloud

Best for: Large nonprofits with complex needs and dedicated IT support.

Salesforce offers 10 free licenses for eligible nonprofits through its Power of Us program. Beyond that, paid plans start at $10 per user per month (billed annually).

Key features:

  • Unlimited customization with drag-and-drop page builder
  • Powerful reporting dashboards and AI-driven insights (Einstein)
  • Integrated donation processing (via third-party apps like Classy or DonorDrive)
  • Volunteer management and case management modules
  • Massive ecosystem of apps and integrations

Pros:

  • Extremely scalable — grows with your organization
  • Generous free tier for qualifying nonprofits
  • Industry-leading analytics and automation

Cons:

  • Steep learning curve; often requires a dedicated admin or consultant
  • Add-on costs for premium features can add up quickly
  • Overkill for small or volunteer-run organizations

Recommendation: If you have a budget for implementation and training, Salesforce Nonprofit Cloud is the most powerful option available. It’s ideal for organizations with 10+ staff members and complex reporting needs.

2. Bloomerang

Best for: Mid-sized nonprofits focused on donor retention and engagement.

Bloomerang pricing starts at $99/month for up to 1,000 constituents (with a 10% nonprofit discount). Plans scale up based on database size.

Key features:

  • Built-in donor retention tracking with "Retention Score" analytics
  • Automated stewardship workflows (thank-you emails, birthday greetings, etc.)
  • Online donation forms with customizable branding
  • Volunteer management and event registration
  • Simple, intuitive interface designed for non-technical users

Pros:

  • Excellent donor retention tools that help you keep supporters longer
  • User-friendly setup and daily use
  • Strong customer support with onboarding assistance

Cons:

  • Limited customization compared to Salesforce
  • Reporting can feel basic for advanced users
  • No free tier (only a free trial)

Recommendation: Bloomerang is a fantastic choice for organizations that prioritize donor relationships and want a system that’s easy to implement without technical expertise. It’s particularly strong for annual giving programs.

4. HubSpot for Nonprofits

Best for: Organizations that prioritize inbound marketing and content-driven fundraising.

HubSpot offers a 40% discount on its Marketing Hub and Sales Hub for eligible nonprofits. Starter plans begin at $20/month (after discount).

Key features:

  • Free CRM with unlimited contacts and deal tracking
  • Email marketing automation with drag-and-drop builder
  • Live chat and chatbot for website engagement
  • SEO tools and blog analytics
  • Integration with fundraising platforms like Givebutter

Pros:

  • Free CRM tier is genuinely useful for small teams
  • Excellent for building email lists and nurturing leads
  • Clean, modern interface with great usability

Cons:

  • Not built specifically for nonprofits — lacks donation and volunteer features natively
  • Advanced features require paid add-ons
  • Limited reporting on fundraising metrics

Recommendation: HubSpot is best used as a marketing and outreach tool alongside a dedicated donation processor. It’s ideal for organizations that run content-heavy campaigns and want to grow their email list.

  • Online donation forms with minimal setup
  • Event registration and attendance tracking
