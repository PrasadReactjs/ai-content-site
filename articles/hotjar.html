Hotjar vs FullStory: The Ultimate Showdown for UX Analytics in 2025

If you're serious about understanding how users interact with your website, you've likely come across two giants in the behavioral analytics space: Hotjar and FullStory. Both tools promise to reveal the "why" behind user behavior, but they approach the problem from different angles. In this detailed comparison, we’ll dissect everything from features and pricing to data privacy and ease of use, helping you decide which platform is the best fit for your business.

We’re targeting this guide around the keyword Hotjar, so we’ll highlight where Hotjar excels and where FullStory might have an edge. By the end, you’ll have a clear roadmap to choose the right tool for your team.

What Are Hotjar and FullStory?

Both Hotjar and FullStory are session recording and heatmap tools designed to give you visual insights into user behavior. However, their core philosophies differ:

  • Hotjar is known for its simplicity, affordability, and all-in-one approach. It combines session recordings, heatmaps, surveys, and feedback widgets in one platform, making it a favorite for small to mid-sized businesses and solo marketers.
  • FullStory is a more enterprise-focused solution that emphasizes automatic data capture, advanced search capabilities, and powerful analytics. It’s built for product teams that need granular, retroactive insights without manual setup.

The choice often comes down to your team size, budget, and how deep you need to go into user behavior analysis.

Feature Comparison: Hotjar vs FullStory

1. Session Recordings

Both tools record user sessions, but they handle them differently. Hotjar’s session recordings are easy to set up—you add a snippet of code, and recordings start flowing. You can filter by page, device, or user behavior (e.g., rage clicks or form abandonment). Hotjar also offers a "Frustration Score" to highlight problematic sessions.

FullStory’s recordings are more powerful in terms of searchability. It uses "omnisearch" to let you find any session based on almost any criteria—even specific text a user typed or a button they clicked. FullStory also offers session indexing that makes it possible to go back and analyze past sessions retroactively, even if you didn’t set up a specific filter beforehand.

Verdict: For most users, Hotjar’s recordings are sufficient and more intuitive. FullStory wins for teams that need forensic-level search across thousands of sessions.

2. Heatmaps

Heatmaps are a core feature in both tools. Hotjar offers click, move, and scroll heatmaps that are visually clear and easy to interpret. You can view them by device type, compare different pages, and see aggregated data quickly. Hotjar also provides "list reports" that show the most-clicked elements in a table format.

FullStory’s heatmaps are more dynamic—they update in real-time and are deeply integrated with session data. You can click on any hot area in a heatmap and instantly see the recorded sessions from users who clicked there. This seamless connection between heatmaps and recordings is a major time-saver for UX researchers.

Verdict: Hotjar wins for simplicity and quick visual insights. FullStory wins for deep integration between heatmaps and session data.

3. Feedback & Surveys

This is where Hotjar truly stands out. Hotjar includes built-in survey tools, on-site feedback widgets, and "Incoming Feedback" (a persistent tab where users can leave comments). You can create pop-up surveys, link them to specific pages, and even target users based on behavior (e.g., users who spent more than 30 seconds on a page).

FullStory does not offer native surveys or feedback tools. You would need to integrate a third-party survey tool (like SurveyMonkey or Typeform) and then manually correlate that data with FullStory’s session recordings. This adds friction and complexity.

Verdict: Hotjar is the clear winner for teams that want qualitative feedback alongside behavioral data.

4. Data Privacy & Compliance

Both tools take privacy seriously, but they handle it differently. Hotjar offers automatic data anonymization, IP masking, and the ability to disable recordings on specific pages. It is GDPR and CCPA compliant out of the box, and you can easily configure privacy settings via a simple dashboard.

FullStory provides more granular controls, including custom masking rules that let you hide specific elements (like credit card fields or email addresses) while keeping other data visible. FullStory also offers data retention policies and advanced consent management for enterprise needs. However, FullStory’s default settings tend to capture more data, which means you need to be more proactive about privacy configuration.

Verdict: Hotjar is better for teams that want privacy to be simple and automatic. FullStory is better for enterprises that need fine-grained control.

5. Pricing

Pricing is often the deciding factor. Hotjar offers a generous free plan (up to 35 daily sessions, 1,000 pageviews, and 2 heatmaps). Paid plans start at around $39/month for the Plus plan (100 daily sessions, unlimited heatmaps, and surveys). The Business plan (around $99/month) adds more session capacity and advanced targeting.

FullStory’s pricing is less transparent—it starts at around $200/month for the "Business" tier, but most teams end up on custom enterprise plans that can cost thousands of dollars per month. FullStory does not offer a free plan, only a limited free trial.

Verdict: Hotjar is significantly more affordable and accessible for startups, SMBs, and individual marketers. FullStory is a serious investment best suited for larger teams with dedicated budgets.

Use Cases: When to Choose Hotjar vs FullStory

Choose Hotjar if:

  • You’re a small to mid-sized business with a limited budget.
  • You need an all-in-one tool (recordings, heatmaps, surveys, feedback).
  • You want quick, visual insights without a steep learning curve.
  • You value simplicity and ease of setup.
  • You regularly collect user feedback through surveys or on-site polls.

Choose FullStory if:

  • You’re an enterprise with complex analytics needs.
  • You need to search through thousands of sessions retroactively.
  • Your product team requires deep, granular data analysis.
  • You have dedicated UX researchers or data analysts on staff.
  • You already have a separate survey tool and want to integrate it.

Practical Tips for Getting Started

Whichever tool you choose, here are actionable tips to maximize your investment:

  • Start with a clear goal: Don’t just record everything. Decide what you want to learn—e.g., "Why are users abandoning the checkout page?" Then set up recordings and heatmaps specifically for that page.
  • Use filters wisely: In Hotjar, filter recordings by page URL, device, or user behavior (like rage clicks). In FullStory, use omnisearch to find sessions where users encountered errors or dropped off.
  • Combine quantitative and qualitative data: Use heatmaps to see where users click, then watch recordings to understand the context. In Hotjar, add a survey to ask users why they clicked a certain button.
  • Set up privacy first: Before launching any recordings, configure data masking and anonymization. Both tools make this easy, but it’s crucial to do it upfront to avoid compliance issues.
  • Create a regular review cadence: Set aside 30 minutes each week to review recordings and heatmaps. Look for patterns, share findings with your team, and prioritize changes based on impact.

Conclusion: Which Tool Should You Choose?

Both Hotjar and FullStory are excellent tools, but they serve different audiences. If you’re looking for an affordable, easy-to-use platform that combines session recordings, heatmaps, and feedback in one place, Hotjar is the clear winner. It’s perfect for marketers, product managers, and small teams who want to quickly understand user behavior without a huge investment.
