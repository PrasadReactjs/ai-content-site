Hotjar vs FullStory: The Ultimate Showdown for UX Analytics in 2024

Understanding how users interact with your website is no longer a luxury—it's a necessity. In the crowded field of behavior analytics, two names consistently rise to the top: Hotjar and FullStory. While both tools offer session recordings, heatmaps, and user feedback features, they serve different audiences and use cases. In this comprehensive comparison, we’ll dive deep into the strengths, weaknesses, and ideal applications of each platform. By the end, you'll have a clear roadmap to choose the right tool for your business—and why Hotjar might be the better fit for most teams.

What is Hotjar?

Hotjar is a user experience (UX) analytics platform designed to help businesses understand visitor behavior through visual tools. Founded in 2014, it has become a go-to solution for startups, SMBs, and marketing teams that need quick, actionable insights without a steep learning curve. Hotjar combines session recordings, heatmaps, surveys, and feedback widgets into one intuitive dashboard.

What is FullStory?

FullStory is a more advanced digital experience analytics platform. It focuses on automatic session capture, powerful search capabilities, and data-rich dashboards. FullStory is built for larger enterprises with dedicated product, engineering, and data science teams who need granular, queryable data to debug issues and analyze user flows at scale.

Feature Comparison: Hotjar vs FullStory

1. Session Recordings

Hotjar offers session recordings that are easy to set up and filter. You can watch individual user sessions, tag key moments, and share clips with your team. The interface is clean and beginner-friendly. Hotjar also provides a "rage click" and "dead click" detection to highlight frustration points.

FullStory records every session automatically with no sampling (on paid plans). Its standout feature is "OmniSearch," which lets you search for any user action (e.g., "users who clicked 'Add to Cart' but didn't checkout"). FullStory also offers "Session Replay with DevTools," allowing developers to inspect network requests and console logs during a replay—critical for debugging.

2. Heatmaps

Hotjar provides click, move, and scroll heatmaps that are easy to generate for any page. You can segment data by device, browser, or user attribute. Heatmaps update in real-time and are a staple for conversion rate optimization (CRO) campaigns.

FullStory also offers heatmaps, but they are more limited in customization. FullStory's heatmaps are best used as a secondary tool, as the platform's primary strength lies in session search and analytics rather than visual heat mapping.

3. User Feedback Tools

Hotjar excels here with built-in feedback widgets, on-page surveys, and "Incoming Feedback" (a simple thumbs-up/thumbs-down widget). You can launch targeted surveys to specific user segments—ideal for gathering qualitative data quickly.

FullStory lacks native feedback tools. To collect user feedback, you'll need to integrate third-party tools like SurveyMonkey or Qualtrics. This is a notable gap for teams that want an all-in-one solution.

4. Data Privacy & Compliance

Both tools offer privacy features, but Hotjar is more straightforward for compliance. Hotjar automatically masks sensitive form fields (like credit card numbers) and allows you to exclude specific pages from recording. It's GDPR, CCPA, and ePrivacy compliant out of the box.

FullStory offers more granular privacy controls, including the ability to redact specific CSS selectors. However, its automatic recording of all sessions can be a double-edged sword—you must configure privacy settings carefully to avoid capturing sensitive data.

5. Pricing

Hotjar offers a generous free tier (up to 35 daily sessions) and affordable paid plans starting at $39/month (Plus plan). The Business plan ($99/month) unlocks more sessions, events, and integrations. Hotjar's pricing is transparent and scales with your needs.

FullStory has a free "Starter" plan (limited to 1,000 sessions/month) but quickly jumps to $199/month for the "Business" plan. Enterprise plans are custom-priced and often require an annual commitment. FullStory is significantly more expensive, especially for high-traffic sites.

When to Choose Hotjar

  • You're a marketer or CRO specialist: Hotjar's heatmaps and surveys are perfect for optimizing landing pages and funnels.
  • You need quick setup: Hotjar can be installed with a single script tag and is ready in minutes.
  • Budget is a concern: Hotjar's free tier and lower-cost plans make it accessible for startups and small teams.
  • You value built-in feedback tools: Surveys and feedback widgets are integrated, saving you from juggling multiple tools.
  • You want to understand "why" users behave a certain way: Hotjar's combination of recordings, heatmaps, and surveys gives you a complete picture.

When to Choose FullStory

  • You're a product or engineering team: FullStory's DevTools integration and OmniSearch are powerful for debugging and technical analysis.
  • You need to scale to millions of sessions: FullStory handles high-volume data with ease and offers robust APIs.
  • You require advanced segmentation: FullStory's query-based search lets you find exact user behaviors across any time period.
  • You have a dedicated data team: FullStory's raw data exports and custom dashboards require more technical expertise to utilize fully.

Practical Tips for Choosing the Right Tool

Tip 1: Start with Hotjar for Quick Wins

If you're new to UX analytics, start with Hotjar. Its free tier lets you test the waters without financial risk. Use heatmaps to identify where users click most, then set up recordings to watch sessions from those pages. Add a simple feedback widget to ask "What's stopping you from buying?"—you'll uncover friction points in days, not weeks.

Tip 2: Use FullStory for Technical Deep Dives

Once you've identified a critical issue (e.g., a checkout bug that only affects mobile users), FullStory's OmniSearch can pinpoint every affected session. Share the replay with your developer, who can view console errors and network requests directly—saving hours of reproduction time.

Tip 3: Combine Both Tools (If Budget Allows)

Some teams use Hotjar for daily UX monitoring and feedback, and FullStory for advanced analytics and debugging. This combination gives you the best of both worlds, though it increases costs and complexity.

Tip 4: Prioritize Privacy from Day One

Whichever tool you choose, configure privacy settings before launching. Mask all form fields, exclude sensitive pages (e.g., account settings), and update your privacy policy. Both tools provide documentation to help you stay compliant.

Conclusion: Which One Should You Choose?

Both Hotjar and FullStory are excellent tools, but they serve different primary audiences. Hotjar is the clear winner for marketers, CRO specialists, and small-to-medium businesses that want an intuitive, all-in-one platform for understanding user behavior. Its heatmaps, surveys, and session recordings are easy to set up and provide immediate value. The free tier and transparent pricing make it a low-risk investment.

FullStory is better suited for enterprise product teams and engineers who need deep technical insights, automatic session capture at scale, and powerful search capabilities. However, its higher price point and lack of native feedback tools can be drawbacks for non-technical teams.

Ready to improve your user experience today? Start with Hotjar—sign up for their free plan and install the script on your site. Within hours, you'll have your first heatmap and session recording. There's no better way to start your UX optimization journey.
