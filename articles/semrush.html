Ahrefs vs Semrush: Which SEO Tool Wins in 2024? (Detailed Comparison)

If you’re serious about SEO, you’ve likely narrowed your tool search down to two giants: Ahrefs and Semrush. Both are industry leaders, but they serve slightly different needs. This detailed comparison will help you decide which platform is the best fit for your workflow, budget, and goals—with a special focus on how Semrush stands out in key areas.

By the end of this guide, you’ll have a clear roadmap for choosing the right tool, plus actionable tips to get the most out of either platform.

Overview: Ahrefs vs Semrush at a Glance

Both Ahrefs and Semrush are comprehensive SEO suites. However, they were built with different core strengths:

  • Ahrefs started as a backlink analysis tool and is still unrivaled for link data. Its interface is clean, and its Site Explorer is a favorite among link builders.
  • Semrush began as a keyword research and competitive analysis tool. It has since expanded into a full marketing platform, covering SEO, PPC, social media, and content marketing.

While Ahrefs excels in depth of link data, Semrush offers a broader feature set that many marketers find more practical for day-to-day work.

Feature-by-Feature Comparison

1. Keyword Research

Semrush is widely considered the gold standard for keyword research. Its Keyword Magic Tool generates hundreds of related keywords, along with metrics like search volume, keyword difficulty, and trend data. You can filter by question keywords, broad match, or phrase match—ideal for building topic clusters.

Ahrefs’ Keywords Explorer is also powerful, but its database is slightly smaller. For most users, Semrush offers more granular data and better organization, especially for PPC and local SEO keyword research.

Tip: Use Semrush to find “low-hanging fruit” keywords by filtering for low difficulty and high volume. Then, export the list and map it to your content calendar.

2. Backlink Analysis

This is Ahrefs’ strongest area. Its backlink index is the largest and most frequently updated in the industry. Ahrefs’ Site Explorer shows every referring domain, anchor text, and link type with incredible speed.

That said, Semrush has closed the gap significantly in recent years. Its Backlink Analytics tool now offers comparable data, plus unique features like the “Backlink Gap” analysis—which shows you links your competitors have that you don’t. For most SEOs, Semrush provides enough link data to build a strong outreach strategy.

Actionable advice: If link building is your primary focus, Ahrefs may still edge ahead. But if you need a tool that also handles content and PPC, Semrush is the better all-rounder.

3. Site Audit & Technical SEO

Both tools offer comprehensive site audits. Ahrefs’ Site Audit is straightforward and flags common issues like broken links, missing meta tags, and slow pages. It’s easy to use but less customizable.

Semrush takes site audits further. You can set up custom crawl rules, schedule weekly audits, and track issue severity with a clear scoring system. It also integrates with Google Search Console and Analytics, giving you a more complete picture of your site’s health.

Pro tip: Use Semrush’s On Page SEO Checker to get specific recommendations for each page, including which keywords to target and how to improve your content.

4. Content Marketing & Writing Tools

This is where Semrush truly shines. Its SEO Content Template gives you a ready-made outline based on top-ranking pages for your target keyword. The SEO Writing Assistant then scores your content in real time for readability, tone, and keyword usage.

Ahrefs has a basic Content Gap tool, but it lacks a dedicated content editor. For content teams, Semrush is the clear winner—it even integrates with Google Docs and WordPress.

Tip: When writing a new blog post, start with Semrush’s SEO Content Template. It will recommend a word count, related terms, and backlink targets based on what’s already ranking.

5. Competitive Analysis

Both tools allow you to spy on competitors, but Semrush offers more depth. Its Domain vs Domain tool compares up to five competitors side by side, showing you their top keywords, traffic estimates, and ad strategies.

Ahrefs’ Competitive Analysis is simpler—it shows the overlapping keywords between domains. For a quick overview, it’s fine. But for a full competitive audit, Semrush provides richer data, including PPC keywords and display advertising insights.

Actionable advice: Run a “Competitive Positioning Map” in Semrush to see where your brand stands against rivals in terms of traffic and keyword share. Use this to identify gaps in your strategy.

6. PPC & Paid Advertising

Ahrefs is primarily an SEO tool and offers very little for PPC. Semrush, on the other hand, is a full-fledged advertising platform. It includes a PPC Keyword Tool, Ad Builder, and even a PLA (Product Listing Ad) feature for ecommerce sites.

If you run Google Ads or Bing Ads, Semrush is essential. You can analyze competitor ad copy, find profitable keywords, and track your ad positions—all from the same dashboard.

7. Reporting & Dashboards

Ahrefs keeps reporting simple—you can export PDFs or CSV files from most tools. However, there’s no custom dashboard builder.

Semrush offers a powerful My Reports feature, where you can drag and drop widgets to create branded, automated reports for clients or stakeholders. This is a huge time-saver for agencies.

Pricing Comparison

  • Ahrefs: Plans start at $99/month (Lite), $199/month (Standard), $399/month (Advanced), and $999/month (Enterprise). The lower tiers are quite limited.
  • Semrush: Plans start at $139.95/month (Pro), $249.95/month (Guru), and $499.95/month (Business). The Pro plan includes many features that Ahrefs reserves for higher tiers.

For most freelancers and small businesses, Semrush offers better value. The Pro plan includes keyword research, site audit, backlink analysis, and content tools—all in one subscription.

Which Tool Should You Choose?

Choose Ahrefs if:

  • Your primary focus is link building and backlink analysis.
  • You prefer a simpler, more streamlined interface.
  • You work mostly with SEO and don’t need PPC or social media tools.

Choose Semrush if:

  • You need an all-in-one marketing platform (SEO + PPC + content + social).
  • You run a content-heavy strategy and want writing assistance.
  • You manage paid ad campaigns alongside organic SEO.
  • You’re an agency or consultant who needs custom reporting.

Final Verdict: Semrush is the More Versatile Choice

Both Ahrefs and Semrush are excellent tools. But for most digital marketers, Semrush offers a more complete solution. It covers SEO, content, PPC, and competitive analysis in one platform, with better reporting and a more generous free tier (limited, but available).

Ahrefs remains the king of backlinks, but Semrush has caught up significantly while adding features that Ahrefs simply doesn’t have. If you can only invest in one tool, Semrush will serve you across more use cases.
