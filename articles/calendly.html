Calendly vs Cal.com: The Ultimate Scheduling Showdown for 2025

If you’ve ever tried to schedule a meeting across multiple time zones or with a group of busy professionals, you know the pain of endless email chains. Scheduling tools like Calendly and Cal.com promise to solve that headache—but which one is right for you? In this comprehensive comparison, we’ll dive deep into features, pricing, customization, and user experience to help you make an informed decision. Whether you’re a solopreneur, a growing team, or a large enterprise, this guide covers everything you need to know.

Overview: What Are Calendly and Cal.com?

Calendly has been the market leader in online scheduling since 2013. It’s known for its simplicity, reliability, and wide adoption across industries. Calendly integrates with over 1,000 apps, including Google Calendar, Outlook, Zoom, and Salesforce, making it a go-to choice for professionals who need a hassle-free booking experience.

Cal.com (formerly Calendso) is the open-source challenger that burst onto the scene in 2021. It offers similar core functionality but with a strong emphasis on customization, privacy, and self-hosting. Cal.com is built for developers and organizations that want full control over their scheduling infrastructure, including white-labeling and custom branding.

Feature Comparison: Calendly vs Cal.com

1. Core Scheduling Features

Both tools let you set your availability, share a booking link, and let invitees pick a time slot that works for everyone. However, the depth of features differs:

  • Calendly offers round-robin, collective, and group event types (e.g., one-on-one, group sessions, or collective availability for teams). It also supports “buffer time” and “minimum notice” settings out of the box.
  • Cal.com provides similar event types but with more granular control. You can set custom availability for each event, create recurring events, and even build “dynamic” booking pages that change based on invitee location or time zone.

2. Customization and Branding

  • Calendly lets you add your logo, change colors, and customize a few text fields—but branding is limited unless you’re on the Professional plan or higher. Free users get “Powered by Calendly” on their booking page.
  • Cal.com is a white-label powerhouse. On the open-source self-hosted version, you can remove all branding entirely. Even on the cloud version, paid plans allow full brand customization, including custom domains and email notifications with your own email server.

3. Integrations and Ecosystem

Both tools integrate with major calendar services (Google, Outlook, iCloud) and video conferencing platforms (Zoom, Google Meet, Microsoft Teams). But the breadth of integrations differs:

  • Calendly boasts over 1,000 native integrations via Zapier, plus deep CRM integrations with Salesforce, HubSpot, and Pipedrive. It also has a native “Calendly for Salesforce” app.
  • Cal.com offers a more modest 100+ native integrations but compensates with an open API and webhooks. Developers can build custom integrations easily. Cal.com also supports self-hosted setups, meaning you can connect it to internal tools without exposing data to third-party servers.

4. Pricing: Free vs Paid Plans

Pricing is often the deciding factor. Here’s a breakdown:

  • Calendly Free: One event type, basic integrations, and a single calendar connection. Limited to 1:1 meetings only.
  • Calendly Essentials ($10/month): Unlimited event types, multiple calendar connections, and team scheduling features.
  • Calendly Professional ($16/month): Adds group events, round-robin, custom branding, and Salesforce integration.
  • Calendly Teams ($20/month per seat): Includes routing rules, workflow automation, and admin controls.
  • Calendly Enterprise: Custom pricing for large organizations with advanced security and compliance needs.
  • Cal.com Free (Cloud): Unlimited event types, one active team member, and limited branding customization. Includes most core features.
  • Cal.com Teams ($12/month per seat): Multiple team members, round-robin, workflow automation, and custom branding.
  • Cal.com Enterprise: Custom pricing with dedicated support, self-hosting options, and advanced admin features.

Key takeaway: Cal.com’s free plan is more generous than Calendly’s, offering unlimited event types and more customization. For teams, Cal.com’s per-seat pricing is slightly cheaper than Calendly’s.

5. Privacy and Data Control

With growing concerns about data privacy, this is a critical differentiator:

  • Calendly stores all data on its servers. It is SOC 2 compliant and offers GDPR-compliant data processing agreements, but you don’t have direct control over where your data resides.
  • Cal.com offers self-hosting (open-source). You can deploy it on your own server, keep all data within your infrastructure, and comply with strict data residency requirements (e.g., HIPAA, internal company policies). This makes Cal.com a strong choice for healthcare, finance, or government organizations.

Practical Tips: Choosing the Right Tool for Your Needs

When to Choose Calendly

  • You need a plug-and-play solution. Calendly’s setup takes minutes, and its UI is intuitive for non-technical users.
  • You rely heavily on CRM integrations. If you use Salesforce, HubSpot, or Pipedrive daily, Calendly’s deep integrations will save you time.
  • You value a large ecosystem. With thousands of apps and a robust marketplace, Calendly fits into almost any workflow.
  • You need enterprise-grade support. Calendly’s Enterprise plan includes dedicated account management and advanced compliance (SOC 2, HIPAA).

When to Choose Cal.com

  • You want full customization. If you need white-label branding, custom domains, or a completely tailored booking experience, Cal.com wins hands-down.
  • You prioritize data privacy. Self-hosting gives you complete control over your data, making Cal.com ideal for privacy-conscious organizations.
  • You have a development team. Cal.com’s open-source nature means you can modify the code, build custom features, and integrate with internal systems without relying on a vendor.
  • You’re on a tight budget. Cal.com’s free cloud plan is more feature-rich than Calendly’s, and self-hosting eliminates subscription costs entirely (though you’ll need server resources).

Actionable Advice: How to Make the Switch

If you’re currently using Calendly and considering a move to Cal.com, here are steps to ensure a smooth transition:

  1. Export your data. Calendly allows you to export event types, booking data, and invitee information via CSV or API.
  2. Map your workflows. Identify any automation or integrations you rely on (e.g., Zapier, CRM sync) and check if Cal.com supports them natively or via webhooks.
  3. Test with a small group. Set up a few event types in Cal.com and share them with a handful of colleagues before rolling out to your entire team.
  4. Update your links. Replace your old Calendly links everywhere—email signatures, social media profiles, website buttons.
  5. Monitor analytics. Both tools provide basic analytics (bookings, cancellations). Use this data to ensure the new system is performing as expected.
