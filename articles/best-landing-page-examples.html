Best Landing Page Examples That Convert: Top Picks, Pricing & Features (2024 Guide)

If you’re searching for the best landing page examples to inspire your next campaign, you’ve come to the right place. A high-converting landing page is the backbone of any successful digital marketing strategy—whether you’re selling a product, collecting leads, or promoting an affiliate offer. The difference between a mediocre page and a stellar one can mean thousands of dollars in lost or gained revenue.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore the top landing page examples across different industries, break down what makes them work, and give you actionable tips to replicate their success. We’ll also cover pricing, features, pros/cons, and provide clear recommendations so you can choose the right approach for your business.

What Makes a Great Landing Page?

Before diving into specific examples, it’s important to understand the core elements that separate an average landing page from a best landing page example:

  • Clear headline that communicates the unique value proposition in under 5 seconds.
  • Compelling call-to-action (CTA) that stands out and tells visitors exactly what to do next.
  • Minimal distractions—no navigation bars, sidebars, or unnecessary links.
  • Social proof such as testimonials, case studies, or trust badges.
  • Mobile responsiveness—over 60% of traffic comes from mobile devices.
  • Fast loading speed (under 3 seconds) to reduce bounce rates.

Top 5 Best Landing Page Examples

1. Unbounce – The Click-Through Landing Page

Industry: SaaS / Landing Page Builder
URL: Unbounce’s own product demo page

Unbounce is a leading landing page builder, and their own landing page is a textbook example of simplicity and clarity. The headline—“Build, Publish & A/B Test Landing Pages Without IT”—immediately addresses the pain point of non-technical marketers. The hero section includes a single CTA button (“Start My Free Trial”) and a short video explaining the product.

Features: Drag-and-drop builder, 100+ templates, A/B testing, dynamic text replacement, and integrations with CRM tools.

Pricing: Starts at $90/month (Essential plan), $135/month (Optimize plan), $225/month (Accelerate plan).

Pros: Extremely easy to use, excellent template library, powerful A/B testing features.
Cons: Relatively expensive for small businesses; some advanced features require higher-tier plans.

Recommendation: Best for marketers and agencies that need a robust, all-in-one landing page solution with built-in optimization tools.

2. Leadpages – The Lead Generation Machine

Industry: Lead Generation / Marketing
URL: Leadpages’ “Free Website & Landing Pages” page

Leadpages focuses on simplicity and lead capture. Their landing page example uses a bold headline (“Create High-Converting Landing Pages in Minutes”) with a secondary headline that reinforces the benefit (“No coding or design skills required”). The page includes a short form (name and email only) and a countdown timer to create urgency.

Features: 200+ templates, pop-up forms, alert bars, A/B testing, Facebook and Instagram ad integration, and CRM connections.

Pricing: $37/month (Standard), $74/month (Pro), custom pricing for Advanced.

Pros: Affordable entry point, excellent for beginners, built-in lead capture tools.
Cons: Templates can feel generic; limited design flexibility compared to Unbounce.

Recommendation: Ideal for small business owners, coaches, and solo entrepreneurs who want a quick, affordable way to generate leads without learning design.

3. ClickFunnels – The Sales Funnel Powerhouse

Industry: E-commerce / Course Sales
URL: ClickFunnels’ “Start Your 14-Day Free Trial” page

ClickFunnels is known for building complete sales funnels, but their landing pages are equally impressive. Their main landing page example uses a long-form sales letter format with bullet points, testimonials, and a “Buy Now” button that appears multiple times. The page is designed to overcome objections and push visitors toward a purchase decision.

Features: Funnel builder, order bumps, upsells, downsells, email automation, and membership site creation.

Pricing: $147/month (Basic), $197/month (Pro), $2,497/month (Funnel Hacker).

Pros: All-in-one funnel solution, high conversion potential for product sales, strong upsell features.
Cons: Expensive for beginners; steep learning curve; page builder is less intuitive than Unbounce.

Recommendation: Perfect for e-commerce sellers, course creators, and experienced marketers who want to build full sales funnels, not just single landing pages.

4. Instapage – The Enterprise Landing Page Platform

Industry: Enterprise / Ad Campaigns
URL: Instapage’s “Landing Page Platform for Ad Campaigns” page

Instapage targets large businesses and agencies with a focus on post-click optimization. Their landing page example uses a clean, professional design with a video background and a prominent “Get a Demo” CTA. The page highlights key integrations with Google Ads, Facebook, and Salesforce, making it ideal for paid traffic campaigns.

Features: Advanced A/B testing, heatmaps, dynamic text replacement, AMP landing pages, and team collaboration tools.

Pricing: Starting at $199/month (Build plan), $299/month (Convert plan), custom pricing for Enterprise.

Pros: Best-in-class analytics, excellent for scaling ad campaigns, team-friendly features.
Cons: High price point; overkill for small businesses; no free trial (only demo).

Recommendation: Recommended for marketing teams, agencies, and enterprises that run high-volume ad campaigns and need granular analytics.

5. GetResponse – The All-in-One Marketing Platform

Industry: Email Marketing / Automation
URL: GetResponse’s “Landing Page Creator” page

GetResponse is primarily an email marketing tool, but their landing page builder is a hidden gem. Their landing page example uses a split-screen design: left side features a compelling headline and a form, while the right side shows a product image. The page also includes trust badges and a money-back guarantee to reduce friction.

Features: Drag-and-drop editor, 150+ templates, webinars, email marketing automation, CRM, and e-commerce tools.

Pricing: $19/month (Basic), $59/month (Plus), $119/month (Professional).

Pros: Very affordable, includes email marketing and webinars, good for beginners.
Cons: Landing page templates are less varied than Unbounce or Instapage; advanced features require higher plans.

Recommendation: Best for small businesses and marketers who want an affordable all-in-one platform that combines landing pages with email marketing and automation.

How to Choose the Best Landing Page Example for Your Needs

With so many options, picking the right best landing page example depends on your specific goals. Here’s a quick decision guide:

  • For lead generation: Choose Leadpages or GetResponse for their low cost and simple form integrations.
  • For product sales and funnels: ClickFunnels is unbeatable for its upsell and order bump features.
  • For A/B testing and optimization: Unbounce or Instapage offer the most advanced testing tools.
  • For enterprise and ad campaigns: Instapage provides the analytics and integrations needed for high-scale campaigns.
  • For beginners on a budget: GetResponse offers the best value with email marketing included.
