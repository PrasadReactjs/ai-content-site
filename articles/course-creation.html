The Ultimate Guide to the Best Online Course Platforms for Course Creation in 2025

Are you ready to turn your expertise into income? Whether you're a coach, educator, or entrepreneur, the right platform for course creation can make or break your online business. What to Look for in a Course Creation Platform

Before diving into the list, let’s set the criteria. The best platform for course creation depends on your goals, tech skills, and budget. Here’s what matters most:

  • Ease of use: Can you build a course without coding or design skills?
  • Pricing model: Flat monthly fee vs. transaction-based pricing (e.g., 5%–10% per sale).
  • Marketing tools: Email integrations, affiliate management, and sales funnels.
  • Student experience: Mobile access, community features, and completion tracking.
  • Scalability: Can the platform grow with you as you add more courses and students?

Now, let’s explore the top contenders.

1. Teachable – Best for Beginners and All-in-One Course Creation

Teachable is one of the most popular platforms for course creation, and for good reason. It offers a clean, intuitive interface that lets you upload videos, quizzes, and downloadable resources in minutes.

Pricing

  • Free Plan: $0/month + 10% transaction fee per sale
  • Basic Plan: $39/month (billed annually) – 5% transaction fee
  • Pro Plan: $119/month – 0% transaction fee
  • Business Plan: $299/month – 0% transaction fee

Key Features

  • Drag-and-drop course builder
  • Built-in quiz and certificate generation
  • Integrated payment processing (credit card, PayPal)
  • Basic affiliate marketing tools
  • Custom domain and branding (Pro and up)

Pros

  • Extremely beginner-friendly – no technical setup required
  • Excellent customer support and extensive knowledge base
  • Supports coaching and membership models alongside courses

Cons

  • Transaction fees on lower plans can eat into profits
  • Limited advanced marketing automation (e.g., email sequences) without third-party tools
  • Customization options are somewhat rigid compared to open-source alternatives

Best for: Solo creators, coaches, and professionals who want a no-code solution to start selling courses fast.

2. Thinkific – Best for Customization and Student Engagement

Thinkific gives you more control over the look and feel of your school. It’s a strong competitor to Teachable, especially if you value design flexibility and community features.

Pricing

  • Free Plan: $0/month – 0% transaction fee (limited to 3 courses)
  • Basic Plan: $49/month – 0% transaction fee
  • Pro Plan: $99/month – 0% transaction fee
  • Premier Plan: $199/month – 0% transaction fee

Key Features

  • Fully customizable course pages (no coding required)
  • Built-in community (discussion forums, direct messaging)
  • Advanced student analytics and progress tracking
  • Native email marketing (limited on Basic plan)
  • Mobile app for students (iOS and Android)

Pros

  • No transaction fees on any paid plan – you keep 100% of sales
  • Strong community features help with student retention
  • Generous free plan for testing the waters

Cons

  • Interface can feel cluttered compared to Teachable
  • Email marketing tools are less powerful than dedicated platforms like ConvertKit
  • Some advanced features (e.g., affiliate reporting) require Pro or Premier plans

Best for: Creators who want to build a branded learning experience with interactive student communities.

3. Kajabi – Best for All-in-One Marketing and Course Creation

Kajabi is the premium choice for creators who want to combine course creation with full-fledged marketing automation. It’s not just a course platform—it’s a business platform.

Pricing

  • Basic Plan: $149/month – 3 courses, 10,000 contacts
  • Growth Plan: $199/month – 15 courses, 25,000 contacts
  • Pro Plan: $399/month – 100 courses, 100,000 contacts

Key Features

  • Built-in email marketing with visual automation builder
  • Sales funnel templates and landing page builder
  • Pipeline management for coaching/consulting
  • Integrated affiliate program management
  • Mobile app for students (custom branded)

Pros

  • All-in-one ecosystem – no need for separate email, landing page, or affiliate tools
  • Powerful automation sequences (e.g., email series, behavioral triggers)
  • Excellent for scaling a full digital product business

Cons

  • Steep learning curve – more complex than Teachable or Thinkific
  • No free plan (only a 14-day trial for $1)
  • Expensive for beginners on a tight budget

Best for: Established creators, marketers, and businesses ready to invest in a complete sales infrastructure.

4. Podia – Best for Simplicity and All-in-One Digital Products

Podia prides itself on being the simplest platform for selling courses, memberships, and digital downloads. If you want to avoid complexity and transaction fees, Podia is a strong contender.

Pricing

  • Free Plan: $0/month – 10% transaction fee (limited to 1 course)
  • Mover Plan: $39/month – 0% transaction fee
  • Shaker Plan: $89/month – 0% transaction fee

Key Features

  • One-click product creation (courses, webinars, digital downloads, memberships)
  • Built-in email marketing (basic sequences)
  • No transaction fees on paid plans
  • Customizable storefront
  • Integrated affiliate program (Shaker plan only)

Pros

  • Easiest setup of any platform – truly drag-and-drop
  • No hidden fees – you keep 100% of revenue on paid plans
  • Unlimited everything (courses, students, bandwidth) on Mover and Shaker plans

Cons

  • Marketing automation is very basic compared to Kajabi
  • Limited customization of course player and pages
  • No native mobile app for students
