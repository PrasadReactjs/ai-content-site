Best Membership Plugin for a Subscription Site: Top 5 Picks for 2025 (In-Depth Review)

Building a subscription site is one of the most profitable ways to generate recurring revenue online. Whether you’re selling courses, exclusive content, or a community, the right membership plugin can make or break your business. With dozens of options available, choosing the best one can feel overwhelming.

In this comprehensive guide, I’ll compare the top membership plugins for WordPress. I’ve tested each one for ease of use, features, pricing, and scalability. By the end, you’ll know exactly which plugin fits your subscription site goals.

What to Look for in a Membership Plugin

Before diving into the reviews, here are the key features every subscription site needs:

  • Content restriction – ability to hide or show content based on membership level
  • Payment gateway integration – Stripe, PayPal, etc.
  • Recurring billing – monthly, yearly, or custom intervals
  • Member management – easy to add, remove, or upgrade users
  • Drip content – schedule content release over time
  • Integration – with email marketing, LMS, or other tools

1. MemberPress – Best Overall for Most Subscription Sites

MemberPress is arguably the most popular WordPress membership plugin. It’s built for serious site owners who need robust features without complexity.

Key Features

  • Unlimited membership levels
  • Drip content and expiration rules
  • Built-in coupon system
  • Powerful content access rules (by tag, category, or individual post)
  • Integration with Stripe, PayPal, and Authorize.net
  • Add-ons for courses (MemberPress Courses), forums, and affiliates

Pricing

  • Basic: $179/year – 1 site
  • Plus: $249/year – 2 sites
  • Pro: $399/year – 5 sites
  • Elite: $599/year – unlimited sites

All plans include a 14-day money-back guarantee.

Pros

  • Extremely user-friendly setup wizard
  • Excellent support and documentation
  • Seamless integration with popular email services (Mailchimp, ConvertKit, etc.)
  • Built-in course builder saves money on separate LMS plugins

Cons

  • No free version (but worth the investment)
  • Some advanced features (like affiliate management) require paid add-ons

Best for: Most subscription sites, especially those selling courses, coaching, or premium content.

2. Restrict Content Pro – Best for Simplicity and Performance

Restrict Content Pro (RCP) is a lightweight, fast plugin that focuses on core membership functionality. It’s perfect if you want a clean, no-bloat solution.

Key Features

  • Unlimited membership levels
  • Recurring payments via Stripe, PayPal, or 2Checkout
  • Drip content and expiration
  • Discount codes
  • Member management dashboard
  • Email notifications

Pricing

  • Personal: $99/year – 1 site
  • Plus: $199/year – 3 sites
  • Professional: $299/year – unlimited sites

30-day money-back guarantee.

Pros

  • Very affordable starting price
  • Lightweight – won’t slow down your site
  • Clean, intuitive interface
  • Developer-friendly with hooks and filters

Cons

  • Fewer built-in features than MemberPress (e.g., no course builder)
  • Add-ons (like Mailchimp integration) cost extra

Best for: Simple subscription sites, bloggers, or developers who want a minimal plugin.

3. Paid Memberships Pro – Best Free Option with Room to Grow

Paid Memberships Pro (PMPro) is a popular freemium plugin. Its free version is surprisingly powerful, making it ideal for startups on a budget.

Key Features (Free Version)

  • Unlimited membership levels
  • Content restriction by level
  • Recurring payments (Stripe, PayPal)
  • Member directory
  • Basic email settings

Pricing

  • Free – core features
  • Plus: $247/year – 1 site (adds drip content, coupons, advanced integrations)
  • Pro: $397/year – 3 sites
  • Enterprise: $597/year – unlimited sites

Pros

  • Free version is fully functional for many use cases
  • Large community and extensive documentation
  • Great for scaling – add-ons for courses, forums, and affiliates

Cons

  • Free version lacks drip content and coupon support
  • Interface feels slightly dated

Best for: Beginners, small subscription sites, or those who want to start free and upgrade later.

4. WishList Member – Best for Advanced Content Protection

WishList Member has been around for years and excels at granular content restriction. It’s a favorite among membership site veterans.

Key Features

  • Advanced protection rules (by URL, page, or custom post type)
  • Drip content and sequential access
  • Integration with 20+ payment gateways
  • Built-in affiliate center
  • Email automation sequences

Pricing

  • Starter: $149/year – 1 site
  • Pro: $249/year – 3 sites
  • Unlimited: $399/year – unlimited sites

Pros

  • Very flexible content protection
  • Built-in affiliate system saves money
  • Supports multiple membership levels with complex rules

Cons

  • Learning curve – not as beginner-friendly as MemberPress
  • Some users report occasional compatibility issues with new themes

Best for: Complex subscription sites with multiple membership tiers and advanced protection needs.

5. LearnDash – Best for Course-Based Subscription Sites

LearnDash is primarily a Learning Management System (LMS), but it includes powerful membership features. If your subscription site is centered on online courses, this is a top contender.

Key Features

  • Built-in course builder with quizzes and certificates
  • Drip-feed lessons
  • Membership-style access via groups
  • Integration with WooCommerce, Stripe, and PayPal
  • Gamification (points, badges)
