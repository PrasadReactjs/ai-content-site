The Ultimate Guide to Mastering Stock Control: Best Inventory Management Software in 2025

If you’re running a business that sells physical products, you already know the pain of losing sales because you ran out of stock—or the agony of paying storage fees for items that just won’t move. That’s where stock control saves the day. Effective stock control ensures you always have the right product in the right place at the right time. In this guide, we’ll compare the best inventory management software options, dive into their pricing, features, pros, and cons, and help you choose the perfect fit for your business.

What is Stock Control and Why Does It Matter?

Stock control (often called inventory management) is the process of overseeing and regulating your inventory levels. It involves tracking stock quantities, locations, and movements so you can avoid overstocking, understocking, and costly errors. Good stock control directly impacts your bottom line by reducing holding costs, increasing order accuracy, and boosting customer satisfaction.

Without robust stock control, businesses face:

  • Stockouts that lead to lost revenue and unhappy customers
  • Excess inventory that ties up cash and incurs storage fees
  • Manual errors from spreadsheets or paper-based systems
  • Inefficient order fulfillment and slower delivery times

The right inventory management software automates these processes, giving you real-time visibility and actionable insights. Let’s explore the top solutions on the market.

1. Zoho Inventory – Best for Small to Mid-Sized Businesses

Zoho Inventory is a feature-rich platform that integrates seamlessly with other Zoho apps and popular e-commerce channels. It’s designed for businesses that need robust stock control without breaking the bank.

Key Features

  • Multi-channel selling (Amazon, eBay, Shopify, Etsy)
  • Barcode scanning and batch tracking
  • Automated purchase orders and reorder points
  • Warehouse management with bin locations
  • Order management and shipping integrations

Pricing

  • Free Plan: Up to 50 orders/month, basic features
  • Standard Plan: $59/month (billed annually) – 500 orders/month, advanced inventory
  • Professional Plan: $129/month – 3,000 orders/month, serial number tracking
  • Enterprise Plan: Custom pricing for high-volume businesses

Pros

  • Excellent value for money with a free tier
  • Strong integrations with major sales channels
  • User-friendly interface with mobile app
  • Comprehensive reporting and analytics

Cons

  • Some advanced features (like serial tracking) require higher plans
  • Limited offline functionality
  • Customer support can be slow on lower-tier plans

Best for: Small to mid-sized businesses already using Zoho apps or needing affordable multi-channel stock control.

2. TradeGecko (Now QuickBooks Commerce) – Best for Wholesale & Distribution

TradeGecko, rebranded as QuickBooks Commerce, is a powerful inventory and order management platform tailored for wholesalers, distributors, and multi-channel retailers. Its strength lies in handling complex stock control across multiple warehouses and sales channels.

Key Features

  • Multi-warehouse inventory tracking with real-time syncing
  • Automated reorder points and purchase orders
  • B2B wholesale ordering portal
  • Demand forecasting and inventory planning
  • Integration with Xero, Shopify, Amazon, and more

Pricing

  • Starter Plan: $79/month – up to 1,000 SKUs, 2 users
  • Growth Plan: $199/month – 5,000 SKUs, 5 users
  • Scale Plan: $399/month – 10,000 SKUs, 10 users
  • Custom plans available for larger businesses

Pros

  • Excellent for B2B and wholesale operations
  • Robust forecasting and demand planning tools
  • Strong multi-warehouse management
  • Seamless QuickBooks integration

Cons

  • Higher price point for advanced features
  • Steep learning curve for new users
  • Limited customization on lower-tier plans

Best for: Growing wholesalers and distributors who need advanced stock control and multi-warehouse support.

3. Cin7 – Best for Complex Inventory Needs

Cin7 is an enterprise-grade inventory management solution designed for businesses with complex stock control requirements, such as multi-location, multi-currency, and multi-channel operations. It’s a comprehensive platform that can handle high-volume SKUs and intricate supply chains.

Key Features

  • Real-time inventory visibility across all locations
  • Advanced lot tracking and expiry date management
  • Manufacturing and bill of materials (BOM) support
  • EDI (Electronic Data Interchange) capabilities
  • Over 500 integrations with e-commerce, POS, and accounting systems

Pricing

  • Starter Plan: $399/month – up to 500 SKUs, 3 users
  • Essential Plan: $699/month – 2,000 SKUs, 5 users
  • Advanced Plan: $1,199/month – 10,000 SKUs, 10 users
  • Custom pricing for enterprise-level needs

Pros

  • Handles extremely complex stock control scenarios
  • Excellent for manufacturers and multi-channel retailers
  • Strong reporting and analytics
  • Dedicated support and onboarding

Cons

  • High cost, especially for smaller businesses
  • Can be overwhelming with too many features
  • Implementation may require professional services

Best for: Medium to large enterprises with complex supply chains, manufacturing, or high-volume stock control needs.

4. Odoo Inventory – Best Open-Source Option

Odoo Inventory is part of the Odoo ERP suite and offers a modular, open-source approach to stock control. It’s highly customizable and scalable, making it a favorite for businesses that want full control over their inventory system.

Key Features

  • Multi-warehouse and multi-location management
  • Barcode scanning and RFID support
  • Automated replenishment and reordering rules
  • Batch and serial number tracking
  • Integration with Odoo’s full suite of business apps

Pricing

  • Community Edition: Free (open-source, self-hosted)
  • Standard Cloud Plan: $24.90/user/month – includes inventory and other apps
  • Custom Enterprise Plans: Starting at $10,000/year for larger deployments

Pros

  • Free, open-source version available
  • Highly customizable with a large app ecosystem
  • Scalable from small to large businesses
  • Strong community support

Cons

  • Requires technical expertise for self-hosting
  • User interface can feel cluttered
  • Some advanced features require paid modules

Best for: Tech-savvy businesses that want a highly customizable, cost-effective stock control solution.
