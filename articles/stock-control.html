The Ultimate Guide to Mastering Stock Control: Best Inventory Management Software in 2025

If you run a business that sells physical products—whether you’re a small e-commerce store, a growing wholesaler, or a multi-warehouse operation—you know that stock control can make or break your bottom line. Poor inventory management leads to stockouts, overstocking, lost sales, and wasted capital. The right software, however, turns chaos into clarity.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll break down the best inventory management solutions for every budget and business size. We’ll cover pricing, key features, pros and cons, and give you our top recommendations. By the end, you’ll know exactly which tool will streamline your stock control and help you grow.

Why Proper Stock Control Matters

Before we dive into the software, let’s quickly touch on why stock control deserves your attention. Effective inventory management ensures you always have the right products in the right quantities. It prevents:

  • Lost revenue from out-of-stock items.
  • Cash flow problems from excess inventory.
  • Wasted time on manual counting and spreadsheets.
  • Errors in order fulfillment and shipping.

Automating your stock control with a dedicated system saves you hours each week and gives you real-time visibility into your business.

Top 5 Inventory Management Software for Stock Control

We’ve evaluated dozens of tools based on features, ease of use, scalability, and value. Here are our top picks for 2025.

1. Zoho Inventory – Best for Small to Mid-Size Businesses

Overview: Zoho Inventory is a cloud-based solution that integrates seamlessly with Zoho’s ecosystem (CRM, Books) and popular e-commerce platforms like Shopify, Amazon, and Etsy.

Key Features:

  • Multi-channel selling and order management
  • Real-time stock control across warehouses
  • Barcode scanning and serial number tracking
  • Automated reorder points and low-stock alerts
  • Batch tracking for expiry dates

Pricing:

  • Free plan: Up to 50 orders/month (limited features)
  • Standard plan: $59/month (billed annually)
  • Professional plan: $129/month
  • Enterprise plan: $299/month

Pros:

  • Affordable entry-level plan
  • Strong integrations with major sales channels
  • User-friendly interface

Cons:

  • Advanced features (e.g., demand forecasting) only in higher tiers
  • Customer support can be slow on free plan

Recommendation: Perfect for small to medium businesses already using Zoho apps or needing a budget-friendly stock control system.

2. Cin7 – Best for Multi-Channel & Multi-Warehouse Operations

Overview: Cin7 is a powerful inventory and order management platform designed for businesses that sell across multiple channels and manage multiple warehouses.

Key Features:

  • Centralized stock control for unlimited warehouses
  • Integration with 700+ apps (e-commerce, POS, accounting)
  • Advanced reporting and demand forecasting
  • Barcode scanning, pick-pack-ship workflows
  • Lot and serial number tracking

Pricing:

  • Starts at $399/month (billed annually) – includes up to 3 users
  • Growth plan: $499/month
  • Enterprise: Custom pricing

Pros:

  • Extremely scalable for growing businesses
  • Deep integrations with major platforms
  • Robust reporting and analytics

Cons:

  • Expensive for small businesses
  • Steep learning curve due to feature depth

Recommendation: Ideal for medium to large businesses with complex stock control needs across multiple sales channels and locations.

3. Fishbowl – Best for Manufacturing & Warehousing

Overview: Fishbowl is a desktop-based inventory management solution (with cloud options) that excels in manufacturing and warehouse environments.

Key Features:

  • Bill of materials (BOM) and work order management
  • Advanced stock control with bin locations
  • QuickBooks and Xero integration
  • RFID and barcode scanning
  • Customizable reporting

Pricing:

  • Starts at $4,395/year (single user) – perpetual license available
  • Additional users: ~$600/year each

Pros:

  • Excellent for manufacturing workflows
  • One-time license option reduces long-term costs
  • Strong inventory tracking for physical goods

Cons:

  • Desktop-first – less flexible for remote teams
  • Higher upfront cost
  • Limited e-commerce integrations

Recommendation: Best for manufacturers and warehouses that need robust stock control for raw materials and finished goods.

4. TradeGecko (Now QuickBooks Commerce) – Best for Wholesale & B2B

Overview: Acquired by Intuit, TradeGecko is now part of QuickBooks Commerce, offering a seamless integration for wholesale businesses.

Key Features:

  • Multi-warehouse stock control
  • B2B sales portal and customer-specific pricing
  • Purchase order management
  • Automated reordering and low-stock alerts
  • Integration with QuickBooks Online

Pricing:

  • Growth plan: $250/month (up to 10,000 SKUs)
  • Scale plan: $500/month (up to 50,000 SKUs)
  • Enterprise: Custom pricing

Pros:

  • Excellent for B2B wholesale operations
  • Native QuickBooks integration
  • User-friendly interface

Cons:

  • Limited customization compared to Cin7
  • No built-in e-commerce storefront

Recommendation: A top choice for wholesalers and B2B distributors who need streamlined stock control and accounting integration.

5. Odoo Inventory – Best Open-Source Option

Overview: Odoo is a modular ERP system with a powerful inventory module that can be used standalone or as part of a full suite.

Key Features:

  • Real-time stock control with multi-warehouse support
  • Barcode scanning and traceability
  • Automated replenishment rules
  • Dropshipping and cross-docking
  • Mobile app for on-the-go management

Pricing:

  • Community edition: Free (self-hosted, limited support)
  • Enterprise edition: Starts at $24.90/user/month (includes hosting and support)

Pros:

  • Highly customizable and scalable
  • Free version available for DIY users
  • Strong community and third-party modules
