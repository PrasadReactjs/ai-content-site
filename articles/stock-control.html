The Ultimate Guide to Mastering Stock Control: Best Inventory Management Software in 2025

If you’re running a business—whether it’s a small e-commerce store, a bustling warehouse, or a multi-location retail chain—you know that stock control is the backbone of your operations. Without a solid system, you’re flying blind: overstocking ties up cash, understocking loses sales, and manual tracking eats up hours. That’s where inventory management software steps in. In this guide, we’ll break down the best tools for stock control in 2025, including pricing, features, pros/cons, and our top recommendations to help you choose the right solution.

Whether you’re a solopreneur or managing a growing team, this article will give you actionable insights to streamline your stock control process, reduce waste, and boost profits. Let’s dive in.

Why Good Stock Control Matters

Before we compare tools, let’s quickly cover why stock control deserves your attention. Poor inventory management leads to:

  • Lost revenue from stockouts
  • Higher carrying costs from excess inventory
  • Wasted time on manual data entry
  • Customer dissatisfaction when orders are delayed or incorrect

Modern inventory management software automates these pain points. It gives you real-time visibility, demand forecasting, and multi-channel syncing—so you can focus on growing your business instead of counting boxes.

Top Inventory Management Software for Stock Control

We’ve evaluated the leading platforms based on features, pricing, user reviews, and suitability for different business sizes. Here are our top picks:

1. Zoho Inventory

Best for: Small to medium businesses (SMBs) looking for an affordable, feature-rich solution.

Key Features:

  • Multi-channel selling (Amazon, eBay, Shopify, Etsy)
  • Real-time stock control with barcode scanning
  • Order management and shipping integrations
  • Inventory forecasting and reorder point alerts
  • Warehouse management with bin locations

Pricing:

  • Free plan: Up to 50 orders/month (limited features)
  • Standard: $59/month (2,000 orders)
  • Professional: $129/month (5,000 orders)
  • Enterprise: Custom pricing

Pros:

  • Excellent integration with Zoho ecosystem (CRM, Books)
  • User-friendly interface
  • Scalable for growing businesses
  • Good mobile app for on-the-go stock control

Cons:

  • Some advanced features (like kitting) only in higher tiers
  • Customer support can be slow on free plan
  • Limited reporting compared to enterprise tools

Recommendation: Zoho Inventory is a solid choice if you’re already using Zoho apps or need a cost-effective way to manage stock control across multiple sales channels. It’s especially strong for small e-commerce businesses.

2. Cin7

Best for: Mid-sized to large businesses with complex inventory needs (e.g., multi-warehouse, manufacturing).

Key Features:

  • Centralized stock control across unlimited locations
  • B2B and B2C order management
  • Manufacturing and bill of materials (BOM) support
  • Advanced demand forecasting and analytics
  • 200+ integrations (QuickBooks, Xero, Shopify)

Pricing:

  • Starts at $349/month (3 users, 5,000 SKUs)
  • Custom pricing for higher volumes
  • No free plan; free demo available

Pros:

  • Handles high-volume and multi-location stock control seamlessly
  • Powerful reporting and dashboards
  • Great for companies that manufacture and sell
  • Dedicated onboarding support

Cons:

  • Steep learning curve for new users
  • Expensive for small businesses
  • Some users report occasional sync delays

Recommendation: If your stock control involves multiple warehouses, complex supply chains, or manufacturing, Cin7 is a powerhouse. It’s not cheap, but it can save you thousands in lost inventory.

3. TradeGecko (by QuickBooks Commerce)

Best for: Wholesale and distribution businesses that need tight integration with accounting.

Key Features:

  • Inventory and order management with real-time stock control
  • Automated purchase orders and reorder points
  • Multi-currency and multi-warehouse support
  • Integration with QuickBooks, Xero, and major e-commerce platforms
  • Demand forecasting and reporting

Pricing:

  • Starts at $79/month (2 users, 500 SKUs)
  • Business plan: $199/month (5 users, 5,000 SKUs)
  • Custom plans for larger needs
  • Free trial available (14 days)

Pros:

  • Excellent accounting integration (especially QuickBooks)
  • Clean, intuitive interface
  • Strong for B2B order management
  • Good customer support

Cons:

  • Limited customization for non-wholesale businesses
  • No built-in barcode scanning (requires third-party app)
  • Some features (like kitting) are basic

Recommendation: TradeGecko is a top pick for wholesalers and distributors who want seamless stock control with their accounting software. It’s affordable for mid-sized operations.

4. Fishbowl Inventory

Best for: Manufacturing and warehouse-focused businesses that need robust asset tracking.

Key Features:

  • Advanced stock control with lot and serial number tracking
  • Manufacturing and BOM management
  • Barcode scanning and mobile app
  • Integration with QuickBooks and Xero
  • Work order and time tracking

Pricing:

  • Starts at $4,395/year (single user, on-premise)
  • Cloud version: starting at $1,195/month (3 users)
  • Free demo available

Pros:

  • Very powerful for manufacturing and assembly
  • Excellent compliance features (FDA, ISO)
  • On-premise option for data-sensitive businesses
  • Strong barcode and RFID support

Cons:

  • High cost, especially for small businesses
  • Outdated interface (feels clunky)
  • Limited integrations beyond accounting

Recommendation: Fishbowl is a specialist tool for manufacturers that need granular stock control and traceability. It’s expensive but worth it for compliance-heavy industries.

5. Sortly

Best for: Small businesses, freelancers, and teams
