The Ultimate Guide to Inventory Management: Best Stock Control Software for 2025

If you run a business that sells physical products, you already know the headache of stock control. Running out of popular items means lost sales, while overstocking ties up cash and eats into your profit margins. The solution? A robust inventory management system that automates tracking, forecasting, and reordering.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll break down the best stock control software on the market, compare pricing and features, and help you choose the right tool for your business size and budget. Whether you’re a solo entrepreneur or a growing enterprise, these tools will save you time and money.

Why Good Stock Control Matters

Effective stock control (also called inventory management) is the backbone of a profitable product business. Without it, you’re flying blind. Here’s what a solid system does for you:

  • Prevents stockouts – Automatically reorder when inventory hits a low threshold.
  • Reduces carrying costs – Avoid paying for storage and insurance on dead stock.
  • Improves cash flow – Free up capital that would otherwise sit on shelves.
  • Boosts accuracy – Eliminate manual counting errors and spreadsheet chaos.
  • Scales with growth – Handle more SKUs, warehouses, and sales channels as you expand.

Now, let’s dive into the top solutions for stock control in 2025.

1. Zoho Inventory – Best for Small to Mid-Size Businesses

Zoho Inventory is a cloud-based stock control solution that integrates seamlessly with the Zoho ecosystem (CRM, Books, etc.) and major ecommerce platforms like Shopify, Amazon, and Etsy.

Key Features

  • Multi-channel inventory syncing (online stores, marketplaces, POS)
  • Barcode scanning and batch tracking
  • Automated reorder points and purchase orders
  • Warehouse management (up to 5 warehouses on the Standard plan)
  • Real-time reporting and analytics

Pricing

  • Free Plan: Up to 50 orders/month, 1 warehouse, basic features
  • Standard Plan: $59/month (billed annually) – 500 orders, 5 warehouses, automation
  • Professional Plan: $119/month – 3,000 orders, 10 warehouses, serial number tracking
  • Enterprise Plan: $199/month – 10,000 orders, unlimited warehouses

Pros

  • Excellent value for money, especially for small businesses
  • Deep integrations with Zoho apps and major ecommerce platforms
  • User-friendly interface with a short learning curve

Cons

  • Limited advanced features (like demand forecasting) compared to high-end tools
  • Customer support can be slow on lower-tier plans

Best For

Growing businesses already using Zoho products or needing an affordable, comprehensive stock control solution without a huge learning curve.

2. TradeGecko (by QuickBooks) – Best for Wholesale & B2B

TradeGecko, now owned by Intuit (makers of QuickBooks), is designed for wholesalers, distributors, and B2B businesses. It excels at managing complex stock control across multiple warehouses and sales channels.

Key Features

  • Inventory tracking with FIFO (First In, First Out) costing
  • B2B sales portal for wholesale customers
  • Purchase order management with supplier performance tracking
  • Demand forecasting and reorder suggestions
  • Integration with QuickBooks, Xero, Shopify, and more

Pricing

  • Starter Plan: $39/month – 1 user, 500 SKUs, 2 warehouses
  • Growth Plan: $79/month – 3 users, 2,000 SKUs, 5 warehouses
  • Business Plan: $159/month – 5 users, 10,000 SKUs, 10 warehouses
  • Enterprise Plan: Custom pricing for unlimited users and SKUs

Pros

  • Purpose-built for wholesale and B2B operations
  • Excellent demand forecasting to prevent overstocking
  • Seamless QuickBooks integration (ideal for existing Intuit users)

Cons

  • Not ideal for small retail or DTC (direct-to-consumer) businesses
  • Some features feel outdated compared to newer competitors

Best For

Wholesalers, distributors, and B2B companies that need robust stock control with multi-warehouse support and advanced costing methods.

3. Cin7 – Best for Large Enterprises & Complex Operations

Cin7 is an enterprise-grade inventory management platform that handles high-volume stock control across multiple locations, currencies, and sales channels. It’s a heavy-duty solution for businesses with complex supply chains.

Key Features

  • Real-time inventory visibility across unlimited warehouses
  • Advanced lot tracking, serial numbers, and expiry dates
  • Automated purchase orders and drop-ship support
  • Integrated POS for retail stores
  • Powerful reporting with customizable dashboards

Pricing

  • Starter Plan: $349/month – 2 users, 500 SKUs, 5 warehouses
  • Essential Plan: $599/month – 5 users, 2,000 SKUs, 10 warehouses
  • Advanced Plan: $1,199/month – 10 users, 10,000 SKUs, unlimited warehouses
  • Enterprise Plan: Custom pricing for large-scale deployments

Pros

  • Unmatched scalability for global operations
  • Comprehensive feature set (barcoding, kitting, manufacturing)
  • Excellent for businesses with complex inventory needs (e.g., food, pharma)

Cons

  • High price point – not suitable for small businesses
  • Steep learning curve due to the sheer number of features

Best For

Large enterprises, manufacturers, and distributors with complex stock control requirements, multiple warehouses, and a budget for premium software.

4. Sortly – Best for Visual Inventory Management

Sortly takes a unique visual approach to stock control. Instead of spreadsheets, you organize inventory with photos, barcodes, and folders. It’s perfect for businesses that deal with physical goods and want a simple, intuitive system.

Key Features

  • Visual inventory with photos and folders
  • Barcode and QR code scanning
  • Low-stock alerts and automated reordering
  • Multi-location tracking (up to 10 locations on Pro plan)
  • Integration with Shopify, WooCommerce, and QuickBooks

Pricing

  • Free Plan: 100 items, 1 user, basic features
  • Pro Plan: $35/month – 10,000 items, 3 users, 10 locations
  • Team Plan: $59/month – 100,000 items, 10 users, 50 locations
  • Business Plan: $119/month – unlimited items, 25 users, unlimited locations
