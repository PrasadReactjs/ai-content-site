Why a VPN Is a Must-Have Cybersecurity Tool

A VPN encrypts your internet traffic and routes it through a remote server, masking your IP address and protecting your data from prying eyes. Beyond privacy, it also:

  • Bypasses geo-blocks for streaming services (Netflix, BBC iPlayer, etc.)
  • Secures public Wi-Fi connections in cafes, airports, and hotels
  • Prevents ISP throttling and tracking
  • Shields your browsing from hackers and government surveillance

When evaluating cybersecurity tools, a VPN should be your first line of defense—but only if it’s fast, reliable, and transparent about its logging policies.

How to Choose the Right VPN for You

Not all cybersecurity tools are created equal. Here’s a quick checklist to narrow down your choice:

  • Privacy & Logging: Look for a no-logs policy that has been independently audited. Switzerland (ProtonVPN) and Panama (NordVPN) are privacy-friendly jurisdictions.
  • Speed: If you stream in 4K or game online, prioritize fast protocols like WireGuard or Lightway (ExpressVPN).
  • Server Network: More servers in more countries mean better speeds and more geo-unblocking options.
  • Simultaneous Connections: Surfshark offers unlimited connections; others cap at 5–10.
  • Extra Features: Ad blocking, split tunneling, and kill switches add layers of protection.
  • Price vs. Value: Long-term plans offer big discounts, but check renewal rates.

Practical Tips for Using a VPN Effectively

Even the best VPN can underperform if you don’t configure it correctly. Follow these tips to maximize your cybersecurity tools:

  • Always enable the kill switch – This cuts internet access if the VPN drops, preventing data leaks.
  • Use split tunneling – Route only sensitive traffic (like banking) through the VPN while letting other apps connect directly for speed.
  • Choose the right protocol – WireGuard is fast and modern; OpenVPN is more secure but slower. Use NordLynx (NordVPN) or Lightway (ExpressVPN) for the best balance.
