Calendly vs Cal.com: The Ultimate Scheduling Showdown (2025)

If you’ve ever tried to schedule a meeting without endless email chains, you already know the value of a good booking tool. For years, Calendly has been the undisputed king of appointment scheduling. But a powerful open-source challenger, Cal.com, has emerged, promising more control, better pricing, and developer-friendly flexibility. Which one is right for you? In this detailed comparison, we’ll break down features, pricing, customization, and use cases to help you decide. By the end, you’ll know exactly which platform fits your workflow—and why Cal.com might be the smarter long-term investment.

What Are Calendly and Cal.com?

Before diving into the nitty-gritty, let’s define each platform.

Calendly

Launched in 2013, Calendly is a proprietary, cloud-based scheduling tool. It’s known for its simplicity: you set your availability, share a link, and let others book time slots. It integrates with Google Calendar, Outlook, Zoom, Salesforce, and hundreds of other apps. Calendly is widely used by sales teams, recruiters, freelancers, and enterprise organizations.

Cal.com

Cal.com (formerly Calendso) launched in 2021 as an open-source alternative. It offers the same core functionality—meeting scheduling, time zone detection, and calendar sync—but with a twist: you can self-host it on your own server, customize the code, and avoid vendor lock-in. Cal.com also offers a hosted cloud version (cal.com) for those who prefer a managed experience. Its open-source nature makes it a favorite among developers, privacy-conscious users, and cost-conscious startups.

Head-to-Head Feature Comparison

Let’s compare the most important features side by side.

1. Pricing

Calendly has a free tier (limited to one event type) and paid plans starting at $10/month (Essentials), $16/month (Teams), and custom Enterprise pricing. The free tier is quite restrictive—no team features, no round-robin, and limited integrations.

Cal.com offers a free cloud tier with unlimited event types. Paid cloud plans start at $12/month (Teams) and $24/month (Enterprise). But the real game-changer: Cal.com is open source, so you can self-host for free (you only pay for your own server). This makes Cal.com dramatically cheaper for anyone with basic technical skills.

Winner: Cal.com – The self-hosting option is unbeatable for budget-conscious users.

2. Customization & Branding

Calendly allows limited branding: you can add your logo and change colors on paid plans. But the booking page still looks distinctly like Calendly.

Cal.com offers full white-labeling on paid plans. You can customize colors, fonts, logos, and even the URL. Self-hosted users have complete control over the UI and can even modify the source code. This is a huge win for agencies and businesses that want a seamless brand experience.

Winner: Cal.com – Unmatched customization, especially for developers.

3. Integrations

Both platforms support major calendar services (Google, Outlook, Apple, and Exchange). Both integrate with Zoom, Google Meet, and Microsoft Teams. But Calendly has a larger native integration library, including Salesforce, HubSpot, Slack, Stripe, and Zapier. Cal.com covers the essentials and supports Zapier, but its native integration list is shorter. However, Cal.com’s open API and webhooks make it easy to build custom integrations.

Winner: Calendly – More out-of-the-box integrations for popular business tools.

4. Team Features

Both platforms offer team scheduling: round-robin, collective, and fixed routing. Calendly’s team features are polished and easy to set up. Cal.com offers similar functionality, but the interface is slightly less intuitive. That said, Cal.com’s “Teams” plan is cheaper per seat and allows more granular permissions.

Winner: Tie – Calendly for ease of use, Cal.com for cost and flexibility.

5. Privacy & Data Control

This is where Cal.com shines. Because it’s open source and can be self-hosted, you retain full control over your data. No third-party servers process your booking information. Calendly, being a closed SaaS, stores all data on its servers—a concern for businesses with strict compliance requirements (GDPR, HIPAA, etc.). Cal.com also supports end-to-end encryption for self-hosted instances.

Winner: Cal.com – Unquestionably superior for privacy and compliance.

6. User Experience

Calendly is famously easy to use. You can set up a booking link in under 5 minutes. The interface is clean, modern, and beginner-friendly. Cal.com’s cloud version is also user-friendly, but the setup process has more steps (e.g., choosing between hosted and self-hosted). Self-hosting requires technical know-how. For non-technical users, Calendly wins on simplicity.

Winner: Calendly – Smoother onboarding and less friction.

7. Developer & API Capabilities

Cal.com is built for developers. It offers a REST API, GraphQL API, webhooks, and the ability to fork the entire codebase. You can embed the scheduler anywhere, build custom workflows, and even contribute to the open-source project. Calendly’s API is also powerful, but it’s proprietary and limited to what the company exposes. With Cal.com, you are not at the mercy of a vendor’s roadmap.

Winner: Cal.com – Unrivaled extensibility.

When to Choose Calendly

  • You value simplicity above all else. If you just want a booking link that works instantly, Calendly is the easiest path.
  • You rely on deep integrations. Sales teams using Salesforce or HubSpot will appreciate Calendly’s native connections.
  • You have a non-technical team. Calendly requires zero setup or maintenance.
  • You don’t need white-labeling. If branding isn’t critical, Calendly’s out-of-the-box experience is hard to beat.

When to Choose Cal.com

  • You want to save money. Self-hosting Cal.com is free, and even the hosted cloud plan is cheaper than Calendly’s equivalent tier.
  • You need full control over data. For healthcare, finance, or any regulated industry, self-hosted Cal.com is the only compliant choice.
  • You want complete brand customization. Agencies and product-led companies can make Cal.com look like their own product.
  • You have development resources. If you can spare a few hours to set up a server, Cal.com offers unmatched flexibility.
  • You believe in open source. Supporting open-source software means you’re not locked into a proprietary ecosystem.

Practical Tips for Making the Switch

If you’re considering moving from Calendly to Cal.com, here’s a step-by-step plan:

  1. Try the hosted Cal.com cloud version first. Sign up for a free account at cal.com and test the features. This gives you a feel for the interface without any commitment.
  2. Export your event types. Calendly allows you to export your event types as JSON. You can then import them into Cal.com (or manually recreate them—it’s quick).
  3. Set up calendar sync. Connect your Google or Outlook calendar to Cal.com. Ensure your availability is mirrored correctly.
  4. Test with a few colleagues. Before switching all your clients, have internal team members book test meetings to verify time zones, notifications, and integrations work.
  5. Update your links. Replace your Calendly links in email signatures, social media bios, and websites with your new Cal.com links.
