Calendly vs Cal.com: The Ultimate Scheduling Showdown for 2024

In the world of online scheduling, two names dominate the conversation: Calendly and Cal.com. Both tools promise to eliminate the back-and-forth of meeting coordination, but they cater to very different audiences. While Calendly is the established market leader with a polished user experience, Cal.com is the open-source challenger that offers unparalleled customization and cost savings. This detailed comparison will help you decide which platform is right for your business, team, or personal workflow.

Let’s dive into the specifics—pricing, features, privacy, integrations, and use cases—so you can make an informed decision.

1. Pricing: The Biggest Differentiator

Pricing is often the first dealbreaker for users, and this is where Cal.com truly shines.

Calendly Pricing

  • Free Plan: Limited to one event type and one calendar connection. Basic features only.
  • Essentials: $10/month (billed annually) – unlocks multiple event types, custom reminders, and payment integrations.
  • Professional: $16/month – adds team scheduling, round-robin, and workflow automation.
  • Teams: $20/month per user – includes Salesforce, HubSpot, and advanced analytics.
  • Enterprise: Custom pricing – for large organizations with advanced security needs.

Cal.com Pricing

  • Free Plan: Generous – includes unlimited event types, multiple calendars, and 1 user. No time limits on meetings.
  • Essentials: $12/month (billed annually) – adds team features, round-robin, and custom routing.
  • Team: $28/month (up to 3 users) – includes workflows, analytics, and priority support.
  • Enterprise: Custom pricing – with SSO, audit logs, and dedicated support.
  • Self-Hosted (Open Source): Completely free – you run it on your own server. This is a game-changer for privacy-conscious users and developers.

Winner: Cal.com. Its free plan is far more robust, and the self-hosted option eliminates recurring costs entirely. If you're a solo professional or a startup on a budget, Cal.com is the clear choice.

2. Core Features: Ease of Use vs. Customization

Calendly is known for its simplicity. You set your availability, share a link, and let the system handle the rest. It’s intuitive, with a clean interface that requires almost no learning curve. Features like automated reminders, buffer times, and group polling work seamlessly out of the box.

Cal.com, on the other hand, is built for flexibility. While its default interface is slightly less polished, it offers deep customization options:

  • Custom booking flows and conditional logic
  • Multiple event types with advanced routing (e.g., "Book first available slot with any team member")
  • Built-in video conferencing (Zoom, Google Meet, Microsoft Teams, and Jitsi for self-hosted)
  • Webhooks and API access for developers
  • Custom branding and white-labeling on paid plans

Winner: It depends. For non-technical users who want a "set it and forget it" experience, Calendly wins. For developers, power users, or businesses that need tailored workflows, Cal.com is superior.

3. Privacy and Data Control

Data sovereignty is a growing concern for businesses, especially those in regulated industries like healthcare or finance.

Calendly stores all your data on its servers. While it is GDPR-compliant and offers SOC 2 certification (on Enterprise plans), you ultimately rely on their security infrastructure.

Cal.com offers a unique advantage: self-hosting. By deploying the open-source version on your own server (using Docker or a cloud provider like AWS), you retain full control over your data. No third party has access to your calendar events, attendee information, or booking history. This is a massive win for privacy advocates and companies with strict compliance requirements.

Winner: Cal.com, hands down. The ability to self-host is a feature no other major scheduling tool offers.

4. Integrations and Ecosystem

Both platforms integrate with the most popular calendar services (Google, Outlook, iCloud) and video conferencing tools. However, the depth of integrations differs.

Calendly Integrations

  • Native connections to Salesforce, HubSpot, Slack, and Mailchimp
  • Zapier integration for thousands of apps
  • Payment processing via Stripe and PayPal
  • Marketing automation triggers (e.g., add leads to CRM after booking)

Cal.com Integrations

  • All major calendar and video platforms
  • Open API and webhooks for custom integrations
  • Zapier and Make (formerly Integromat) support
  • Built-in payment gateways (Stripe, PayPal, and more)
  • Third-party apps via the Cal.com App Store (community-built plugins)

Winner: Calendly has a slight edge in out-of-the-box CRM integrations, but Cal.com offers more flexibility for custom builds. If you rely heavily on Salesforce or HubSpot, Calendly is smoother. If you want to connect to a niche tool or build a custom pipeline, Cal.com is better.

5. Team and Collaboration Features

Both tools support team scheduling, but the approach differs.

Calendly offers round-robin, collective availability, and "first available" routing. It’s easy to set up a team page where clients can book with any available member. However, managing permissions and visibility can be limited on lower-tier plans.

Cal.com provides similar team features but adds more granular control. You can create custom booking flows that route leads based on criteria (e.g., location, language, or expertise). The "managed events" feature allows admins to create templates for the whole team. Plus, the open-source nature means you can modify the code to add custom logic.

Winner: Cal.com, especially for larger teams or those with complex routing needs. Calendly is easier for small teams but less flexible at scale.

6. User Interface and Experience

Calendly’s interface is polished, modern, and extremely user-friendly. Booking a meeting feels effortless for both the host and the attendee. The mobile app is also well-designed and functional.

Cal.com has improved significantly but still feels slightly more "technical." The default UI is clean, but some advanced options are hidden behind menus. The mobile experience is functional but not as refined as Calendly’s. However, with self-hosting, you can completely redesign the booking page using custom CSS and HTML.

Winner: Calendly, for its superior out-of-the-box user experience. Cal.com catches up if you invest time in customization.

7. Scalability and Enterprise Readiness

Calendly is battle-tested at scale, with large enterprises like Uber and Zendesk using it. Their Enterprise plan offers SSO, audit logs, and dedicated support. It’s a safe choice for organizations that need reliability and compliance.

Cal.com is newer to the enterprise space but is rapidly maturing. Its self-hosted option is ideal for companies that want to avoid vendor lock-in. The API-first design also makes it easy to embed scheduling into custom applications. However, enterprise support and advanced security features (like SAML SSO) are only available on the paid Enterprise plan.

Winner: Calendly for traditional enterprises. Cal.com for tech-forward companies that prioritize data control and customization.
