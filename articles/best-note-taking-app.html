The Ultimate Guide to Finding the Best Note Taking App in 2025

In a world overflowing with information, the ability to capture, organize, and retrieve your thoughts quickly is no longer a luxury—it's a necessity. Whether you're a student juggling lecture notes, a professional managing project ideas, or a creative brainstorming your next big project, the best note taking app can transform your productivity. But with dozens of options flooding the market, how do you choose the perfect one for your workflow?

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll break down the top contenders for the best note taking app of 2025. We'll compare pricing, features, pros, and cons, and offer actionable recommendations to help you make an informed decision. Let's dive in.

Why You Need a Dedicated Note Taking App

Before we compare apps, it's worth understanding why a dedicated tool beats a simple text file or sticky notes. A good note taking app offers:

  • Organization: Folders, tags, and search functionality keep your notes accessible.
  • Syncing: Access your notes across all your devices—phone, tablet, and computer.
  • Rich media support: Add images, audio recordings, PDFs, and even sketches.
  • Collaboration: Share notes with teammates or classmates in real-time.
  • Backup and security: Never lose your ideas again.

Now, let's explore the top apps vying for the title of best note taking app.

1. Notion – The All-in-One Powerhouse

Notion has taken the productivity world by storm. It's not just a note taking app; it's a workspace that combines notes, databases, wikis, and project management. For users who want a single tool to replace multiple apps, Notion is arguably the best note taking app available.

Key Features

  • Block-based editor (text, images, tables, code, embeds)
  • Relational databases with views (table, board, calendar, gallery, timeline)
  • Templates for everything from meeting notes to habit trackers
  • Real-time collaboration and comments
  • API and integrations with Slack, Google Drive, and more
  • AI assistant for summarizing and generating content

Pricing

  • Free: Unlimited pages and blocks, 7-day page history, up to 10 guests
  • Plus: $10/month (billed annually) – unlimited file uploads, 30-day page history
  • Business: $18/month (billed annually) – advanced permissions, 90-day page history
  • Enterprise: Custom pricing – advanced security and admin controls

Pros

  • Extremely flexible – adapts to any workflow
  • Powerful database features (great for project management)
  • Beautiful, clean interface
  • Generous free tier

Cons

  • Steep learning curve for beginners
  • Can feel overwhelming with too many options
  • Offline mode is limited on mobile
  • No native handwriting support

Best For

Power users, teams, and anyone who wants a customizable workspace. If you love organizing your life with databases and templates, Notion is the best note taking app for you.

2. Evernote – The Veteran with Staying Power

Evernote has been a household name in note taking for over a decade. Despite newer competitors, it remains a top contender for the best note taking app thanks to its robust feature set and mature ecosystem.

Key Features

  • Powerful OCR (optical character recognition) for searching inside images and PDFs
  • Web clipper for saving articles and web pages
  • Notebooks and tags for organization
  • Audio recording with playback
  • Handwriting and sketch support
  • AI-powered search and suggestions
  • Integrations with Google Calendar, Outlook, and Slack

Pricing

  • Free: 60 MB monthly uploads, syncs up to 2 devices
  • Personal: $14.99/month (billed annually) – 10 GB monthly uploads, unlimited devices
  • Professional: $17.99/month (billed annually) – 20 GB monthly uploads, AI features
  • Teams: $27.99/month (billed annually) – collaboration and admin tools

Pros

  • Excellent search functionality (finds text in handwritten notes and images)
  • Reliable syncing across platforms
  • Great web clipper for research
  • Mature and well-supported

Cons

  • Free tier is very limited (only 2 devices, low uploads)
  • Can become slow with large notebooks
  • Outdated editor compared to newer apps
  • Expensive for premium features

Best For

Researchers, journalists, and heavy web clippers. If you need to save and search through massive amounts of information, Evernote is still the best note taking app for that specific use case.

3. Obsidian – The Knowledge Graph King

Obsidian has gained a cult following among note-takers who love interconnected thinking. It uses Markdown files stored locally, and its unique "graph view" visualizes connections between your notes. For anyone building a personal knowledge base, Obsidian is often the best note taking app.

Key Features

  • Local Markdown files (privacy-first, offline by default)
  • Backlinks and graph view to see connections
  • Plugin ecosystem (community-built plugins for nearly everything)
  • Canvas for visual brainstorming
  • Daily notes and templates
  • Obsidian Sync and Publish (paid add-ons)

Pricing

  • Free: Full app, all core features, community plugins
  • Sync: $5/month (billed annually) – encrypted sync across devices
  • Publish: $10/month (billed annually) – host a website from your notes
  • Commercial: $50/user/year – for workplace usage

Pros

  • Complete privacy and ownership of your data
  • Extremely powerful linking and graph capabilities
  • Highly customizable with plugins
  • Fast and lightweight

Cons

  • Steep learning curve for non-technical users
  • No native mobile handwriting support
  • Syncing requires a paid add-on or third-party service
  • Limited collaboration features

Best For

Writers, researchers, and developers who love Markdown and want to build a "second brain." If you value privacy and deep connections between ideas, Obsidian is the best note taking app for you.

4. Microsoft OneNote – The Free Champion

OneNote is Microsoft's freeform note taking app that comes bundled with Office. It's incredibly generous with its free tier and offers a unique canvas-based approach that mimics a physical notebook. For budget-conscious users, it's arguably the best note taking app.

Key Features

  • Freeform canvas (click anywhere to start typing)
  • Handwriting and drawing with a stylus
  • Audio and video recording synced to notes
  • Powerful integration with Microsoft 365
  • Tagging system for to-do lists and priorities
  • Search within images and handwritten text
  • Cross-platform (Windows, Mac, iOS, Android, Web)

Pricing

  • Free: 5 GB storage, full features, unlimited devices
  • Microsoft 365 Personal: $69.99/year – 1 TB storage, Office apps
  • Microsoft 365 Family
