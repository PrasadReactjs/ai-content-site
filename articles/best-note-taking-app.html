The Ultimate Guide to Finding the Best Note Taking App in 2025

In a world overflowing with information, the ability to capture, organize, and retrieve your thoughts quickly can make or break your productivity. Whether you're a student juggling lecture notes, a professional managing project ideas, or a creative collecting inspiration, the best note taking app is your digital second brain. But with dozens of options on the market, each boasting unique features, how do you choose the right one?

In this comprehensive guide, we'll break down the top contenders for the best note taking app in 2025. We'll compare pricing, dive into features, weigh pros and cons, and give you actionable recommendations so you can start capturing ideas like a pro—without the overwhelm.

Why You Need a Dedicated Note Taking App

Before we jump into the list, let's address the "why." A generic text editor or a pile of sticky notes might work for a day, but a dedicated note taking app offers:

  • Organization: Folders, tags, and search functions keep everything findable.
  • Sync Across Devices: Access your notes on your phone, tablet, and laptop seamlessly.
  • Rich Media Support: Embed images, audio recordings, PDFs, and even handwriting.
  • Collaboration: Share notes and work on them with your team in real time.
  • Backup & Security: Never lose a brilliant idea again.

Now, let's explore the apps that are vying for the title of best note taking app.

1. Notion – The All-in-One Powerhouse

Best for: Project management, databases, and teams who want a flexible workspace.

Pricing

  • Free Plan: Unlimited pages, blocks, and up to 7 collaborators.
  • Plus Plan: $10/month (billed annually) – unlimited file uploads, 30-day version history.
  • Business Plan: $18/month per user – advanced permissions, team analytics.
  • Enterprise: Custom pricing.

Key Features

  • Powerful database & wiki functionality
  • Kanban boards, calendars, and timeline views
  • Rich block-based editor (type, drag, drop anything)
  • Templates for everything from meeting notes to habit trackers
  • Strong API and third-party integrations (Slack, Zapier, Google Drive)

Pros

  • Extremely customizable – adapts to any workflow
  • Excellent for collaborative projects and team wikis
  • Combines notes, tasks, and databases in one place

Cons

  • Steep learning curve for new users
  • Can feel overwhelming for simple note-taking
  • Offline mode is limited on mobile

Verdict: Notion is a serious contender for the best note taking app if you need a robust system that goes beyond simple notes. It's ideal for power users and teams.

2. Evernote – The Veteran with AI Smarts

Best for: Professional organization, web clipping, and heavy-duty document scanning.

Pricing

  • Free Plan: 60 MB monthly uploads, sync across 2 devices.
  • Personal Plan: $14.99/month – 10 GB uploads, offline access, AI-powered search.
  • Professional Plan: $17.99/month – 20 GB uploads, calendar integration, task automation.
  • Teams: $24.99/month per user.

Key Features

  • Powerful web clipper (save entire articles, emails, or screenshots)
  • AI-powered search that can find text inside images and PDFs
  • Document scanning with optical character recognition (OCR)
  • Notebooks, stacks, and tags for deep organization
  • Task management and calendar integration

Pros

  • Industry-leading web clipping and document scanning
  • Excellent search capabilities
  • Mature ecosystem with years of refinement

Cons

  • Free tier is very limited (2 devices, low uploads)
  • Can feel bloated and slow on older devices
  • Interface has become cluttered over the years

Verdict: Evernote remains a strong choice for the best note taking app if you do a lot of research, web clipping, and need powerful search. However, its pricing has become steep.

3. Obsidian – The Local-First Knowledge Base

Best for: Writers, researchers, and developers who value privacy and linking ideas.

Pricing

  • Free Plan: Unlimited local notes, core features.
  • Catalyst License: $25+ one-time (early access, supports development).
  • Commercial License: $50/user/year (for work use).
  • Sync & Publish: $5/month each.

Key Features

  • All notes stored as plain Markdown files on your local drive
  • Bi-directional linking and graph view to visualize connections
  • Extensive plugin community (over 1,000 plugins)
  • Fully offline by default
  • Customizable themes and editor

Pros

  • Complete privacy and ownership of your data
  • Incredibly fast and lightweight
  • Ideal for building a "second brain" (Zettelkasten method)

Cons

  • No built-in cloud sync (requires paid add-on or third-party tool)
  • Steep learning curve for linking and plugins
  • Mobile app is less polished than desktop

Verdict: Obsidian is the best note taking app for anyone who wants total control over their data and loves building interconnected knowledge. It's a top pick for researchers and writers.

4. Microsoft OneNote – The Free & Familiar Option

Best for: Students, Windows users, and anyone who wants a free, feature-rich app.

Pricing

  • Free Plan: 5 GB of OneDrive storage, unlimited notes, all features.
  • Microsoft 365 Personal: $6.99/month – 1 TB OneDrive storage, Office apps.
  • Microsoft 365 Family: $9.99/month – up to 6 users, 6 TB total storage.

Key Features

  • Freeform canvas – click anywhere to type or draw
  • Excellent handwriting and drawing support (best in class on tablets)
  • Audio recording synced with notes
  • Powerful integration with Outlook, Teams, and Office
  • Cross-platform (Windows, Mac, iOS, Android, Web)

Pros

  • Completely free with a Microsoft account
  • Intuitive for students (especially with a stylus)
  • Seamless integration with the Microsoft ecosystem

Cons

  • Limited formatting options compared to competitors
  • Search can be slow with large notebooks
  • No built-in task management or databases
