The Ultimate Guide to Finding the Best Note Taking App in 2025

In a world overflowing with information, the ability to capture, organize, and retrieve your thoughts quickly can be a game-changer. Whether you're a student juggling lecture notes, a professional managing project ideas, or a creative brainstorming your next big project, the best note taking app can transform chaos into clarity. But with dozens of options on the market, how do you choose the right one?

This comprehensive guide breaks down the top contenders, comparing pricing, features, pros, and cons. By the end, you’ll have a clear recommendation tailored to your workflow. Let’s dive in.

What Makes a Note Taking App the "Best"?

Before comparing apps, it’s important to define your needs. The best note taking app for you should offer:

  • Cross-platform syncing – Access notes on phone, tablet, and desktop.
  • Organization tools – Tags, folders, notebooks, or databases.
  • Search functionality – Including OCR for handwritten notes.
  • Export options – PDF, Markdown, or plain text.
  • Collaboration features – Sharing and real-time editing.
  • Pricing that fits your budget – Free tiers vs. premium subscriptions.

Now, let’s review the top five contenders for the title of best note taking app.

1. Notion – The All-in-One Powerhouse

Notion has exploded in popularity because it blurs the line between note-taking, project management, and databases. It’s ideal for users who want a single workspace for everything.

Key Features

  • Rich text editor with markdown shortcuts
  • Database views (table, board, calendar, gallery, list)
  • Templates for notes, wikis, and projects
  • Real-time collaboration
  • Web clipper and integrations (Slack, Google Drive, Figma)
  • AI assistant for summarization and writing

Pricing

  • Free: Unlimited blocks for personal use, 7-day page history, 5MB file uploads
  • Plus: $10/month – Unlimited file uploads, 30-day page history
  • Business: $18/month – Advanced collaboration, admin tools
  • Enterprise: Custom pricing

Pros

  • Extremely flexible – can be a note app, wiki, or project manager
  • Powerful database features for organizing complex information
  • Great for team collaboration

Cons

  • Steep learning curve – not ideal for simple, quick notes
  • Offline mode is limited on mobile
  • Can feel slow with large databases

Best for: Power users, teams, and anyone who wants a second brain for work and life.

2. Evernote – The Veteran with OCR Power

Evernote has been a staple for years, and its strength lies in its robust search and organization. It remains one of the best note taking app options for research-heavy users.

Key Features

  • OCR search – finds text inside images and PDFs
  • Notebooks and tags for organization
  • Web clipper with annotation
  • Audio recording with playback
  • Calendar integration
  • Task management

Pricing

  • Free: 60MB monthly upload, 1 device, basic search
  • Personal: $14.99/month – 10GB upload, unlimited devices, OCR, PDF annotation
  • Professional: $17.99/month – 20GB upload, AI-powered search
  • Teams: $27.99/user/month

Pros

  • Best-in-class search, including handwritten and scanned text
  • Excellent web clipper for saving articles
  • Mature, reliable platform

Cons

  • Free tier is very limited (only one device)
  • Expensive compared to competitors
  • Editor feels outdated and clunky

Best for: Researchers, journalists, and users who need to search through thousands of notes and documents.

3. Obsidian – The Local-First Knowledge Base

Obsidian has taken the "personal knowledge management" world by storm. It stores all notes as plain Markdown files on your local device, giving you complete ownership and privacy.

Key Features

  • Local Markdown files – no vendor lock-in
  • Graph view to visualize connections between notes
  • Plugin system (thousands of community plugins)
  • Backlinks and bidirectional linking
  • Daily notes and templates
  • Obsidian Sync (paid add-on)

Pricing

  • Free: All core features, unlimited notes, community plugins
  • Sync: $5/month – Encrypted sync across devices
  • Publish: $10/month – Host notes as a website
  • Catalyst: $25+ one-time – Early access and supporter badge

Pros

  • Complete data ownership – notes are just text files
  • Extremely fast and lightweight
  • Highly customizable with plugins

Cons

  • Steep learning curve for non-technical users
  • No native web clipper (third-party solutions exist)
  • Sync and publish require paid plans

Best for: Developers, writers, and knowledge workers who value privacy and long-term note longevity.

4. Microsoft OneNote – The Free Powerhouse

OneNote is often overlooked, but it’s a surprisingly capable app, especially if you’re already in the Microsoft ecosystem. It’s free for most users and offers a unique notebook structure.

Key Features

  • Freeform canvas – click anywhere to type
  • Notebooks, sections, and pages hierarchy
  • Handwriting and drawing support
  • Audio recording synced to notes
  • Tagging system (to-do, important, etc.)
  • Integration with Outlook and Teams

Pricing

  • Free: 5GB storage via OneDrive, all features
  • Microsoft 365 Personal: $6.99/month – 1TB storage, desktop apps
  • Microsoft 365 Family: $9.99/month – Up to 6 users

Pros

  • Completely free with generous storage
  • Excellent handwriting and drawing support (great for tablets)
  • Seamless integration with Windows and Office apps

Cons

  • No Markdown support
  • Search is not as powerful as Evernote
  • Mobile app can be slow and buggy

Best for: Students with tablets, Windows users, and anyone who wants a free, robust note-taking solution.

5. Bear – The Elegant Writer’s Companion

Bear is a beautifully designed app exclusive to Apple devices. It focuses on simplicity and speed, making it a favorite among writers and creatives.

Key Features

  • Markdown editor with live preview
  • Tag-based organization (nested tags)
  • Theme customization
  • Export to PDF, HTML, DOCX, and more
  • iCloud sync across Apple devices
  • Advanced encryption options

Pricing

  • Free: Basic features, no sync, limited export
  • Bear Pro: $2.99/month or $29.99/year – Sync, themes, export, more
