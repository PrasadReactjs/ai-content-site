The Ultimate Guide to the Best Email Marketing Tools for Marketing Automation in 2025

Email marketing remains one of the highest-ROI channels for businesses—generating an average of $36 for every $1 spent. But the real game-changer is marketing automation. Without it, you're manually sending emails, segmenting lists, and hoping for the best. With it, you can nurture leads, trigger personalized sequences, and scale your revenue without burning out.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll break down the best email marketing platforms for marketing automation, including detailed pricing, features, pros/cons, and honest recommendations. Whether you're a solopreneur, a growing ecommerce brand, or a B2B SaaS company, you'll find the perfect tool to automate your email marketing and grow your bottom line.

What Is Marketing Automation in Email Marketing?

Marketing automation refers to software that automates repetitive marketing tasks—like sending welcome emails, abandoned cart reminders, or re-engagement sequences—based on user behavior, time triggers, or data points. In email marketing, this means you can create complex workflows that send the right message to the right person at the right time, without manual intervention.

The best email marketing platforms combine drag-and-drop builders, advanced segmentation, A/B testing, and analytics to make automation accessible for everyone.

Top 5 Email Marketing Platforms for Marketing Automation

After testing dozens of tools and analyzing user reviews, here are the five best email marketing platforms that excel in marketing automation.

1. ActiveCampaign – Best for Advanced Automation & CRM

Best for: Small to medium businesses, ecommerce, B2B, and marketers who need deep automation logic.

Key Features

  • Visual automation builder with conditional logic (if/then, splits, delays)
  • Built-in CRM with deal tracking and lead scoring
  • Site tracking, event tracking, and predictive sending
  • Over 870 integrations (Shopify, WordPress, Salesforce, etc.)
  • Dynamic content and personalization tags

Pricing

  • Starter: $15/month (1,000 contacts, unlimited emails)
  • Plus: $49/month (1,000 contacts, includes landing pages, lead scoring)
  • Professional: $79/month (1,000 contacts, predictive sending, split automation)
  • Enterprise: Custom pricing (custom reporting, dedicated support)

Pros

  • Unmatched automation depth—you can build complex, multi-branch workflows
  • Excellent email deliverability rates
  • CRM integration is seamless for sales teams

Cons

  • Steep learning curve for beginners
  • Email templates are less modern than competitors
  • Price increases quickly as your list grows

Recommendation

If you're serious about marketing automation and need advanced logic, lead scoring, and CRM integration, ActiveCampaign is the gold standard. It's ideal for businesses that want to automate entire customer journeys from lead to sale.

2. Mailchimp – Best for Beginners & Small Businesses

Best for: Newbies, freelancers, and small ecommerce stores with simple automation needs.

Key Features

  • Pre-built automation templates (welcome, abandoned cart, re-engagement)
  • Drag-and-drop email builder with smart design tools
  • Customer journey builder (visual, but simpler than ActiveCampaign)
  • Built-in audience segmentation and tags
  • Free plan available (up to 500 contacts, 1,000 emails/month)

Pricing

  • Free: $0/month (500 contacts, basic templates, Mailchimp branding)
  • Essentials: $13/month (500 contacts, 3 seats, A/B testing)
  • Standard: $20/month (500 contacts, advanced automation, retargeting)
  • Premium: $350/month (10,000 contacts, advanced segmentation, phone support)

Pros

  • Extremely user-friendly interface
  • Great free plan for testing
  • Strong integration with ecommerce platforms (Shopify, WooCommerce)

Cons

  • Automation is limited compared to ActiveCampaign or ConvertKit
  • Price jumps dramatically as your list grows
  • Customer support can be slow on lower tiers

Recommendation

Mailchimp is perfect for absolute beginners or small businesses that only need basic marketing automation (e.g., welcome series, birthday emails). However, as you scale, you'll likely outgrow its capabilities.

3. ConvertKit – Best for Creators & Bloggers

Best for: Authors, course creators, YouTubers, and content-focused businesses.

Key Features

  • Visual automation builder with triggers, conditions, and actions
  • Tag-based segmentation (instead of lists—very flexible)
  • Landing pages and opt-in forms with no coding
  • Subscriber scoring and custom fields
  • Built-in digital product sales (digital downloads, paid newsletters)

Pricing

  • Free: $0/month (up to 1,000 subscribers, unlimited emails, limited automation)
  • Creator: $29/month (1,000 subscribers, all automation, landing pages, email sequences)
  • Creator Pro: $59/month (1,000 subscribers, subscriber scoring, advanced reporting)

Pros

  • Clean, distraction-free editor
  • Excellent for building email sequences (automated courses, launch sequences)
  • Tagging system is powerful for segmenting engaged vs. cold subscribers

Cons

  • No built-in CRM or ecommerce features (apart from basic sales)
  • Email templates are plain text—no fancy drag-and-drop design
  • Limited reporting compared to ActiveCampaign

Recommendation

If you're a creator who wants to build automated email funnels (e.g., a 5-day email challenge or a product launch sequence), ConvertKit is your best bet. Its tag-based automation makes it easy to send targeted content based on subscriber interests.

4. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) – Best for Budget-Conscious Marketers

Best for: Startups, nonprofits, and businesses that send transactional emails alongside marketing.

Key Features

  • Email automation workflows (welcome, cart recovery, re-engagement)
  • Transactional email API (great for order confirmations, password resets)
  • Built-in SMS marketing and WhatsApp campaigns
  • Customer relationship management (CRM) included
  • Segmentation based on behavior, demographics, and engagement

Pricing

  • Free: $0/month (300 emails/day, unlimited contacts, basic automation)
  • Starter: $25/month (20,000 emails/month, no daily limit, remove Brevo branding)
  • Business: $65/month (unlimited contacts, advanced automation, A/B testing, phone support)

Pros

  • Very affordable—pricing is based on emails sent, not contacts
  • Excellent transactional email capabilities
  • Multi-channel (email + SMS + WhatsApp) in one platform

Cons

  • Automation builder is less intuitive
