The Ultimate Guide to Choosing the Best Membership Plugin for WordPress in 2025

Are you ready to turn your expertise into recurring revenue? Whether you're launching a premium course, a private community, or a content subscription site, choosing the best membership plugin is the single most important decision you'll make. With dozens of options on the market, each promising to be the perfect solution, it’s easy to feel overwhelmed.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll break down the top membership plugins, compare their features and pricing, and help you pick the right tool for your specific needs. By the end, you’ll have a clear roadmap to launch your membership site with confidence.

What Makes a Great Membership Plugin?

Before we dive into the reviews, let’s define what you should look for in the best membership plugin for your site:

  • Ease of use: Can you set it up without a developer?
  • Payment integration: Does it support Stripe, PayPal, or other gateways?
  • Content restriction: Can you protect pages, posts, or categories?
  • Drip content: Can you schedule content release over time?
  • Pricing flexibility: Does it support one-time, recurring, or tiered memberships?
  • Community features: Does it include forums or integration with tools like BuddyPress?
  • Customer support: Is there reliable help when you need it?

With these criteria in mind, let’s explore the top contenders.

1. MemberPress – The All-in-One Powerhouse

MemberPress consistently ranks as the best membership plugin for serious site owners. It’s a premium solution that handles everything from payment collection to content dripping and access control.

Key Features

  • Unlimited memberships and subscription levels
  • Drip content and expiration rules
  • Built-in coupon and discount system
  • Integration with popular LMS plugins (LearnDash, LifterLMS)
  • Advanced access rules (by tag, category, or individual post)
  • Automatic email reminders for expiring memberships

Pricing

  • Basic Plan: $179/year (1 site)
  • Plus Plan: $239/year (2 sites)
  • Pro Plan: $299/year (5 sites)
  • All plans include a 14-day money-back guarantee.

Pros

  • Extremely robust and feature-rich
  • Excellent documentation and support
  • Seamless integration with email marketing tools (Mailchimp, ConvertKit)
  • Developer-friendly with hooks and filters

Cons

  • No free version (but worth the investment)
  • Can be overkill for very simple sites
  • Learning curve for advanced features

Best for: Course creators, membership sites with multiple tiers, and businesses needing a reliable, scalable solution.

2. Restrict Content Pro – Lightweight and Fast

If you prefer a lean, no-frills approach, Restrict Content Pro (RCP) is a fantastic choice. It’s built by the same team behind the popular Easy Digital Downloads plugin, so you know it’s optimized for performance.

Key Features

  • Simple content restriction with shortcodes and template tags
  • Multiple subscription levels with custom pricing
  • Drip content and membership expiration
  • Built-in payment gateway support (Stripe, PayPal, and more)
  • Integration with Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign, and Zapier
  • Developer-friendly with extensive hooks

Pricing

  • Personal: $99/year (1 site)
  • Plus: $199/year (3 sites)
  • Professional: $299/year (unlimited sites)
  • All plans include a 30-day money-back guarantee.

Pros

  • Lightweight and fast – won’t slow down your site
  • Clean, intuitive interface
  • Excellent customer support
  • Great for developers who want control

Cons

  • Fewer built-in features compared to MemberPress
  • No built-in LMS or community tools
  • Requires add-ons for advanced functionality

Best for: Developers, small membership sites, and users who value speed and simplicity.

3. Paid Memberships Pro – The Free Powerhouse

Paid Memberships Pro (PMPro) is unique because it offers a fully functional free version that can handle many membership scenarios. The premium add-ons extend its capabilities even further.

Key Features

  • Unlimited membership levels and pricing options
  • Content restriction by page, post, or category
  • Drip content and membership expiration
  • Built-in payment gateways (Stripe, PayPal, Authorize.net)
  • User-friendly member management dashboard
  • Active community and extensive documentation

Pricing

  • Free: Core plugin (limited add-ons)
  • Standard: $247/year (1 site, includes all add-ons)
  • Plus: $397/year (5 sites)
  • Unlimited: $597/year (unlimited sites)

Pros

  • Free version is surprisingly powerful
  • Active development and regular updates
  • Large library of add-ons (email marketing, discounts, etc.)
  • Great for testing before committing to a paid plan

Cons

  • Free version lacks some advanced features
  • Interface can feel cluttered
  • Support for free users is limited (forum only)

Best for: Budget-conscious site owners, beginners, and those who want to start free and scale later.

4. WishList Member – Simplicity Meets Power

WishList Member has been around for years and remains a solid contender. It’s known for its straightforward setup and powerful automation features.

Key Features

  • Drag-and-drop content protection
  • Automated membership levels based on user actions
  • Drip content and sequential access
  • Integration with major payment gateways and email services
  • Built-in affiliate management
  • One-click migration from other membership plugins

Pricing

  • Basic: $149/year (1 site)
  • Plus: $249/year (3 sites)
  • Pro: $399/year (unlimited sites)
  • 30-day money-back guarantee.

Pros

  • Very easy to set up and use
  • Automation features save time
  • Good integration with learning management systems
  • Migration tool makes switching easy

Cons

  • Fewer advanced customization options
  • Support can be slow at times
  • Some users report compatibility issues with certain themes
