Hotjar vs FullStory: The Ultimate Comparison for User Experience Analytics (2024)

If you're serious about understanding how users interact with your website, you've likely come across two of the most popular tools in the behavioral analytics space: Hotjar and FullStory. Both promise to reveal the "why" behind user behavior through session recordings, heatmaps, and feedback tools—but they take fundamentally different approaches. In this comprehensive comparison, we’ll break down every key difference, helping you decide which platform best fits your team’s needs, budget, and technical maturity.

Whether you're a product manager, UX researcher, or growth marketer, this guide will give you the actionable insights you need to make an informed choice. Let’s dive in.

What Are Hotjar and FullStory?

Hotjar is an all-in-one user experience (UX) analytics platform that provides session recordings, heatmaps, surveys, and feedback widgets. It’s designed for teams that want a quick, visual understanding of user behavior without heavy technical setup. Hotjar is widely used by startups, small-to-medium businesses, and marketing teams.

FullStory, on the other hand, is a more advanced digital experience intelligence platform. It focuses on automatic session capture, powerful search capabilities, and granular event-level analytics. FullStory is built for product and engineering teams that need deep, queryable data to diagnose bugs, analyze user flows, and optimize conversion funnels.

Core Features: Side-by-Side Comparison

1. Session Recordings

Both tools record user sessions, but the quality and depth differ significantly.

  • Hotjar: Sessions are easy to set up and view. You can filter by page, device, or user attribute. Hotjar also offers "rage clicks" and "dead clicks" detection. However, recordings are sampled—you don’t get every session unless you’re on a higher-tier plan.
  • FullStory: FullStory captures 100% of sessions automatically (on all paid plans). Every click, scroll, and page interaction is recorded and indexed. This means you can search for any user action—like "clicked checkout button"—and instantly find every session where that happened. FullStory’s session playback is also pixel-perfect and includes console logs for debugging.

Verdict: If you need complete, searchable session data for debugging and deep analysis, FullStory wins. For quick visual reviews of sampled sessions, Hotjar is sufficient.

2. Heatmaps

Heatmaps are a core feature for both tools, but they serve different use cases.

  • Hotjar: Offers click, move, and scroll heatmaps. You can generate them for any page quickly. The interface is intuitive, and you can filter by device type or traffic source. Hotjar also provides "insights" that highlight areas of high engagement or frustration.
  • FullStory: Heatmaps are available but are more data-centric. FullStory’s heatmaps are generated from your session data and can be segmented by any user attribute or behavior. However, the setup is less visual and more query-based. FullStory also offers "zone-based" heatmaps that track specific elements even if the page layout changes.

Verdict: For marketers and designers who want quick, visual heatmaps, Hotjar is better. For product teams that need element-level tracking across dynamic pages, FullStory has the edge.

3. Search and Filtering

This is where the two tools diverge most dramatically.

  • Hotjar: Basic filtering based on URL, device, browser, and custom attributes. You can also filter by "rage clicks" or "U-turns." However, you cannot search for specific user actions like "clicked the signup button" or "filled out the email field."
  • FullStory: FullStory’s search is its superpower. You can use natural language queries like "Find sessions where users clicked 'Add to Cart' but didn't complete checkout." It also supports regex and boolean operators. This makes it incredibly powerful for identifying specific user behaviors, bugs, and conversion blockers.

Verdict: FullStory is unmatched for searchability. If you need to find specific user actions across thousands of sessions, FullStory is the only choice.

4. Feedback Tools

Hotjar shines here with its built-in feedback widgets, surveys, and polls. You can easily add on-page feedback forms, NPS surveys, and exit-intent surveys. These are great for collecting qualitative insights directly from users.

FullStory does not offer native feedback tools. You would need to integrate a third-party tool like SurveyMonkey or Qualtrics. However, FullStory can be used to analyze how users interact with those feedback forms once they’re implemented.

Verdict: Hotjar is the clear winner for collecting direct user feedback without extra tools.

5. Pricing

Pricing is a major consideration, especially for smaller teams.

  • Hotjar: Offers a generous free plan (up to 35 daily sessions, 1,000 pageviews/month, and 3 heatmaps). Paid plans start at $39/month (Plus) and go up to $99/month (Business). The free plan is excellent for small websites or testing the waters.
  • FullStory: No free plan. Pricing starts at around $200/month (Business plan) and scales with session volume. Enterprise plans can cost thousands per month. FullStory’s pricing is significantly higher, but you get 100% session capture and advanced search.

Verdict: Hotjar is far more affordable and accessible. FullStory is a premium investment for serious product analytics.

Which Tool Should You Choose? Practical Tips

Choose Hotjar if:

  • You have a limited budget or want to start with a free plan.
  • You need quick, visual heatmaps and session recordings without technical setup.
  • You want to collect user feedback through surveys and polls.
  • Your team is small (marketing, UX, or customer success) and doesn’t need advanced search.
  • You’re running a content site, blog, or e-commerce store with moderate traffic.

Choose FullStory if:

  • You need to capture 100% of sessions for comprehensive analysis.
  • Your team needs to search for specific user behaviors (e.g., "users who clicked error messages").
  • You are debugging technical issues or tracking complex user flows.
  • You have a larger budget and a dedicated product or engineering team.
  • You want to integrate session data with tools like Segment, Mixpanel, or Amplitude.

Actionable Advice for Making the Most of Your Chosen Tool

If you go with Hotjar:

  • Use the free plan to test on a high-traffic page first. Focus on heatmaps to identify where users click vs. where you expect them to click.
  • Set up "rage click" alerts to quickly spot frustrating UI elements.
  • Combine session recordings with on-page surveys to get both behavioral and qualitative data.
  • Regularly export heatmap data to share with your design team for A/B testing ideas.

If you go with FullStory:

  • Take advantage of FullStory’s "Search Everywhere" feature. Start by searching for common friction points like "clicked 'Back' button" or "scrolled to bottom but didn't click CTA."
  • Use "Funnels" to analyze drop-off points in your conversion flow. FullStory automatically detects where users leave.
  • Set up "Watchlists" for specific elements (e.g., a broken button or a new feature) to get notified when users interact with them.
  • Integrate FullStory with your bug tracker (Jira, Asana) to automatically create tickets when you spot an issue in a session.

Conclusion: Hotjar vs FullStory – Which One Is Right for You?

Both Hotjar and FullStory are excellent tools, but they serve different purposes. Hotjar is a user-friendly, affordable entry point for understanding user behavior through heatmaps, recordings, and feedback. FullStory is a powerful, enterprise-grade platform for deep behavioral analytics, debugging, and cross-team collaboration.
