Hotjar vs FullStory: The Ultimate Comparison for User Experience Analytics

When it comes to understanding how users interact with your website or app, two names dominate the conversation: Hotjar and FullStory. Both tools offer session recordings, heatmaps, and user feedback features, but they serve different needs and budgets. If you're evaluating which platform is right for your team, this comprehensive comparison will help you decide.

In this guide, we’ll break down the key differences between Hotjar and FullStory, focusing on features, pricing, usability, and real-world use cases. By the end, you’ll know exactly which tool aligns with your business goals—and why FullStory might be the better choice for data-driven teams.

Overview: Hotjar vs FullStory

Hotjar and FullStory are both powerful user experience (UX) analytics tools, but they approach the problem from different angles. Hotjar is often praised for its simplicity and affordability, making it a favorite among small businesses and marketers. FullStory, on the other hand, is built for scale and depth, offering advanced features like automatic event tracking and session replay with pixel-perfect accuracy.

Let’s dive into the specifics.

Feature Comparison

1. Session Recordings

Both tools record user sessions, but the quality and searchability differ.

  • Hotjar: Offers session recordings with basic filters (e.g., by URL, device, or duration). You can watch individual sessions, but searching for specific user behaviors (like clicks or scrolls) requires manual tagging.
  • FullStory: Provides high-fidelity session replays that capture every interaction, including rage clicks, dead clicks, and form field errors. Its powerful search engine lets you find sessions based on custom events, user attributes, or even console logs—without any manual setup.

Winner: FullStory for depth and searchability.

2. Heatmaps

Heatmaps visualize where users click, move, and scroll on your pages.

  • Hotjar: Offers click, move, and scroll heatmaps with easy-to-understand visualizations. You can overlay multiple pages and compare performance. Good for quick insights.
  • FullStory: Includes heatmaps but with an added layer of intelligence—you can segment heatmaps by user behavior or events. For example, see where users who abandoned a cart clicked vs. those who completed a purchase.

Winner: Tie for basic use; FullStory for advanced segmentation.

3. Event Tracking

This is where FullStory truly shines.

  • Hotjar: Relies on manual event tracking. You must set up events via the Hotjar dashboard or use its API. This can be time-consuming for large sites.
  • FullStory: Offers automatic event capture. It logs every click, pageview, scroll, and form interaction out of the box. You can also define custom events using a visual editor or CSS selectors—no coding required.

Winner: FullStory for automation and ease of use.

4. User Feedback

Both tools allow you to collect feedback, but the methods differ.

  • Hotjar: Offers feedback widgets, surveys, and polls. You can target specific pages or user segments. Great for quick qualitative insights.
  • FullStory: Focuses more on behavioral analytics. While it doesn’t have built-in surveys, you can integrate with third-party tools like Qualtrics or Typeform. FullStory’s strength is analyzing why users behave a certain way, not just what they say.

Winner: Hotjar for direct feedback; FullStory for behavioral context.

5. Integrations

Both tools integrate with popular platforms, but FullStory has a broader ecosystem.

  • Hotjar: Integrates with Google Analytics, WordPress, Shopify, and more. Limited API access on lower plans.
  • FullStory: Offers deep integrations with Google Analytics, Segment, Optimizely, and major CRM and support tools. Its API is robust, allowing you to export data and trigger actions based on user behavior.

Winner: FullStory for extensibility.

Pricing Comparison

Pricing is often the deciding factor for small teams.

  • Hotjar: Starts free for up to 35 daily sessions (with limited features). Paid plans begin at $39/month for 100 daily sessions and scale up. Enterprise plans are custom-priced.
  • FullStory: Offers a free tier for up to 1,000 sessions per month, but with limited functionality. Paid plans start at around $500/month for 10,000 sessions. Enterprise plans with advanced features are significantly more expensive.

Winner: Hotjar for budget-conscious teams; FullStory for serious analytics.

Ease of Use

Both tools are user-friendly, but they cater to different skill levels.

  • Hotjar: Extremely easy to set up—just add a tracking snippet and start recording. The dashboard is intuitive, making it ideal for non-technical users like marketers or product managers.
  • FullStory: Has a steeper learning curve due to its advanced features. However, once configured, it offers unparalleled insights. The session replay interface is polished, and the search functionality is powerful.

Winner: Hotjar for simplicity; FullStory for power users.

Practical Tips: Choosing the Right Tool

Here’s actionable advice based on your needs:

  • Choose Hotjar if: You’re a startup or small business with a limited budget. You need quick heatmaps or session recordings without complex setup. You want to collect user feedback via surveys.
  • Choose FullStory if: You’re a mid-to-large company with a dedicated UX or product team. You need automatic event tracking and deep session search. You’re willing to invest in a tool that scales with your growth.

For example, an e-commerce site with high traffic might benefit from FullStory’s ability to find sessions where users encountered JavaScript errors or rage-clicked on a non-functional button. Meanwhile, a small blog might be perfectly served by Hotjar’s free plan.

Real-World Use Cases

Case 1: SaaS Product Optimization

A SaaS company used FullStory to identify why users were dropping off during onboarding. By searching for sessions with “dead clicks” on the sign-up button, they discovered a CSS conflict that prevented the button from working on mobile devices. Fixing this increased conversions by 15%.

Case 2: Marketing Campaign Analysis

A marketing team used Hotjar to test a new landing page design. They created heatmaps and watched session recordings to see where users scrolled. Based on the data, they moved the call-to-action above the fold, resulting in a 20% lift in click-throughs.

Conclusion: Which One Should You Choose?

Both Hotjar and FullStory are excellent tools, but they serve different purposes. Hotjar is a cost-effective, easy-to-use solution for small teams that need basic user insights. FullStory is a premium, data-rich platform designed for teams that need to analyze user behavior at scale.

If you’re serious about understanding every nuance of user behavior—and have the budget to match—FullStory is the clear winner. Its automatic event tracking, powerful search, and high-fidelity session replays give you the deepest insights into what users are doing and why.

Ready to take your UX analytics to the next level? Start a free trial of FullStory today and see how it can transform your product decisions. For smaller teams, Hotjar’s free plan is a great way to dip your toes into user analytics. Either way, the most important step is to start observing your users—and let the data guide your growth.
