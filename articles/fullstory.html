Hotjar vs FullStory: Which User Experience Tool Wins in 2024?

Understanding how users interact with your website is no longer a luxury—it’s a necessity. Two giants dominate the user experience (UX) analytics space: Hotjar and FullStory. Both promise to reveal what visitors do, where they click, and why they leave. But which one is right for your business?

In this detailed comparison, we’ll break down features, pricing, ease of use, and ideal use cases. By the end, you’ll know exactly which tool aligns with your goals—and how to get the most out of FullStory if it’s the right fit.

What Are Hotjar and FullStory?

Both Hotjar and FullStory are session recording and heatmapping tools. They help you visualize user behavior, identify friction points, and optimize conversion funnels. However, their approaches differ significantly.

Hotjar Overview

Hotjar is a popular, user-friendly platform designed for small to medium-sized businesses. It offers session recordings, heatmaps, feedback polls, and surveys. Its strength lies in simplicity and affordability, making it a go-to for startups and marketing teams.

FullStory Overview

FullStory is a premium product analytics platform built for data-driven teams. It captures every click, scroll, and rage click with high fidelity. FullStory excels at advanced search, automatic event tracking, and developer-friendly features. It’s ideal for product managers, UX researchers, and engineering teams who need deep, quantitative insights.

Feature Comparison: Hotjar vs FullStory

Let’s dive into the core features side by side. We’ll focus on what matters most: session replay, heatmaps, event tracking, and integrations.

1. Session Recordings

  • Hotjar: Records user sessions with basic filters. You can watch replays of individual users, but searching for specific behaviors (e.g., “users who clicked the pricing page but didn’t convert”) requires manual effort. Recordings are stored for 30–365 days depending on your plan.
  • FullStory: Offers pixel-perfect replay with advanced search. You can query by element, URL, user attribute, or custom event. For example: “Show me sessions where users clicked the ‘Add to Cart’ button but never visited the checkout page.” FullStory stores data indefinitely on higher-tier plans.

2. Heatmaps

  • Hotjar: Provides click, move, and scroll heatmaps. You can segment by device type or traffic source. Heatmaps are easy to create and interpret, even for non-technical users.
  • FullStory: Heatmaps are dynamic and tied directly to session data. They update in real-time as new data streams in. FullStory also offers “rage click” heatmaps, which highlight areas where users repeatedly click in frustration—a powerful UX insight.

3. Event Tracking

  • Hotjar: Events are manual. You must set up custom events or use Hotjar’s “triggers” to capture specific interactions. This can be time-consuming for large sites.
  • FullStory: Automatically tracks all clicks, page visits, scrolls, and form interactions. No setup required. FullStory’s “Autocapture” engine logs every event, making it a dream for teams that hate manual tagging.

4. Feedback and Surveys

  • Hotjar: Includes built-in feedback widgets, polls, and surveys. You can target specific pages or user segments. This is a standout feature for gathering qualitative insights directly from users.
  • FullStory: Does not offer native survey tools. However, you can integrate with third-party survey platforms like Qualtrics or Typeform.

5. Integrations

  • Hotjar: Integrates with Google Analytics, Shopify, WordPress, and basic CRMs. It’s lightweight but limited for enterprise stacks.
  • FullStory: Offers deep integrations with tools like Segment, Amplitude, Mixpanel, and Slack. FullStory’s API is robust, allowing you to push session data into your data warehouse or BI tools.

Pricing: Hotjar vs FullStory

Pricing is where the two tools diverge the most.

Hotjar Pricing

  • Free Plan: 35 daily sessions, 3 heatmaps, 1 survey. Great for testing.
  • Plus Plan ($39/month): 100 daily sessions, 10 heatmaps, unlimited surveys.
  • Business Plan ($99/month): 500 daily sessions, 50 heatmaps, advanced filters.
  • Scale Plan ($213/month): 1,000 daily sessions, 100 heatmaps, custom branding.

FullStory Pricing

  • Free Plan: 1,000 sessions per month, limited search, 1 user seat.
  • Business Plan (Custom Pricing): Starts around $200–$500 per month for 10,000 sessions. Includes advanced search, unlimited heatmaps, and API access.
  • Enterprise Plan (Custom Pricing): Unlimited sessions, dedicated support, and data retention controls. Typically $1,000+/month.

Key Takeaway: Hotjar is budget-friendly for small sites. FullStory is an investment that pays off for high-traffic, data-intensive teams.

Ease of Use

Hotjar

Hotjar is intuitive. You can install it via a simple JavaScript snippet or a plugin (WordPress, Shopify). The dashboard is clean, with clear labels. Non-technical team members can start analyzing within minutes.

FullStory

FullStory has a steeper learning curve. Its search syntax (called “OmniSearch”) is powerful but requires practice. The interface is dense with data—ideal for analysts but overwhelming for casual users. However, once mastered, FullStory’s speed and precision are unmatched.

Ideal Use Cases

When to Choose Hotjar

  • You run a small e-commerce store or content site.
  • You need quick, visual insights without a technical setup.
  • You want to collect user feedback via surveys and polls.
  • Your budget is under $200 per month.

When to Choose FullStory

  • You have a high-traffic SaaS or enterprise web application.
  • Your team includes product managers, engineers, and data analysts.
  • You need to debug user issues (e.g., “Why is the checkout button not working for Safari users?”).
  • You want to integrate session data into your data warehouse for custom analytics.

Practical Tips for Getting the Most Out of FullStory

If you decide to go with FullStory—and many teams do for its depth—here are actionable tips to maximize your investment:

1. Master OmniSearch

FullStory’s search is its superpower. Learn the syntax early. For example:

  • clicked button[data-testid="add-to-cart"] – Find all sessions where users clicked a specific button.
  • rage click count > 3 – Identify frustrated users.
  • page url contains "/checkout" and not page url contains "/confirmation" – Find drop-offs in the checkout flow.

2. Set Up Custom Dashboards

FullStory allows you to create dashboards for specific metrics like “Conversion Funnel,” “Error Rates,” or “Feature Adoption.” Use these to monitor key KPIs daily.

3. Use Session Replay for Debugging

When a user reports a bug, don’t guess. Search for their session by email or user ID. Watch the replay to see exactly what happened. This is invaluable for support and engineering teams.
