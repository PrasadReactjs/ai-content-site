Hotjar vs FullStory: The Ultimate Comparison for User Behavior Analytics

Understanding how users interact with your website is no longer optional—it’s essential for conversion rate optimization, UX improvements, and product growth. Two of the most popular tools in this space are Hotjar and FullStory. While both offer session recordings, heatmaps, and analytics, they cater to different audiences and use cases. In this comprehensive comparison, we’ll dive deep into features, pricing, ease of use, and integrations to help you decide which platform is right for your business. By the end, you’ll have a clear understanding of why FullStory might be the superior choice for data-driven teams—or why Hotjar’s simplicity could be your perfect fit.

1. What Are Hotjar and FullStory?

Hotjar is an all-in-one behavior analytics tool designed for marketers, product managers, and UX designers. It offers session recordings, heatmaps, feedback polls, and surveys. Hotjar is known for its ease of use and affordable pricing, making it a go-to for startups and small businesses.

FullStory, on the other hand, is an enterprise-grade digital experience analytics platform. It focuses on automatic session capture, advanced search, and data-driven insights. FullStory prides itself on its “Google-like” search for user interactions, making it ideal for product teams at scale who need granular, retroactive analysis.

2. Feature Comparison: Hotjar vs FullStory

Session Recordings

Both tools record user sessions, but their approaches differ significantly. Hotjar offers session recordings with filters by page, device, and user behavior (e.g., rage clicks, errors). However, recordings are sampled—you don’t get every session unless you’re on a higher plan. FullStory, by contrast, records every single session automatically, with no sampling. This is a game-changer for teams that need complete data coverage. FullStory also provides “session replay” with pixel-perfect rendering, even on SPA (Single Page Applications).

Heatmaps

Hotjar excels here with its intuitive heatmap creation. You can generate click, move, and scroll heatmaps for any page. FullStory offers heatmaps too, but they’re less visual and more integrated into its analytics dashboard. For quick, visual insights, Hotjar wins. For correlation with other metrics (e.g., conversion funnels), FullStory is stronger.

Search and Filtering

This is where FullStory truly shines. Its “OmniSearch” lets you query user behavior retrospectively—for example, “find all sessions where a user clicked ‘Add to Cart’ but never checked out.” FullStory indexes every click, scroll, and text input, making it searchable like a database. Hotjar’s filtering is more basic: you can filter by page, device, or date, but you can’t pinpoint specific interactions without manual tagging.

Feedback Tools

Hotjar offers built-in feedback widgets, polls, and surveys—perfect for collecting qualitative data. FullStory lacks native feedback tools; it’s purely observational. If you need user feedback integrated with behavior data, Hotjar is better. But for pure behavioral analytics, FullStory’s depth is unmatched.

3. Pricing: Which One Fits Your Budget?

Pricing is a critical factor. Hotjar has a generous free plan (up to 35 daily sessions, 1,000 pageviews) and paid plans starting at $39/month (Plus) and $99/month (Business). FullStory, however, is more expensive. Its free plan (Business) offers 1,000 sessions/month, but paid plans start at $199/month (Pro) and scale up significantly for enterprise. For small teams with tight budgets, Hotjar is the clear winner. For larger teams that need volume and advanced features, FullStory’s cost is justified by its data completeness.

4. Ease of Use and Implementation

Hotjar is incredibly easy to set up—just add a JavaScript snippet, and you’re recording within minutes. The interface is clean and intuitive, even for non-technical users. FullStory also uses a snippet, but its setup requires more configuration (e.g., privacy settings, event tagging). FullStory’s dashboard has a steeper learning curve, but once mastered, it offers unparalleled control. If you value speed and simplicity, choose Hotjar. If you’re willing to invest time in setup for deeper insights, FullStory is worth the effort.

5. Data Privacy and Compliance

Both tools prioritize privacy. Hotjar automatically masks sensitive data (e.g., passwords, credit cards) and offers GDPR compliance tools. FullStory goes a step further with “Privacy by Design” features, including automatic PII redaction, consent management, and data retention controls. FullStory also provides SOC 2 Type II certification, which is critical for enterprise clients. For compliance-heavy industries (finance, healthcare), FullStory is the safer bet.

6. Integrations and Ecosystem

Hotjar integrates with popular tools like Google Analytics, WordPress, Shopify, and Slack. FullStory offers deeper integrations with product analytics tools (Amplitude, Mixpanel), CRMs (Salesforce), and development tools (Jira, GitHub). FullStory’s API is more robust, allowing custom data exports and webhooks. If you’re building a data stack, FullStory fits seamlessly. For simple marketing workflows, Hotjar is sufficient.

7. Real-World Use Cases

When to Use Hotjar

  • Small businesses looking for quick UX insights without a big budget.
  • Marketers who need heatmaps and feedback polls to optimize landing pages.
  • UX designers who want to watch a few sessions to identify obvious friction points.

When to Use FullStory

  • Product teams at scale that need to analyze thousands of sessions retroactively.
  • Data analysts who want to correlate user behavior with conversion funnels and A/B tests.
  • Enterprise companies requiring full session coverage, advanced search, and strict compliance.

8. Pros and Cons at a Glance

Hotjar Pros

  • Free plan available
  • Easy to set up and use
  • Built-in feedback and survey tools
  • Visual heatmaps

Hotjar Cons

  • Session sampling on lower plans
  • Limited search and filtering
  • No retroactive analysis

FullStory Pros

  • 100% session capture
  • Powerful OmniSearch
  • Pixel-perfect session replay
  • Enterprise-grade privacy and compliance

FullStory Cons

  • Higher cost
  • Steeper learning curve
  • No native feedback tools

9. Practical Tips for Choosing the Right Tool

  1. Evaluate your data needs: If you need to analyze every user interaction, choose FullStory. If sampling is acceptable, Hotjar works.
  2. Consider your team’s technical skill: Non-technical teams will appreciate Hotjar’s simplicity. Data-savvy teams will leverage FullStory’s advanced features.
  3. Budget for growth: FullStory’s pricing scales with session volume. If you anticipate rapid growth, factor in the cost.
  4. Test both with free trials: Hotjar’s free plan is generous. FullStory’s free plan (1,000 sessions/month) is enough for a trial. Use both on a test page to compare.
  5. Integrate with your stack: Check which tools you already use. If you rely on Google Analytics and hotjar, Hotjar is a natural fit. If you use Amplitude or Jira, FullStory integrates better.

10. Conclusion: Which One Should You Choose?

Both Hotjar and FullStory are powerful tools, but they serve different purposes. Hotjar is ideal for quick, visual insights on a budget—perfect for marketers and small teams. FullStory, however, is built for data-driven product teams who need complete session coverage, retroactive search, and enterprise-grade compliance.
