The Ultimate Guide to Choosing a Platform to Create Online Course: Pricing, Features, Audience Reach, and Monetization

Are you ready to turn your expertise into a profitable digital product? The e-learning industry is booming, and there has never been a better time to create online course content that reaches a global audience. However, with dozens of platforms vying for your attention, choosing the right one can feel overwhelming.

In this comprehensive comparison, we break down the top online course platforms by pricing, features, audience reach, and monetization options. Whether you are a first-time creator or a seasoned educator, this guide will help you make an informed decision—and we’ve included affiliate recommendations to help you get started with confidence.

Why Choosing the Right Platform Matters

Your choice of platform directly impacts your course’s success. The right platform simplifies the process to create online course content, provides tools to engage students, and maximizes your revenue. The wrong one can mean hidden fees, limited reach, or poor user experience. Let’s dive into the top contenders.

Top Online Course Platforms Compared

1. Teachable

Best for: Solo creators and small businesses who want full control over branding and pricing.

Pricing

  • Free Plan: $0 (transaction fees of 5% + $1 per sale)
  • Basic Plan: $39/month (5% transaction fees)
  • Pro Plan: $119/month (0% transaction fees)
  • Business Plan: $299/month (custom features)

Key Features

  • Drag-and-drop course builder
  • Built-in quizzes, certificates, and drip content
  • Custom domain and branding
  • Email marketing integrations (Mailchimp, ConvertKit)
  • Native affiliate program management

Audience Reach

Teachable does not provide a built-in marketplace. You must drive your own traffic via social media, email, or ads. This gives you full ownership of your audience but requires marketing effort.

Monetization Options

  • One-time payments, subscriptions, or payment plans
  • Course bundles and coaching add-ons
  • Affiliate marketing for your students

Verdict: Teachable is an excellent choice if you already have an audience or are willing to invest in marketing. The free plan lets you test the waters before committing.

2. Thinkific

Best for: Creators who want a robust, all-in-one platform with no transaction fees on higher plans.

Pricing

  • Free Plan: $0 (limited to 3 courses, no transaction fees)
  • Basic Plan: $49/month
  • Pro Plan: $99/month
  • Premier Plan: $199/month

Key Features

  • Intuitive course builder with multimedia support
  • Built-in community features (discussion forums)
  • AI-powered course outlines and assessments
  • Membership site capabilities
  • Advanced reporting and analytics

Audience Reach

Like Teachable, Thinkific is a self-hosted platform. However, it offers a free plan with no transaction fees—perfect for testing how to create online course content without upfront costs. You own your student data and can export it anytime.

Monetization Options

  • One-time sales, subscriptions, bundles
  • Payment plans and coupons
  • Affiliate program (Pro and Premier plans)

Verdict: Thinkific stands out for its generous free plan and community features. It’s ideal for building a course-based membership site.

3. Kajabi

Best for: Established creators who want an all-in-one business platform (courses, email, marketing, sales).

Pricing

  • Basic Plan: $149/month (1 website, 3 products, 10,000 contacts)
  • Growth Plan: $199/month (15 products, 25,000 contacts)
  • Pro Plan: $399/month (100 products, 100,000 contacts)

Key Features

  • Fully integrated email marketing and automation
  • Landing page builder with templates
  • Pipeline builder for sales funnels
  • Built-in affiliate program management
  • Video hosting and streaming

Audience Reach

Kajabi is a closed ecosystem—you build your audience using its marketing tools. It does not have a marketplace, but its email and funnel tools help you convert traffic effectively.

Monetization Options

  • One-time purchases, subscriptions, and payment plans
  • Upsells and order bumps
  • Affiliate commissions for your students

Verdict: Kajabi is powerful but expensive. It’s best for creators who already have a list and want to streamline sales and marketing.

4. Udemy

Best for: Beginners who want instant access to a massive student marketplace.

Pricing

  • Free to publish
  • Revenue share: 97% to instructor (when students use your coupon), 63% (organic sales), or 25% (via Udemy ads)

Key Features

  • Built-in marketplace with millions of active students
  • Course creation tools (video, quizzes, coding exercises)
  • Instructor dashboard with analytics
  • Mobile app for students

Audience Reach

Udemy provides massive exposure. You don’t need to market your course—students find you. However, you have no control over pricing (Udemy often runs $10–$20 sales). You also do not own the student email list.

Monetization Options

Limited to course sales. No subscriptions, bundles, or affiliate programs for instructors. You earn per enrollment.

Verdict: Udemy is great for building credibility and passive income, but it is not a long-term business model due to low pricing and lack of ownership.

5. Skillshare

Best for: Creators who produce short, project-based classes (e.g., design, photography, writing).

Pricing

  • Free to apply as a teacher
  • Royalty model: Based on minutes watched by premium members (typically $0.05–$0.10 per minute)

Key Features

  • Course creation tools (video, projects, discussions)
  • Community of creative learners
  • Teacher referral program

Audience Reach

Skillshare has a large, engaged subscriber base. Your class gets promoted to relevant students. However, you earn royalties, not direct sales—and payouts depend on watch time.

Monetization Options

Only through royalty payments. No direct sales, subscriptions, or affiliate options.

Verdict: Skillshare is best for exposure and building a following, but not for high income. Use it as a funnel to your own platform.
