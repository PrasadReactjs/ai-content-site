Best Online Course Platforms Compared: Pricing, Features, and Monetization in 2025

If you've ever thought about sharing your expertise and create online course content, you're not alone. The e-learning industry is booming, and choosing the right platform is the single most important decision you'll make. Whether you're a coach, consultant, artist, or corporate trainer, the platform you select determines how you price, market, and profit from your knowledge.

In this comprehensive comparison, we'll break down the top online course platforms across four critical dimensions: pricing, features, audience reach, and monetization options. By the end, you'll have a clear roadmap to pick the perfect home for your digital course business.

Why Platform Choice Matters When You Create Online Course Content

Not all platforms are created equal. Some are built for beginners who want simplicity; others cater to established creators needing advanced marketing funnels. Your choice impacts your profit margins, student experience, and long-term scalability. Let's dive into the leading contenders.

1. Teachable: Best for All-in-One Course Creation

Pricing

  • Free Plan: $0/month + 5% transaction fee per sale ($1 + 5% for paid plans)
  • Basic Plan: $39/month (billed annually) – 5% transaction fee
  • Pro Plan: $119/month – 0% transaction fee
  • Business Plan: $299/month – 0% transaction fee, custom user roles

Features

  • Drag-and-drop course builder with multimedia support
  • Built-in quizzes, certificates, and drip content scheduling
  • Customizable sales pages and checkout
  • Native email marketing and affiliate management
  • Integrated payment processing (Stripe/PayPal)

Audience Reach

Teachable has a moderate built-in marketplace. Most of your traffic must come from your own marketing efforts (email lists, social media, ads). However, their affiliate program allows other creators to promote your courses.

Monetization Options

  • One-time payments, subscriptions, or payment plans
  • Course bundles and membership tiers
  • Affiliate commissions (you set the rate)
  • Upsells via order bumps

Best for: Solopreneurs and small teams who want a polished, all-in-one solution without coding.

2. Thinkific: Best for Customization and No Transaction Fees

Pricing

  • Free Plan: $0/month – up to 3 courses, unlimited students, 0% transaction fees
  • Basic Plan: $49/month – unlimited courses, 0% fees
  • Pro Plan: $99/month – advanced features like assignments and communities
  • Premier Plan: $199/month – priority support and onboarding

Features

  • Robust course builder with video, text, and downloadable resources
  • Built-in community (discussion forums)
  • Advanced quiz engine with branching logic
  • Custom domain and full branding control
  • Direct integrations with Mailchimp, ConvertKit, Zapier

Audience Reach

Like Teachable, Thinkific relies heavily on your own marketing. They do offer a marketplace (Thinkific App Store) for add-ons but no built-in student discovery. However, their free plan is a powerful growth tool—you can create online course content and test the waters risk-free.

Monetization Options

  • One-time purchases, subscriptions, memberships
  • Payment plans and free trial options
  • Coupon codes and bulk pricing
  • Third-party affiliate tracking via integrations

Best for: Creators who want zero transaction fees and maximum design flexibility from day one.

3. Kajabi: Best for All-in-One Marketing and Sales

Pricing

  • Basic Plan: $149/month – 3 products, 1,000 active members
  • Growth Plan: $199/month – 15 products, 10,000 active members
  • Pro Plan: $399/month – 100 products, 20,000 active members
  • No transaction fees on any plan

Features

  • Full website builder (pages, blogs, landing pages)
  • Built-in email marketing with automations
  • Pipeline builder for sales funnels
  • Native membership site and community
  • Video hosting and course delivery

Audience Reach

Kajabi is a closed ecosystem—you bring your own audience. However, its marketing tools (pipelines, email sequences, landing pages) are the most advanced in this comparison, helping you convert traffic into sales more effectively.

Monetization Options

  • One-time offers, subscriptions, and free trials
  • Upsells, downsells, and order bumps
  • Affiliate program management (native)
  • Membership tiers and bundle deals

Best for: Established creators who want a full marketing suite and are willing to invest for higher conversions.

4. Udemy: Best for Maximum Audience Reach

Pricing

  • Free to publish – Udemy takes a revenue share
  • Instructor revenue: 97% for organic sales (via your coupon), 25% for marketplace sales (when students find you through Udemy)
  • No monthly subscription fees

Features

  • Basic course builder (video, text, quizzes)
  • Built-in marketplace with millions of active learners
  • Q&A section and student discussion boards
  • Mobile app for students
  • Limited customization (Udemy branding on course pages)

Audience Reach

This is Udemy's superpower. With over 60 million students worldwide, your course can be discovered by people searching for your topic. However, competition is fierce, and pricing is often driven down by Udemy's frequent sales.

Monetization Options

  • Fixed price (you set) but Udemy can run promotions at discounts
  • No subscriptions or memberships
  • No affiliate program (but you can use your own coupon links)
  • Revenue share structure favors Udemy for marketplace sales

Best for: Beginners who want to create online course content to build authority and get student feedback, with less focus on profit margins.

5. Skillshare: Best for Creative and Lifestyle Topics

Pricing

  • Free to join – revenue is based on royalty pool
  • Instructors earn based on minutes watched and premium member referrals
  • Typical earnings: $0.05–$0.20 per minute watched

Features

  • Simple project-based course structure
  • Student community and class projects
  • No direct sales—students pay a subscription to Skillshare
  • Limited analytics and no email marketing

Audience Reach

Skillshare has a loyal subscriber base (over 12 million members). If your topic is creative (photography, design, writing, crafts), this is a great discovery platform.

Monetization Options

  • Royalty-based earnings (not direct sales)
  • No control over pricing or promotions
  • No upsells or memberships
  • Best for passive income and exposure

Best for: Creative professionals building a brand and portfolio, not maximizing per-course revenue.
