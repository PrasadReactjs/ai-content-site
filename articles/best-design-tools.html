The Ultimate Guide to the Best Design Tools in 2024: Features, Pricing & Honest Reviews

Whether you're a seasoned graphic designer, a UI/UX professional, or a complete beginner launching your first side hustle, having the best design tools in your arsenal can make or break your workflow. The right software doesn't just help you create—it helps you create faster, smarter, and with more polish. But with hundreds of options flooding the market, finding the perfect fit for your budget and skill set can feel overwhelming.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ve tested and compared the top contenders to help you choose the best design tools for your specific needs. We’ll break down pricing, key features, and the honest pros and cons of each platform. Let’s dive in.

1. Figma: The Reigning Champion of Collaborative Design

Figma has rapidly become the industry standard for UI/UX design, and for good reason. It’s a browser-based vector design tool that prioritizes real-time collaboration, making it indispensable for remote teams.

Key Features

  • Real-time collaboration: Multiple team members can work on the same file simultaneously.
  • Auto Layout: Creates responsive, adaptive designs that adjust automatically.
  • Developer handoff: Inspect mode lets developers copy CSS, iOS, and Android code directly.
  • Plugins & Community: Thousands of free plugins and UI kits.

Pricing

  • Free Plan: Unlimited files, up to 3 Figma and 3 FigJam files. Great for individuals.
  • Professional Plan: $12 per editor/month (billed annually). Unlimited projects, version history, and shared fonts.
  • Organization Plan: $45 per editor/month. Advanced admin controls, single sign-on (SSO), and dedicated support.

Pros & Cons

  • Pros: Cross-platform (works on Windows, Mac, Linux, Chrome OS); excellent for team workflows; lightweight and fast.
  • Cons: Requires a stable internet connection (though offline mode is improving); can be overwhelming for absolute beginners.

Recommendation: Figma is the best design tool for UI/UX teams and anyone who needs seamless collaboration. If you work solo, the free tier is more than enough to get started.

2. Adobe Photoshop: The Unmatched Powerhouse for Raster Graphics

Photoshop needs no introduction. It remains the gold standard for photo editing, digital painting, and complex raster graphics. Despite being over 30 years old, Adobe continues to innovate with AI-driven features.

Key Features

  • Generative Fill (AI): Use text prompts to add, remove, or extend images with stunning accuracy.
  • Layers & Masks: Industry-leading non-destructive editing capabilities.
  • Brushes & Painting: Hundreds of customizable brushes with pressure sensitivity support.
  • Integration: Deep integration with other Adobe Creative Cloud apps like Illustrator and After Effects.

Pricing

  • Photography Plan: $9.99/month (includes Photoshop + Lightroom + 20GB cloud storage). Best value for photographers.
  • Single App Plan: $22.99/month (Photoshop only).
  • Creative Cloud All Apps: $54.99/month (includes 20+ apps).

Pros & Cons

  • Pros: Unmatched depth of features; vast community and tutorials; professional-grade output.
  • Cons: Subscription-only model (no lifetime purchase); steep learning curve; resource-heavy on older computers.

Recommendation: Choose Photoshop if you do heavy photo manipulation, digital painting, or need pixel-perfect control. For vector work or UI design, look elsewhere—it’s not the best design tool for those tasks.

3. Canva: The Beginner-Friendly, All-in-One Design Platform

Canva democratized design. It’s a web-based tool that uses drag-and-drop simplicity and thousands of templates to help anyone create professional-looking graphics in minutes. It’s perfect for social media posts, presentations, flyers, and even basic video editing.

Key Features

  • Massive template library: Over 250,000 free templates and 100+ design types.
  • Magic Studio (AI): AI-powered tools like Magic Eraser, Magic Write (copywriting), and text-to-image.
  • Brand Kit: Save your brand colors, fonts, and logos for consistent output.
  • Collaboration: Real-time editing and commenting (similar to Google Docs).

Pricing

  • Free Plan: 250,000+ free templates, 5GB cloud storage, basic resizing.
  • Canva Pro: $12.99/month (billed annually) or $14.99 month-to-month. Unlocks premium templates, 100GB storage, background remover, and brand kits.
  • Canva for Teams: $14.99/month per person (billed annually). Adds team folders, templates, and workflow approvals.

Pros & Cons

  • Pros: Extremely easy to use; no design experience needed; huge asset library; affordable.
  • Cons: Limited customization compared to professional tools; output can look generic if you rely too heavily on templates; no advanced vector or photo editing.

Recommendation: Canva is the best design tool for non-designers, small business owners, and social media managers who need speed and simplicity. Upgrade to Pro if you need background removal or brand consistency.

4. Sketch: The Veteran Vector Tool for Mac Users

Sketch was once the darling of UI design before Figma dethroned it. Still, it remains a powerful, lightweight vector tool exclusively for macOS. It’s particularly loved for its robust plugin ecosystem and symbol-based design system.

Key Features

  • Symbols & Overrides: Create reusable components that update globally across your design.
  • Smart Layout: Similar to Figma’s Auto Layout, for responsive design.
  • Developer Handoff: Built-in inspection and export tools.
  • Plugins: Thousands of plugins via Sketch Runner and the community.

Pricing

  • Sketch Standard: $10 per editor/month (billed annually). Includes web app for sharing and feedback.
  • Sketch Business: $20 per editor/month. Adds SSO, custom roles, and advanced analytics.
  • One-time purchase (legacy): No longer available for new users.

Pros & Cons

  • Pros: Fast and native on Mac; excellent for design systems; strong plugin community.
  • Cons: macOS only; losing ground to Figma in collaboration; limited prototyping (no advanced animations).

Recommendation: If you’re a solo Mac user who values speed and a native feel, Sketch is still a solid choice. However, for most teams, Figma’s cross-platform collaboration makes it the better best design tool overall.
