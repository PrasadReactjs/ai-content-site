Typeform vs Jotform: Which Form Builder Wins in 2024? A Detailed Comparison

When it comes to building online forms, two names dominate the conversation: Typeform and Jotform. Both platforms are powerful, but they serve very different needs. If you're searching for a form builder that balances ease of use, customization, and affordability, you've likely typed "Jotform" into Google more than once. In this detailed comparison, we'll break down the key differences between Typeform and Jotform, covering design, functionality, pricing, integrations, and use cases. By the end, you'll know exactly which tool fits your workflow—and why Jotform might be the smarter choice for most users.

1. User Interface and Ease of Use

Typeform: Beautiful but Limited

Typeform is famous for its sleek, conversational interface. Forms appear one question at a time, creating an engaging, survey-like experience. The drag-and-drop builder is intuitive, but customization options are somewhat restricted unless you dive into CSS. For beginners, Typeform is easy to start with, but advanced users may feel boxed in by the linear format.

Jotform: Flexible and Familiar

Jotform offers a more traditional form layout, similar to Google Forms or Wufoo. Its drag-and-drop builder is packed with widgets, fields, and layout options. You can create multi-column forms, add tables, file uploads, and conditional logic without touching code. The learning curve is slightly steeper due to the sheer number of features, but once you master it, Jotform gives you far more control over the final look and behavior.

Verdict: If you want a beautiful, linear survey, choose Typeform. If you need a powerful, customizable form for data collection, Jotform wins hands down.

2. Templates and Design Customization

Typeform Templates

Typeform offers around 100+ professionally designed templates, mostly focused on surveys, quizzes, and lead generation. You can change colors, fonts, and background images, but the one-question-at-a-time layout limits structural changes. The design is modern and minimal, but it can feel repetitive.

Jotform Templates

Jotform boasts over 10,000+ templates covering everything from order forms to registration, payment forms, and even COVID-19 screening tools. Many templates are pre-integrated with popular apps like PayPal, Stripe, and Salesforce. The design editor allows you to tweak every element—fonts, spacing, column layout, and even custom CSS. For businesses that need a unique look, Jotform is far more versatile.

Verdict: Jotform offers more templates and deeper customization. Typeform is prettier out of the box but limited in variety.

3. Form Logic and Conditional Fields

Typeform Logic

Typeform supports basic conditional logic—show or hide questions based on previous answers. It's easy to set up with a visual branch editor. However, complex conditions (like multiple nested rules or calculations) are harder to implement. The logic also only works within a single form, not across multiple pages.

Jotform Logic

Jotform's conditional logic is far more advanced. You can show/hide fields, skip pages, calculate totals, send emails, and even update other forms based on user input. The "Conditions" panel is visual and supports "if-then-else" logic with multiple triggers and actions. For complex workflows—like multi-step applications or dynamic pricing—Jotform is the clear winner.

Verdict: Jotform's logic is more powerful and flexible. Typeform is simpler but less capable for complex scenarios.

4. Integrations and Automation

Typeform Integrations

Typeform integrates with 500+ apps via Zapier, plus native connections to Google Sheets, Mailchimp, HubSpot, Slack, and more. However, native integrations are limited on lower-tier plans. The automation features (like sending data to a CRM) require manual setup or Zapier subscriptions.

Jotform Integrations

Jotform offers 100+ native integrations, including Google Drive, Dropbox, PayPal, Stripe, AWeber, and Salesforce. It also has a built-in "Form Builder" that can connect to 200+ apps via its own "Integrations" menu—no Zapier needed. Plus, Jotform includes a powerful "Approvals" workflow tool for automating review processes, which Typeform lacks entirely.

Verdict: Jotform provides more native integrations and built-in automation. Typeform relies heavily on third-party tools like Zapier.

5. Pricing and Value for Money

Typeform Pricing

  • Free Plan: 10 forms, 100 responses/month, Typeform branding
  • Plus: $25/month (billed yearly) – 1 user, 1,000 responses/month
  • Business: $50/month – 3 users, 10,000 responses/month
  • Enterprise: Custom pricing

Typeform's pricing scales quickly, and you pay extra for features like removing branding or advanced logic.

Jotform Pricing

  • Free Plan: 5 forms, 100 submissions/month, 100 MB storage, Jotform branding
  • Bronze: $24/month (billed yearly) – 25 forms, 1,000 submissions, 1 GB storage, no branding
  • Silver: $29/month – 50 forms, 2,500 submissions, 10 GB storage
  • Gold: $79/month – 100 forms, 10,000 submissions, 100 GB storage

Jotform's plans include more submissions, storage, and features at similar or lower price points. The free plan is also more generous for small projects.

Verdict: Jotform offers better value, especially for growing businesses. Typeform is more expensive for comparable features.

6. Data Collection and Storage

Typeform Storage

Typeform stores responses in its own database. You can export data as CSV or PDF, but there's no built-in file storage for attachments. File uploads are limited to 1 GB on paid plans. For long-term data management, you'll need to integrate with Google Sheets or a database.

Jotform Storage

Jotform includes robust storage options: you can save submissions directly to Google Drive, Dropbox, OneDrive, or Amazon S3. File uploads are supported up to 10 GB per file on higher plans. Plus, Jotform offers a "Submissions" table where you can view, edit, and manage all entries without leaving the platform—a huge time-saver.

Verdict: Jotform excels at data storage and management. Typeform is more limited and requires external tools.

7. Mobile Responsiveness and Accessibility

Typeform Mobile

Typeform forms are fully responsive and look great on mobile devices—one question at a time adapts naturally to small screens. However, the conversational style can feel slow for users who want to fill out a form quickly.

Jotform Mobile

Jotform forms are also mobile-responsive, but the traditional layout may require more scrolling on phones. However, Jotform offers a dedicated "Mobile Forms" feature that optimizes forms for smartphones, and you can even create offline forms that sync later.

Verdict: Typeform has a slight edge in mobile aesthetics, but Jotform provides more practical mobile tools.

8. Customer Support and Community

Typeform Support

Typeform offers email support on all plans, with live chat on Business and Enterprise. The help center has articles and a community forum, but response times can be slow on free/Plus plans.

Jotform Support

Jotform provides 24/7 live chat and email support on all paid plans, plus a comprehensive knowledge base, video tutorials, and an active user forum. The support team is known for quick, helpful responses—even on the free plan.

Verdict: Jotform offers better support availability and resources.
