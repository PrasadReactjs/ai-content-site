Typeform vs Jotform: The Ultimate Comparison for 2024 – Which Form Builder Wins?

In the digital age, online forms are the backbone of lead generation, customer feedback, event registration, and data collection. Two of the most popular form builders on the market are Typeform and Jotform. While both tools allow you to create forms without coding, they cater to vastly different audiences and use cases. In this comprehensive comparison, we’ll break down every key feature, pricing tier, user experience, and integration to help you decide which platform is right for you. If you’re leaning toward a more flexible, feature-rich solution, Jotform might be exactly what you need—but let’s dive into the details first.

1. Overview: What Are Typeform and Jotform?

Typeform is best known for its sleek, conversational, one-question-at-a-time interface. It’s designed to boost engagement and completion rates by making forms feel like a chat. Marketers, designers, and brands that prioritize aesthetics often choose Typeform.

Jotform, on the other hand, is a powerhouse of customization and functionality. It offers over 10,000 ready-made form templates, a drag-and-drop builder, advanced conditional logic, payment integrations, and even a PDF editor. Jotform is built for businesses that need versatility, from simple contact forms to complex, multi-page applications with file uploads and e-signatures.

2. Ease of Use & Form Builder

Typeform: Beautiful but Limited

Typeform’s builder is intuitive and visually stunning. You start with a blank canvas or a template, then add question blocks. The interface is clean, but customization options are somewhat restricted. For example, you can’t freely position elements on the page—everything follows a linear, one-screen-at-a-time flow. This works well for short surveys but can feel restrictive for long forms.

Jotform: Drag-and-Drop Powerhouse

Jotform’s builder is more traditional but far more flexible. You can drag fields anywhere on the page, resize them, and add custom CSS for total control. The conditional logic is robust: you can show/hide fields, send emails, calculate values, and even trigger webhooks based on user input. For power users, Jotform is the clear winner. It also offers a mobile app for building forms on the go—something Typeform lacks.

3. Templates & Design Options

Typeform: Modern, Minimalist Templates

Typeform offers about 200+ templates, all with a modern, uncluttered look. You can customize colors, fonts, and background images, but the overall layout remains the same. The result is always professional and engaging, but you’ll have a hard time creating a form that looks radically different from the standard Typeform aesthetic.

Jotform: 10,000+ Templates and Full Design Control

This is where Jotform truly shines. With over 10,000 templates covering every industry—healthcare, education, real estate, e-commerce, and more—you’re almost guaranteed to find a starting point. You can also start from scratch and design a form that matches your brand exactly. Jotform supports custom fonts, images, videos, and even allows you to embed forms seamlessly into your website with no iframe code (using its advanced embed options).

4. Features & Functionality

Typeform Key Features

  • Conversational interface – excellent for engagement
  • Logic jumps – show questions based on previous answers
  • Hidden fields – pass data from URLs or cookies
  • Calculations – basic math (sums, averages)
  • Integrations – 120+ apps (Slack, Google Sheets, Mailchimp, etc.)
  • Video questions – ask questions via video

Jotform Key Features

  • Advanced conditional logic – show/hide fields, send emails, update calculations
  • File uploads – with drag-and-drop, up to 1GB per file (paid plans)
  • Payment integrations – PayPal, Stripe, Square, and 30+ gateways
  • E-signatures – built-in signature field (no extra cost)
  • PDF editor – automatically generate PDFs from form submissions
  • Form analytics – detailed reports with graphs and export options
  • Widgets & apps – 350+ integrations and 200+ widgets (maps, calendars, surveys)
  • HIPAA compliance – available on paid plans for healthcare

When it comes to raw functionality, Jotform offers significantly more tools out of the box. Typeform focuses on simplicity, which is great for short forms, but power users will quickly hit a ceiling.

5. Pricing: Free Plans & Paid Tiers

Typeform Pricing

  • Free plan: 10 forms, 100 responses/month, 10 questions per form. No logic jumps or file uploads.
  • Plus plan: $29/month (billed annually) – 1 user, 1,000 responses/month, remove Typeform branding.
  • Business plan: $59/month – 3 users, 10,000 responses/month, advanced logic, priority support.
  • Enterprise: Custom pricing – unlimited responses, dedicated support, SSO.

Note: Typeform’s free plan is very limited. To get basic features like logic jumps, you need the Plus plan at $29/month.

Jotform Pricing

  • Free plan: 5 forms, 100 submissions/month, 100 MB storage. Includes conditional logic and file uploads.
  • Bronze plan: $34/month (billed annually) – 25 forms, 1,000 submissions/month, 1 GB storage, remove branding.
  • Silver plan: $49/month – 50 forms, 10,000 submissions/month, 10 GB storage, advanced form analytics.
  • Gold plan: $99/month – 100 forms, 100,000 submissions/month, 100 GB storage, HIPAA compliance.
  • Enterprise: Custom pricing – unlimited forms, submissions, and storage.

Jotform’s free plan is more generous—you get conditional logic and file uploads without paying. For small businesses, the Bronze plan ($34/month) offers far more value than Typeform’s Plus plan at $29/month.

6. Integrations & Automation

Both platforms integrate with popular apps, but Jotform has a much larger ecosystem. Typeform connects to 120+ apps via Zapier and native integrations (Google Sheets, Mailchimp, HubSpot, etc.). Jotform offers 350+ native integrations and 200+ widgets, plus a powerful App Market. You can also use Jotform’s API for custom workflows.

For automation, Jotform’s conditional logic allows you to set up complex rules without external tools. For example, you can automatically send a thank-you email with a PDF attachment, create a new row in Google Sheets, and post a Slack message—all from a single form submission. Typeform requires Zapier for most advanced automations, which adds cost and complexity.

7. Mobile Experience & Accessibility

Both forms are responsive by default, but there are key differences:

  • Typeform – The conversational design works beautifully on mobile. However, there is no native mobile app for building forms.
  • Jotform – Offers a mobile app for iOS and Android that lets you build forms, view submissions, and even collect data offline. This is a huge advantage for field workers, event staff, and anyone who needs to collect data on the go.
