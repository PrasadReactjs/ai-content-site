Health Tech & Biohacking: The Future of Human Performance

Welcome to the frontier of human optimization. In this edition, we explore the explosive convergence of health tech and biohacking — a movement that blends cutting-edge devices, data-driven biology, and DIY experimentation to upgrade your body and mind. From wearable sensors that track your glucose in real time to nootropics that sharpen cognition, the tools to engineer your own biology are more accessible than ever. Whether you’re a seasoned biohacker or a curious beginner, this newsletter will equip you with actionable insights, practical tools, and evidence-based strategies to take control of your health.

What Is Health Tech and Biohacking?

At its core, health tech and biohacking refers to the practice of using technology, science, and self-experimentation to enhance physical and mental performance. It spans a wide spectrum: from FDA-approved medical devices to unregulated supplements, from sleep tracking apps to implantable microchips. The common thread is the desire to move beyond “normal” health and unlock peak human potential.

Biohackers often adopt a “quantified self” approach — using sensors, blood tests, and logs to measure variables like heart rate variability (HRV), blood glucose, ketone levels, and sleep quality. They then tweak diet, exercise, supplements, or environment to optimize these metrics. The ultimate goal? More energy, sharper focus, better sleep, and longer life.

Top Health Tech Devices for Biohackers in 2025

Here are the most impactful tools currently shaping the health tech and biohacking landscape:

  • Continuous Glucose Monitors (CGMs) — Once reserved for diabetics, CGMs like the Dexcom G7 or Abbott Libre 3 now empower biohackers to see how different foods, exercise, and stress affect their blood sugar. Real-time data helps you avoid energy crashes and reduce inflammation.
  • Wearable Rings (Oura Ring, Ultrahuman) — These sleek rings track sleep stages, HRV, body temperature, and activity. They provide a “readiness score” to guide your daily habits and recovery.
  • Smart Sleep Masks (Dodow, Somnox) — Devices that use light, sound, or vibrations to shorten sleep latency and enhance deep sleep. Some integrate with EEG headbands for neurofeedback.
  • Red Light Therapy Panels — Clinically studied for mitochondrial health, skin rejuvenation, and muscle recovery. Brands like Joovv and MitoRed offer home-use panels.
  • Nootropic Stacks — Smart drugs and supplements (e.g., modafinil, racetams, lion’s mane) that boost focus, memory, and mental clarity. Always start with low doses and consult a doctor.

Practical Biohacking Tips You Can Start Today

You don’t need a lab coat or a six-figure budget to begin biohacking. Here are three actionable strategies grounded in health tech and biohacking principles:

1. Master Your Sleep with Data

Use a wearable to track your sleep cycles. Aim for 7–9 hours with at least 90 minutes of deep sleep. Optimize your environment: blackout curtains, cool room (65–68°F), and no screens 60 minutes before bed. Try a “sleep cocktail” of magnesium glycinate, glycine, and apigenin to fall asleep faster.

2. Experiment with Time-Restricted Eating

Combine your CGM data with an eating window (e.g., 8 hours) to stabilize glucose. Many biohackers report better mental clarity and energy when they skip breakfast and eat lunch and dinner. Use an app like Zero or DoFasting to track your fasts.

3. Cold Exposure for Resilience

Cold plunges (50–59°F for 2–5 minutes) boost dopamine, reduce inflammation, and improve HRV. Start with a cold shower for 30 seconds and work up. Pair with a wearable to monitor your body’s response.

Biohacking Risks and Ethical Considerations

While health tech and biohacking offer exciting possibilities, they also carry risks. Self-experimentation without medical oversight can lead to nutrient imbalances, hormone disruption, or adverse reactions to supplements. Some devices (like unregulated brain stimulators) may cause harm. Always research thoroughly, start with one variable at a time, and consult healthcare professionals when introducing new protocols.

Ethically, the movement raises questions about equity: expensive CGMs and panels are out of reach for many. As the field grows, advocates push for more affordable, accessible tools so biohacking isn’t just for the wealthy.

Future Trends: Where Health Tech Is Headed

The next wave of health tech and biohacking includes:

  • Epigenetic Testing — Companies like TruDiagnostic analyze DNA methylation to estimate biological age and provide personalized lifestyle interventions.
  • Smart Contact Lenses — Prototypes from Mojo Vision and others promise to display health metrics directly on your eye.
  • AI-Powered Personalized Nutrition — Services like InsideTracker and Zoe combine blood biomarkers, microbiome analysis, and AI to generate meal plans optimized for your unique biology.
  • Implantable Biochips — Microchips that monitor internal health markers (e.g., lactate, pH) and wirelessly send data to your phone.

Conclusion: Your Biohacking Journey Starts Now

Health tech and biohacking are not just trends — they represent a fundamental shift in how we approach well-being. By leveraging data, technology, and self-experimentation, you can take unprecedented control of your health. Start small: pick one metric to track (like sleep or glucose), choose one tool, and commit to a 30-day experiment. Document your results, adjust, and iterate.

Remember: the goal is not perfection, but progress. Your biology is your most valuable asset — invest in it wisely.
