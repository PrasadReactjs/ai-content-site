Health Tech & Biohacking: The Ultimate Newsletter Guide to Optimizing Your Biology

Welcome to this edition of our deep dive into the world of health tech and biohacking. If you've ever wondered how to upgrade your biology using cutting-edge technology, you're in the right place. From wearable sensors that track your sleep cycles to nootropics that sharpen your focus, the convergence of biology and gadgets is reshaping how we approach wellness. In this newsletter, we’ll explore the latest trends, actionable tips, and expert insights to help you biohack smarter—not harder.

Whether you're a seasoned biohacker or a curious beginner, the goal is simple: use health tech to gain control over your healthspan, energy, and cognitive performance. Let's get started.

What Is Biohacking? A Quick Refresher

Biohacking is the practice of making small, strategic changes to your lifestyle, diet, and environment to improve your body's performance. When combined with health tech, it becomes a data-driven approach to self-optimization. Think of it as "personalized medicine for the DIY crowd." Common biohacking tools include:

  • Wearable devices (e.g., Oura Ring, Whoop, Apple Watch) for tracking sleep, heart rate variability (HRV), and activity.
  • Continuous glucose monitors (CGMs) like Levels or Dexcom to understand how food affects your blood sugar.
  • Nootropics and supplements such as L-theanine, creatine, or modafinil for cognitive enhancement.
  • Light therapy (red light panels, blue-blocking glasses) to regulate circadian rhythms.
  • Cold exposure and heat therapy (ice baths, saunas) for recovery and resilience.

The key is to test, measure, and iterate. Without health tech, biohacking is just guesswork. With it, you get real-time feedback.

Top Health Tech Trends in Biohacking Right Now

1. Wearables Go Beyond Step Counting

Modern wearables are no longer just pedometers. The latest generation of health tech devices can measure HRV, skin temperature, blood oxygen saturation (SpO2), and even electrodermal activity (stress levels). For biohackers, HRV is a goldmine—it indicates how well your nervous system is recovering. Devices like the Oura Ring Gen 3 and Whoop 4.0 provide daily readiness scores, helping you decide when to push hard or rest.

Actionable tip: Wear your device consistently for 30 days to establish a baseline. Then, experiment with one variable (e.g., sleep duration, meal timing) and track how your HRV changes.

2. Continuous Glucose Monitors (CGMs) for Metabolic Health

Once reserved for diabetics, CGMs are now mainstream in the biohacking community. A small sensor on your arm sends real-time glucose data to your phone. This allows you to see exactly how different foods, exercise, and stress spike or crash your blood sugar. Stabilizing glucose is a cornerstone of longevity and energy management.

Actionable tip: Try a 2-week CGM trial. After each meal, note how you feel (energy, focus, cravings) and compare it to your glucose curve. You’ll quickly learn which "healthy" foods actually work for your body.

3. Smart Sleep Tech

Sleep is the ultimate biohack. New health tech like the Eight Sleep Pod Cover or ChiliPad actively regulates bed temperature to keep you in deep sleep longer. Meanwhile, apps like Sleep Cycle use sound analysis to wake you during light sleep. Some biohackers also use electroencephalogram (EEG) headbands like Muse to train their brain for better sleep onset.

Actionable tip: Set your bedroom temperature to 65–68°F (18–20°C). Use a sleep tracker to find your optimal wake window (usually 90-minute cycles).

4. Red Light Therapy at Home

Red and near-infrared light therapy devices (like those from Joovv or Mito Red Light) are booming. Research suggests they can boost mitochondrial function, reduce inflammation, improve skin health, and even enhance muscle recovery. Many biohackers now have panels in their home for daily use.

Actionable tip: Use a red light panel for 10–15 minutes per day, from a distance of 6–12 inches. Focus on areas like the face, neck, or sore muscles.

How to Build Your Own Biohacking Stack: A Practical Guide

With so many gadgets and supplements, it's easy to get overwhelmed. Here’s a step-by-step approach to build a personalized health tech and biohacking stack:

  • Step 1: Define your goal. Do you want better sleep, more energy, sharper focus, or longevity? Pick one primary goal.
  • Step 2: Choose one tracker. Start with a wearable that measures your key metric (e.g., Oura for sleep, Whoop for recovery).
  • Step 3: Add one intervention. For example, if you want better sleep, try a CGM to see how late-night eating affects your rest. Or use blue-blocking glasses 2 hours before bed.
  • Step 4: Measure for 2 weeks. Don't change anything else. Compare your baseline data to the intervention period.
  • Step 5: Iterate. Keep what works, drop what doesn't. Add one new variable at a time.

Remember: the best health tech is the one you actually use. Don't buy every gadget at once.

Expert Insights: What the Data Says

Dr. Andrew Huberman, a neuroscientist at Stanford, often emphasizes that light exposure is the most powerful biohack. He recommends viewing natural sunlight within 30–60 minutes of waking to set your circadian clock. Meanwhile, Dr. Peter Attia (author of Outlive) advocates for CGMs and continuous monitoring of apoB (a cholesterol marker) for longevity.

In the health tech space, companies like InsideTracker now offer blood test analysis that gives you personalized recommendations for diet, exercise, and supplements based on your biomarkers. This is the future of biohacking: data-driven, precise, and actionable.

Common Biohacking Mistakes to Avoid

Even with the best health tech, it's easy to fall into traps. Here are three common pitfalls:

  • Over-optimization paralysis: Trying to track everything at once leads to burnout. Focus on one metric at a time.
  • Ignoring the basics: No gadget can fix poor sleep, a bad diet, or chronic stress. Fix the foundations first.
  • Confusing correlation with causation: Just because your HRV went up after a cold plunge doesn’t mean it was the only factor. Control for variables.

The Future of Health Tech and Biohacking

We're moving toward a world where health tech becomes invisible and predictive. Imagine a smart ring that alerts you to an impending illness 48 hours before symptoms appear, or a wearable that delivers personalized nutrient timing based on your real-time blood glucose. Companies like Levels, Veri, and Superset are already building this ecosystem.

Another frontier is "digital therapeutics"—apps and devices prescribed by doctors for conditions like insomnia, anxiety, or ADHD. For example, the FDA-cleared EndeavorRx video game improves attention in children with ADHD. Biohackers are now using similar neurofeedback tools for cognitive enhancement.
