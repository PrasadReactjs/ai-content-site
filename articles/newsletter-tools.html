The Ultimate Guide to the Best Newsletter Tools in 2025: Pricing, Features, and Recommendations

Email marketing isn’t dead—it’s evolving. In fact, for every $1 you spend on email marketing, the average return is $42. Whether you’re a solopreneur, a growing startup, or an established e-commerce brand, choosing the right newsletter tools can make or break your campaign’s success. With dozens of platforms claiming to be the best, how do you separate the hype from the genuinely useful?

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll break down the top newsletter tools on the market. We’ll cover pricing, standout features, pros and cons, and give you clear recommendations based on your business size and goals. By the end, you’ll know exactly which platform to choose—and how to maximize your ROI.

1. Mailchimp: Best for Beginners and Small Businesses

Mailchimp remains one of the most recognizable names in email marketing. Its drag-and-drop builder, pre-designed templates, and free tier make it a go-to for those just starting with newsletter tools.

Pricing

  • Free Plan: Up to 500 contacts, 1,000 sends/month, basic templates, and Mailchimp branding.
  • Essentials: Starts at $13/month (500 contacts). Removes branding, adds A/B testing, and custom templates.
  • Standard: $20/month (500 contacts). Includes customer journey builder, predictive segmentation, and retargeting ads.
  • Premium: $350/month (10,000 contacts). Advanced segmentation, phone support, and multivariate testing.

Key Features

  • Drag-and-drop email builder with 100+ templates
  • Audience segmentation and tags
  • Basic automation (welcome emails, abandoned cart)
  • Landing page and form builder
  • Integrations with Shopify, WooCommerce, WordPress
  • Built-in analytics (open rates, click maps, ROI tracking)

Pros

  • Excellent free plan for up to 500 contacts
  • User-friendly interface with minimal learning curve
  • Strong template library and design flexibility
  • Wide integration ecosystem

Cons

  • Free plan includes Mailchimp branding in emails
  • Automation features are limited on lower tiers
  • Pricing jumps significantly as your list grows
  • Customer support can be slow on free/essentials plans

Best For

Small businesses, bloggers, and solo entrepreneurs who want a simple, all-in-one newsletter tool with a low barrier to entry.

2. ConvertKit: Best for Creators and Course Builders

ConvertKit (now part of Kit) was built specifically for creators, authors, and online educators. Its focus on subscriber tagging and visual automation makes it a powerful newsletter tool for nurturing a highly engaged audience.

Pricing

  • Free Plan: Up to 1,000 subscribers, unlimited landing pages, and forms. Limited to one automation sequence.
  • Creator: Starts at $29/month (1,000 subscribers). Unlimited automation, email sequences, and subscriber scoring.
  • Creator Pro: $59/month (1,000 subscribers). Adds newsletter referral system, advanced reporting, and deliverability support.

Key Features

  • Visual automation builder (triggers, conditions, actions)
  • Subscriber tagging and custom fields
  • Landing pages and opt-in forms (no coding)
  • Built-in commerce (sell digital products directly)
  • Subscriber scoring to identify hot leads
  • Integrations with Teachable, Gumroad, WordPress

Pros

  • Intuitive tagging system for deep segmentation
  • Clean, distraction-free email editor
  • Excellent deliverability rates
  • Free plan supports up to 1,000 subscribers

Cons

  • Limited design flexibility (no drag-and-drop templates)
  • More expensive per subscriber than Mailchimp at higher volumes
  • No A/B testing on Creator plan
  • Lacks advanced e-commerce features (no abandoned cart)

Best For

Bloggers, YouTubers, podcasters, and course creators who need a reliable newsletter tool focused on audience growth and automation.

3. ActiveCampaign: Best for Advanced Automation and CRM

ActiveCampaign is the heavyweight champion when it comes to automation. It combines email marketing with a built-in CRM, making it a top newsletter tool for B2B companies and e-commerce stores that want to scale.

Pricing

  • Starter: $15/month (1,000 contacts). Email campaigns, forms, and basic automation.
  • Plus: $49/month (1,000 contacts). Adds landing pages, lead scoring, and conditional content.
  • Professional: $79/month (1,000 contacts). Predictive sending, split automations, and attribution reporting.
  • Enterprise: Custom pricing. Custom reporting, custom mail server, and dedicated support.

Key Features

  • Visual automation builder with 500+ pre-built recipes
  • Built-in CRM with lead scoring and deal tracking
  • Conditional content (show different blocks based on subscriber data)
  • Predictive sending (optimizes send times per user)
  • Split automations (A/B test entire email sequences)
  • Deep integrations with Salesforce, HubSpot, and Shopify

Pros

  • Unmatched automation capabilities
  • CRM integration eliminates the need for a separate tool
  • Excellent segmentation with custom fields and tags
  • Strong deliverability and spam testing tools

Cons

  • Steep learning curve for beginners
  • Email editor feels dated compared to newer tools
  • Pricing increases quickly as list grows
  • No native video hosting or advanced design tools

Best For

Growing businesses and marketing teams that need a powerful newsletter tool with CRM and complex automation workflows.

4. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue): Best for Budget-Conscious Marketers

Brevo offers a unique pricing model based on email volume rather than subscriber count. This makes it one of the most affordable newsletter tools for businesses with large lists but moderate sending frequency.

Pricing

  • Free Plan: 300 emails/day, unlimited contacts, and basic templates.
  • Starter: $25/month (20,000 emails/month). No daily limit, remove branding.
  • Business: $65/month (20,000 emails/month). Adds A/B testing, multi-user access, and advanced automation.
  • Enterprise: Custom pricing. Dedicated IP, phone support, and custom integrations.

Key Features

  • Drag-and-drop email builder with responsive templates
  • Marketing automation (workflows, triggers, conditions)
  • Built-in CRM (limited on Starter)
  • SMS marketing and transactional emails
  • A/B testing and heatmaps
  • Facebook Ads integration

Pros

  • Pricing based on emails sent, not contacts—ideal for large lists
  • Free plan includes unlimited contacts (with daily send limit)
