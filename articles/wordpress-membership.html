Best WordPress Membership Plugin 2025: Top Picks, Pricing & Features Compared

Are you looking to turn your website into a thriving membership site? Whether you're selling online courses, exclusive content, or a subscription community, the right WordPress membership plugin is the backbone of your business. With dozens of options on the market, choosing the perfect one can be overwhelming.

In this comprehensive guide, we've tested and compared the best WordPress membership plugins to help you make an informed decision. We'll break down pricing, key features, pros and cons, and give you our honest recommendations—so you can start building your membership site with confidence.

Why You Need a WordPress Membership Plugin

Before diving into the specific plugins, let's understand why a dedicated WordPress membership plugin is essential:

  • Content restriction – Protect your premium content from non-members
  • Payment integration – Accept recurring subscriptions seamlessly
  • User management – Control access levels and member roles
  • Automated workflows – Send welcome emails, expiration notices, and more
  • Analytics – Track member growth, churn, and revenue

The right plugin simplifies all of this, letting you focus on creating valuable content for your members.

Top 5 WordPress Membership Plugins Compared

1. MemberPress – Best All-in-One Solution

MemberPress is widely regarded as the gold standard for WordPress membership sites. It's incredibly powerful yet user-friendly, making it ideal for beginners and advanced users alike.

Pricing

  • Basic: $179/year (1 site)
  • Plus: $299/year (2 sites)
  • Pro: $599/year (5 sites)

Key Features

  • Unlimited membership levels and pricing tiers
  • Drip content and content expiration
  • Built-in course builder (MemberPress Courses)
  • Powerful access rules (by page, category, tag, or custom URL)
  • Integration with Stripe, PayPal, Authorize.net
  • Automatic email reminders and coupons
  • Developer-friendly with hooks and API

Pros

  • Very intuitive interface with visual setup wizard
  • Excellent documentation and support
  • Lightweight and doesn't slow down your site
  • Works seamlessly with most WordPress themes

Cons

  • No free version (but 14-day money-back guarantee)
  • Advanced features (like subscriptions) require the Pro plan

Best for: Most membership sites, especially those selling courses or recurring subscriptions.

2. Restrict Content Pro – Best for Simple Membership Sites

If you want a straightforward, no-frills WordPress membership plugin that just works, Restrict Content Pro (RCP) is an excellent choice. It's clean, fast, and focused on core membership functionality.

Pricing

  • Personal: $99/year (1 site)
  • Plus: $199/year (3 sites)
  • Professional: $349/year (unlimited sites)

Key Features

  • Unlimited membership levels and discount codes
  • Content restriction by post type, taxonomy, or individual page
  • Drip content and content expirations
  • Built-in payment gateways (Stripe, PayPal, Braintree)
  • Member management dashboard
  • Email templates for member notifications
  • Add-ons for Mailchimp, bbPress, and more

Pros

  • Extremely lightweight and fast
  • Easy to set up and configure
  • Great for sites with simple membership needs
  • Affordable pricing

Cons

  • No built-in course builder (requires add-ons)
  • Limited advanced automation features out of the box

Best for: Bloggers, content creators, and small businesses who need basic content restriction without complexity.

Paid Memberships Pro (PMPro) is the most popular free WordPress membership plugin, and for good reason. The core plugin is completely free, with premium add-ons available for advanced features.

Pricing

  • Free core plugin (unlimited members and levels)
  • Premium add-ons: $297/year (unlimited sites) or individual add-ons from $47

Key Features

  • Unlimited membership levels and discount codes
  • Content restriction by post, page, category, or custom post type
  • Drip content and content expiration
  • Built-in payment gateways (Stripe, PayPal, 2Checkout)
  • Member directory and profile fields
  • Email templates and automation
  • Integration with popular LMS plugins (LearnDash, LifterLMS)

Pros

  • Free core plugin is very capable
  • Large community and extensive documentation
  • Highly extensible with add-ons
  • No limits on members or levels

Cons

  • Free version lacks some advanced features (e.g., subscriptions, recurring payments)
  • User interface feels a bit dated
  • Add-ons can become expensive if you need many

Best for: Budget-conscious users or those who want to start free and upgrade later.

4. WishList Member – Best for Community Sites

WishList Member is a veteran WordPress membership plugin known for its robust membership management and community features. It's particularly strong if you want to integrate forums or group functionality.

Pricing

  • Standard: $247/year (3 sites)
  • Plus: $397/year (10 sites)
  • Pro: $597/year (unlimited sites)

Key Features

  • Unlimited membership levels and pricing models
  • Content dripping, expiration, and sequential access
  • Built-in forum integration (bbPress, BuddyPress)
  • Advanced protection (by user role, IP, or cookie)
  • Mass import/export members
  • Integration with Stripe, PayPal, ClickBank, and more
  • Shortcodes and widgets for member areas

Pros

  • Excellent community and forum support
  • Very flexible with membership levels
  • Strong focus on member management

Cons

  • Steeper learning curve than MemberPress
  • No built-in course builder
  • Interface can feel cluttered

Best for: Community-driven membership sites, forums, and social networks.
