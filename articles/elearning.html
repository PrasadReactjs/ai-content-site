Choosing the Right eLearning Platform: A Comprehensive Comparison of Pricing, Features, and Monetization

The global elearning market is booming, projected to reach over $1 trillion by 2032. Whether you're a seasoned educator, a corporate trainer, or a content creator looking to monetize your expertise, selecting the right online course platform is the single most important decision you'll make. With dozens of options available, each with unique pricing structures, audience reach, and monetization tools, the choice can be overwhelming.

This guide provides a thorough, side-by-side comparison of the leading elearning platforms. We'll break down their strengths, weaknesses, and ideal use cases, helping you find the perfect fit for your teaching style and business goals. We'll also include practical tips to maximize your success and monetization potential.

Top eLearning Platforms Compared

1. Teachable: The All-in-One Creator Hub

Pricing: Free plan available (with transaction fees). Paid plans start at $39/month (Basic), $119/month (Pro), and $199/month (Business). Transaction fees range from 5% (Free) down to 0% on higher tiers.

Features: Teachable excels in ease of use. It offers a drag-and-drop course builder, customizable sales pages, integrated email marketing, quizzes, certificates, and a built-in affiliate program system. It also supports multimedia content (video, PDFs, audio).

Audience Reach: Moderate. Teachable provides a basic marketplace but relies heavily on your own marketing efforts. You can embed courses on your own website or use Teachable's hosted domain.

Monetization Options: Excellent for course sales, subscriptions, and bundles. The affiliate program feature allows you to recruit partners who earn commissions. You can also offer payment plans and coupons.

Best For: Solo creators, coaches, and small businesses who want a professional-looking school without technical hassle.

2. Thinkific: The Feature-Rich Alternative

Pricing: Free plan (limited). Paid plans start at $49/month (Basic), $99/month (Pro), and $199/month (Premier). No transaction fees on paid plans.

Features: Thinkific is known for its robust feature set, including advanced course drip content, membership sites, quizzes, surveys, and a powerful community builder. It also offers a built-in app builder for iOS and Android, plus seamless integrations with email marketing tools like Mailchimp and ConvertKit.

Audience Reach: Moderate to high. Thinkific's community features help retain students. It also has a marketplace (Thinkific Plus) for larger audiences.

Monetization Options: Similar to Teachable—course sales, subscriptions, bundles, and payment plans. The community feature can be monetized as a premium membership tier.

Best For: Course creators who want advanced features, community engagement, and no transaction fees. Ideal for growing businesses.

3. Kajabi: The All-in-One Business Platform

Pricing: Premium. Plans start at $149/month (Basic), $199/month (Growth), and $399/month (Pro). No transaction fees.

Features: Kajabi is a full-fledged marketing and sales platform. It includes a powerful email marketing engine, landing page builder, sales funnel automation, pipelines, and a built-in CRM. Course creation is intuitive, with support for videos, quizzes, and assessments. It also offers a mobile app and community features.

Audience Reach: High for those who use its marketing tools. Kajabi's automation and sales funnels help you build and nurture an audience directly.

Monetization Options: Excellent. You can sell courses, memberships, coaching programs, and digital downloads. The pipeline feature allows you to create complex sales sequences, upsells, and downsells.

Best For: Serious entrepreneurs and established businesses who want an all-in-one solution and are willing to invest for premium features and automation.

4. Udemy: The Massive Marketplace

Pricing: Free to publish. Revenue share is 97% for instructor-generated sales (via coupons) and 25% for organic marketplace sales. Udemy frequently offers deep discounts, which can affect your earnings.

Features: Minimal. You get a basic course builder, video hosting, quizzes, and discussion forums. No advanced marketing or customization tools. Udemy handles all hosting, payment processing, and student acquisition.

Audience Reach: Massive. Udemy has over 60 million students worldwide. Your course can be discovered by a huge, built-in audience.

Monetization Options: Limited but high volume. You rely on Udemy's traffic. Your earnings per sale are lower due to the revenue split, but you can sell thousands of courses. You can also run promotions and use coupons to drive sales.

Best For: Instructors who want to reach a massive audience quickly, especially for high-volume, lower-priced courses. Good for building reputation and getting reviews.

5. Skillshare: The Subscription-Based Model

Pricing: Free to publish. You earn royalties based on minutes watched by Premium members. Rates vary but typically range from $0.05 to $0.10 per minute watched.

Features: Very basic. Skillshare provides a simple course uploader, video hosting, and community features (discussions, projects). No advanced marketing or customization.

Audience Reach: Large. Skillshare has millions of active subscribers who pay a monthly fee for unlimited access to all courses.

Monetization Options: Passive and royalty-based. You earn recurring income based on student engagement. High-quality, popular courses can generate significant passive income over time.

Best For: Creative professionals (design, photography, writing, etc.) who want to earn passive income from a subscription model. Good for building a following and getting feedback.

Key Factors to Consider When Choosing

Pricing and Transaction Fees

  • Free vs. Paid Plans: Platforms like Udemy and Skillshare are free to start but take a large revenue share. Teachable and Thinkific have paid plans but lower (or zero) transaction fees.
  • Hidden Costs: Consider payment processing fees (usually 2-3%), third-party integrations (e.g., email marketing), and add-ons (e.g., custom domain, advanced analytics).
  • Monetization Model: If you plan to charge $50+ per course, a platform with no transaction fees (Kajabi, Thinkific) may be better. For low-priced courses, Udemy's volume can work.

Features and Customization

  • Course Builder: Look for drag-and-drop editors, multimedia support, and the ability to create drip content, quizzes, and assignments.
  • Marketing Tools: Essential for building your own audience. Features like email marketing, landing pages, sales funnels, and affiliate programs are crucial for self-hosted platforms.
  • Community: Built-in community features (forums, groups) help with student retention and engagement, which is valuable for subscription models.
  • Mobile App: Some platforms offer white-label mobile apps (Kajabi, Thinkific) for a more professional experience.

Audience Reach and Discovery

  • Marketplace vs. Self-Hosted: Udemy and Skillshare bring students to you. Teachable, Thinkific, and Kajabi require you to drive your own traffic via SEO, social media, ads, or email lists.
  • SEO and Marketing: If you have an existing audience (e.g., blog, YouTube channel, email list), self-hosted platforms give you full control. If you're starting from scratch, a marketplace can provide initial traction.

Monetization Flexibility

  • Course Sales: One-time payments are standard on most platforms.
  • Subscriptions: Kajabi and Thinkific excel at membership sites. Skillshare is subscription-only for students.
  • Bundles and Upsells: Kajabi's pipeline feature allows for sophisticated sales funnels. Teachable and Thinkific support course bundles.
  • Affiliate Programs: Teachable and Kajabi have built-in affiliate systems. You can also use third-party tools like Post Affiliate Pro with other platforms.
