Best CRM for Nonprofits: Top Fundraising Tools to Supercharge Your Mission

Running a nonprofit is challenging. You’re juggling donor relationships, volunteer coordination, event planning, and financial reporting—often with a lean team and a tight budget. The right Customer Relationship Management (CRM) system can transform your operations, but with so many options, how do you choose?

In this guide, we’ll break down the best CRM for nonprofits, focusing on their fundraising tools, pricing, features, and real pros and cons. Whether you’re a small grassroots organization or a growing foundation, these recommendations will help you maximize impact without breaking the bank.

Why Nonprofits Need Specialized Fundraising Tools

Generic CRMs miss the mark for nonprofits. You need fundraising tools that handle recurring donations, grant tracking, donor segmentation, and tax receipt generation. The best nonprofit CRMs are built to:

  • Automate thank-you emails and receipts
  • Track donor engagement across email, events, and social media
  • Generate reports for board meetings and grant applications
  • Integrate with payment processors like Stripe or PayPal
  • Offer discounted or free pricing for nonprofits

Below, we compare the top contenders, so you can pick the perfect fit for your organization.

1. Salesforce Nonprofit Cloud (formerly NPSP)

Overview

Salesforce is the 800-pound gorilla of CRMs. Their Nonprofit Cloud is built on the world’s most powerful CRM platform, offering enterprise-grade fundraising tools for mid-to-large nonprofits.

Key Features

  • Donor management with 360° views
  • Recurring donation management
  • Grant and program tracking
  • Custom dashboards and reporting
  • Volunteer management (via add-ons)
  • AI-powered insights with Einstein Analytics

Pricing

Salesforce offers 10 free licenses for nonprofits via their Power of Us program. Additional licenses start at $25/user/month (billed annually). For full fundraising automation, expect to pay $50–$100/user/month.

Pros

  • Unmatched customization and scalability
  • Massive app ecosystem (AppExchange)
  • Excellent for large, complex organizations
  • Free initial licenses

Cons

  • Steep learning curve
  • Expensive add-ons for advanced features
  • Requires dedicated admin or consultant
  • Can be overkill for small nonprofits

Best For

Nonprofits with 10+ staff members, a budget for training, and a need for deep customization.

2. Bloomerang

Overview

Bloomerang is a purpose-built nonprofit CRM that focuses on donor retention and relationship building. It’s designed to be intuitive, so you can start using it on day one.

Key Features

  • Donor engagement scoring
  • Automated gift acknowledgments
  • Event management with online registration
  • Built-in email marketing (with templates)
  • Mobile app for on-the-go access
  • Pre-built reports for board meetings

Pricing

Start at $119/month for 1,000 contacts (billed annually). Scales up to $349/month for 10,000 contacts. No per-user fees—unlimited users included.

Pros

  • Extremely user-friendly
  • Great donor retention analytics
  • No per-user cost
  • Excellent customer support

Cons

  • Limited customization compared to Salesforce
  • No built-in volunteer management
  • Advanced reporting requires add-ons

Best For

Small to mid-sized nonprofits that prioritize donor retention and want a fast setup.

3. Neon One (formerly Neon CRM)

Overview

Neon One is an all-in-one solution combining CRM, fundraising, and membership management. It’s particularly strong for organizations that run events and membership programs.

Key Features

  • Membership management with auto-renewals
  • Peer-to-peer fundraising tools
  • Event ticketing and registration
  • Donor portal for self-service
  • Integrated email marketing
  • Grant management

Pricing

Starts at $89/month for 1,000 contacts. Mid-tier plans around $169/month. Enterprise plans custom quoted. Transaction fees apply (2.5% + $0.30).

Pros

  • Comprehensive event and membership features
  • Good for organizations with recurring revenue models
  • Built-in donation forms
  • Strong community support

Cons

  • Interface can feel dated
  • Some features require technical setup
  • Transaction fees can add up

Best For

Membership-based nonprofits, museums, and organizations that run frequent events.

4. Kindful

Overview

Kindful (now part of Bloomerang) is a donor management platform known for its beautiful interface and powerful integrations. It’s designed to make fundraising data accessible to everyone on your team.

Key Features

  • Visual donor timelines
  • Automated data enrichment from social media
  • Integration with QuickBooks, Mailchimp, and more
  • Custom donation forms
  • Text-to-give capability
  • Real-time fundraising dashboards

Pricing

Starts at $159/month for 1,000 contacts. Scales to $449/month for 10,000 contacts. No per-user fees.

Pros

  • Beautiful, intuitive interface
  • Strong integration ecosystem
  • Great for visual reporting
  • No user limits

Cons

  • Less customizable than Salesforce
  • Limited advanced automation
  • Higher starting price than some competitors

Best For

Nonprofits that want a beautiful, easy-to-use CRM with strong accounting integration.

5. DonorPerfect

Overview

DonorPerfect is a veteran in the nonprofit space, offering robust fundraising tools with a focus on data security and compliance. It’s trusted by thousands of organizations worldwide.

Key Features

  • Customizable donor profiles
  • Batch gift entry for large donations
  • Online donation forms with recurring options
  • Email marketing (with tracking)
  • Grant and foundation research tools
  • HIPAA-compliant options available

Pricing

Starts at $99/month for 1,000 contacts. Mid-tier plans around $199/month. Enterprise plans custom quoted. Per-user fees apply ($10–$20/user/month).

Pros

  • Proven reliability and data security
  • Excellent for grant tracking
  • Good reporting and analytics
  • Large user community

Cons

  • Interface can feel clunky
  • Per-user fees increase costs
  • Limited mobile app functionality

Best For

Established nonprofits that need robust grant management and data security features.
