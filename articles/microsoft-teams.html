Slack vs Microsoft Teams: The Ultimate Comparison for 2025

In the modern workplace, choosing the right collaboration tool can make or break your team's productivity. Two giants dominate the space: Slack and Microsoft Teams. While both offer messaging, file sharing, and integrations, they cater to different needs. This comprehensive comparison will help you decide which platform fits your organization—with a special focus on Microsoft Teams and its growing ecosystem.

Whether you're a startup founder, IT manager, or remote team lead, understanding the nuances between these tools is critical. Let’s dive deep into features, pricing, security, and usability to help you make an informed decision.

Overview: What Are Slack and Microsoft Teams?

Slack launched in 2013 as a pure-play team communication app. It’s known for its intuitive interface, threaded conversations, and extensive third-party app integrations. Slack is popular among tech startups, creative agencies, and businesses that prioritize simplicity and flexibility.

Microsoft Teams, released in 2017, is part of the Microsoft 365 suite. It integrates deeply with Office apps like Word, Excel, and SharePoint, making it a natural choice for enterprises already using Microsoft products. Teams combines chat, video conferencing, file storage, and collaboration into a single hub. As of 2025, Teams boasts over 320 million monthly active users, outpacing Slack in enterprise adoption.

Key Features Comparison

1. User Interface and Ease of Use

Slack offers a clean, minimalist design. Channels are organized by topic, and the left sidebar shows all workspaces and direct messages. Slack’s search functionality is powerful, allowing users to find messages, files, and even code snippets quickly.

Microsoft Teams has a more complex interface due to its integration with the Microsoft ecosystem. The left rail includes Activity, Chat, Teams, Calendar, Calls, and Files tabs. While this can feel overwhelming to new users, it provides a unified workspace. Teams also supports customizable tabs and bots, but the learning curve is steeper.

Verdict: Slack wins for simplicity. Microsoft Teams wins for depth of features.

2. Communication: Messaging and Calling

Both platforms support direct messages, group chats, and channels. However, there are key differences:

  • Threads: Slack pioneered threaded replies, keeping conversations organized. Microsoft Teams also supports threads, but they can feel less intuitive.
  • Video Conferencing: Slack offers Slack Huddles for quick audio calls and Slack Connect for external collaboration. However, video calls in Slack are limited to 50 participants on free plans and require paid add-ons for larger meetings.
  • Microsoft Teams excels here: it supports up to 1,000 participants (with view-only mode for 20,000) and includes features like live captions, background blur, breakout rooms, and Together Mode. Teams also integrates with Microsoft Phone System for enterprise calling.

Verdict: Microsoft Teams is superior for large meetings and enterprise calling. Slack is better for lightweight, quick audio chats.

3. File Sharing and Collaboration

Slack integrates with Google Drive, Dropbox, and other cloud services. Files are stored in each workspace, and free plans are limited to 5GB of storage. Collaboration on documents typically requires opening files in external apps.

Microsoft Teams uses SharePoint and OneDrive for file storage. Teams allows real-time co-authoring on Word, Excel, and PowerPoint documents directly within the app. Version history and permissions are managed through SharePoint, making it ideal for organizations that need robust document management.

Verdict: Microsoft Teams is unmatched for collaborative document editing. Slack is better for quick file sharing with external partners.

4. Integrations and App Ecosystem

Slack boasts over 2,400 apps in its App Directory, including popular tools like Asana, Trello, Zoom, and GitHub. Slack’s open API allows custom integrations, and Slack Connect enables secure collaboration with external organizations.

Microsoft Teams integrates natively with all Microsoft 365 apps (Outlook, Planner, Power BI, etc.) and offers connectors for third-party services. While Teams has fewer third-party apps than Slack, its deep integration with Power Platform allows users to build custom workflows and bots without coding.

Verdict: Slack has a larger third-party ecosystem. Microsoft Teams offers deeper integration with Microsoft products.

5. Security and Compliance

Both platforms offer enterprise-grade security, but Microsoft Teams has an edge due to its compliance with more standards:

  • Slack: Supports SAML SSO, data encryption at rest and in transit, and compliance with SOC 2, SOC 3, and GDPR. Enterprise Grid plans include advanced data retention policies.
  • Microsoft Teams: Backed by Microsoft’s security infrastructure, Teams complies with HIPAA, FedRAMP, ISO 27001, and more. It includes features like conditional access policies, Data Loss Prevention (DLP), and eDiscovery. Microsoft also offers Customer Key for encryption at rest.

Verdict: Microsoft Teams is better for regulated industries like healthcare, finance, and government.

Pricing Comparison

Slack:

  • Free: 10,000 message search limit, 10 apps, 1:1 video calls
  • Pro: $7.25/user/month (billed annually) – unlimited messages, integrations, and group video calls
  • Business+: $12.50/user/month – advanced identity management and compliance
  • Enterprise Grid: Custom pricing – unlimited workspaces and advanced security

Microsoft Teams:

  • Free: Unlimited chat, 60-minute group meetings, 5GB cloud storage
  • Microsoft 365 Business Basic: $6.00/user/month – includes web and mobile Office apps, 1TB storage
  • Microsoft 365 Business Standard: $12.50/user/month – includes desktop Office apps and advanced meeting features
  • Microsoft 365 E3: $23.00/user/month – full security and compliance suite

Verdict: Microsoft Teams offers better value for organizations already using Microsoft 365. Slack is more affordable for small teams that don't need Office apps.

Practical Tips: Which Platform Should You Choose?

Here’s actionable advice based on your organization’s needs:

  • Choose Microsoft Teams if: You use Microsoft 365, need advanced video conferencing, or operate in a regulated industry. Teams is also better for organizations with over 500 users due to its scalability.
  • Choose Slack if: Your team is small (<50 people), you rely on many third-party apps, or you prioritize a clean, intuitive interface. Slack is also excellent for external collaboration with clients or partners.
  • Hybrid approach: Some organizations use both—Slack for internal communication and Microsoft Teams for meetings and document collaboration. However, this can lead to confusion and duplication of efforts.

Pros and Cons Summary

Slack Pros:

  • Superior user experience and search
  • Extensive third-party integrations
  • Great for external collaboration (Slack Connect)
  • Lightweight and fast

Slack Cons:

  • Limited video conferencing features
  • Expensive for large teams ($7.25+/user/month)
  • Less comprehensive security for regulated industries
  • Limited file storage on free plan

Microsoft Teams Pros:

  • Deep integration with Microsoft 365
  • Robust video conferencing (up to 1,000 participants)
  • Strong security and compliance
  • Real-time co-authoring on documents

Microsoft Teams Cons:

  • Steeper learning curve
  • Can be resource-heavy (CPU/memory)
  • Less intuitive for small teams
  • Third-party app ecosystem smaller than Slack
