Figma vs Sketch: The Ultimate Design Tool Comparison for 2024

If you're a UI/UX designer, product manager, or startup founder, you've likely faced the burning question: Should I use Figma or Sketch? Both are industry-leading design tools, but they cater to different workflows, team structures, and budgets. In this comprehensive comparison, we'll break down every critical aspect—from collaboration and pricing to plugin ecosystems and performance—so you can make an informed decision for your next project.

1. Overview: What Are Figma and Sketch?

Figma is a cloud-based, browser-first design tool that has taken the design world by storm since its launch in 2016. It enables real-time collaboration, version history, and seamless sharing without any local installation (though a desktop app is available). Figma works on Windows, macOS, and Linux.

Sketch has been a veteran in the design space since 2010. It's a macOS-exclusive native app known for its lightning-fast performance, powerful vector editing, and robust plugin ecosystem. Sketch introduced a web-based workspace in recent years, but its core remains a desktop application.

2. Core Comparison: Figma vs Sketch

Platform & Accessibility

  • Figma: Cross-platform (Windows, macOS, Linux, browser, mobile). No installation required for basic use. Perfect for teams with mixed operating systems.
  • Sketch: macOS only. Requires a Mac or Hackintosh. Web viewer available for stakeholders, but editing is Mac-bound.

Winner: Figma – Its universal accessibility is a game-changer for distributed teams.

Real-Time Collaboration

  • Figma: Built for multiplayer editing. Multiple designers can work on the same file simultaneously, see each other's cursors, and leave comments. Changes sync instantly.
  • Sketch: Collaboration requires additional tools like Sketch Cloud or third-party plugins (e.g., Abstract, Plant). Real-time co-editing is not native—it's more of a "handoff" model.

Winner: Figma – Sketch has improved with web-based sharing, but Figma's real-time collaboration is still unmatched.

Performance & Offline Use

  • Figma: Runs in the browser, so performance depends on internet speed and browser optimization. It can lag on large, complex files. Offline mode is limited (you can work offline, but features are reduced).
  • Sketch: Native macOS app with near-instant responsiveness, even on massive files (e.g., 100+ artboards). Fully functional offline—no internet required for editing.

Winner: Sketch – For power users who need speed and offline reliability, Sketch remains the performance king.

Pricing & Licensing

  • Figma: Free tier (3 projects, unlimited editors). Paid plans start at $12/month (Professional) or $45/month (Organization). Education and startup discounts available.
  • Sketch: One-time license fee of $99/year (includes updates and Cloud). No free tier, but a 30-day trial is available. Sketch also offers a Free Viewer web app.

Winner: Figma for small teams and freelancers (free tier). Sketch can be cheaper long-term for individual designers who don't need cloud features.

Plugin & Asset Ecosystem

  • Figma: Growing community with 1,000+ plugins and widgets. Strong integration with tools like Jira, Zeplin, and Notion. However, some advanced plugins (e.g., for data visualization) are still maturing.
  • Sketch: Mature ecosystem with 900+ plugins, many specifically for prototyping, icon design, and developer handoff. Sketch's plugin library is deeper in certain niches (e.g., symbol management, animation).

Winner: Sketch (slightly) – It has a longer history and more specialized plugins, though Figma is catching up fast.

Prototyping & Handoff

  • Figma: Built-in prototyping (transitions, overlays, scrollable areas) and developer handoff (inspect mode, CSS/Android/iOS code export). No need for third-party tools.
  • Sketch: Prototyping requires plugins like Anima or ProtoPie. Handoff tools like Zeplin or Avocode are commonly used. Native prototyping is basic.

Winner: Figma – For a seamless all-in-one workflow, Figma's integrated prototyping and handoff are more efficient.

Version History & Branching

  • Figma: Automatic version history (30 days on free plan, unlimited on paid). Branching and merging are available but not as sophisticated as code-based tools.
  • Sketch: Version history requires Sketch Cloud or third-party tools (e.g., Abstract). Native branching is not built-in.

Winner: Figma – Cloud-native versioning is simpler and more reliable for most teams.

3. Practical Tips & Actionable Advice

When to Choose Figma

  • You work on a cross-platform team (Windows + Mac).
  • You need real-time collaboration with stakeholders or other designers.
  • You prefer an all-in-one tool for design, prototyping, and handoff.
  • You have a tight budget and can leverage the free tier.
  • You frequently share designs with clients who need browser access.

When to Choose Sketch

  • You're a solo designer or small team on macOS only.
  • You work offline frequently or on large, complex files.
  • You rely on specialized plugins (e.g., for icon design, data visualization).
  • You prefer a one-time payment model over subscription.
  • You need maximum performance for high-fidelity prototypes.

Hybrid Approach

Many agencies use both tools: Sketch for heavy-duty design work and Figma for client collaboration and feedback. You can export from Sketch to Figma using plugins like "Sketch to Figma" or simply copy-paste layers. This gives you the best of both worlds.

4. Conclusion: Which Tool Should You Choose?

There is no universal "best" tool—only the best for your specific context. Figma excels in collaboration, accessibility, and integrated workflows, making it ideal for modern, distributed teams and startups. Sketch remains a powerhouse for macOS users who demand raw performance, offline reliability, and a mature plugin ecosystem.

Call to Action: Still undecided? Try both for 30 days. Start with Figma's free tier to test collaboration, and download Sketch's free trial to benchmark performance.
