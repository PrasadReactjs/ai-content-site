The 7 Best CRM for Real Estate in 2025: Pricing, Features & Honest Reviews

In today's fast-paced property market, having the right real estate software can be the difference between closing 20 deals a year and closing 50. Whether you're a solo agent, a boutique brokerage, or a growing team, a Customer Relationship Management (CRM) system is no longer a luxury—it's a necessity. But with dozens of options flooding the market, how do you choose the best CRM for real estate that actually fits your workflow and budget?

We've tested, compared, and ranked the top contenders based on pricing, features, ease of use, and real-world feedback. In this comprehensive guide, we'll break down the pros and cons of each platform, share practical tips for implementation, and help you pick the perfect real estate software for your business.

Why You Need a Specialized Real Estate CRM

Generic CRMs like Salesforce or HubSpot are powerful, but they weren't built for the unique demands of real estate. A dedicated real estate software solution offers features tailored to agents: automated property matching, IDX integration, drip campaigns for lead nurturing, and commission tracking. Without these, you're wasting hours manually entering data and missing out on follow-ups.

According to the National Association of Realtors, 44% of buyers found their agent through a referral. A CRM helps you nurture those relationships systematically. The right system will automate your marketing, organize your contacts, and even predict which leads are ready to buy—so you can focus on showing homes and closing deals.

Top 7 CRM for Real Estate: Detailed Reviews

1. Follow Up Boss – Best for Teams & Lead Response

Pricing: Starts at $69/month (single user), $299/month (team of 3).

Key Features: Smart lead routing, two-way texting, call tracking, Zapier integrations, and a powerful mobile app.

  • Pros: Lightning-fast lead distribution; excellent for high-volume teams; integrates with 50+ lead sources (Zillow, Realtor.com, etc.).
  • Cons: No built-in website or IDX; limited marketing automation compared to others.

Best For: Teams that generate leads from multiple sources and need instant follow-up. If speed is your priority, this real estate software delivers.

2. kvCORE – Best All-in-One Platform

Pricing: Starts at $199/month (includes website, IDX, and CRM).

Key Features: AI-powered lead scoring, automated nurture campaigns, custom website builder, and a full marketing suite.

  • Pros: Everything in one place—no need for separate tools; robust automation; excellent for brand-building.
  • Cons: Expensive for solo agents; learning curve for beginners.

Best For: Agents who want a complete ecosystem—website, CRM, and marketing—under one roof.

3. BoomTown – Best for Lead Generation

Pricing: Custom quotes only (typically $300–$800/month).

Key Features: Built-in lead generation (Facebook ads, landing pages), automated workflows, and a mobile app.

  • Pros: Comprehensive lead gen tools; high-converting templates; dedicated support.
  • Cons: Expensive; long setup time; contracts required.

Best For: Growth-focused teams willing to invest heavily in lead generation.

4. Real Geeks – Best Budget Option for Solo Agents

Pricing: Starts at $99/month (includes website and IDX).

Key Features: IDX website, lead capture, automated email campaigns, and a simple CRM.

  • Pros: Affordable; easy to set up; includes a professional website.
  • Cons: Limited integrations; basic reporting; no advanced automation.

Best For: New agents or those on a tight budget who need a solid starter real estate software.

5. LionDesk – Best for Video & Text Marketing

Pricing: Starts at $39/month (single user), $99/month (team).

Key Features: Video messaging, two-way texting, drip campaigns, and a transaction management module.

  • Pros: Affordable; excellent video features; strong mobile app.
  • Cons: Outdated user interface; limited lead gen tools.

Best For: Agents who prioritize personalized video follow-ups and text communication.

6. Wise Agent – Best for Transaction Management

Pricing: Starts at $29/month (single user).

Key Features: Transaction pipeline, document storage, e-signatures, and contact management.

  • Pros: Very affordable; strong transaction tools; good for compliance.
  • Cons: Limited marketing automation; basic reporting.

Best For: Agents who need a simple, low-cost way to manage deals and contacts.

7. Pipedrive – Best for Sales Pipeline Management

Pricing: Starts at $14.90/month (Essential plan).

Key Features: Visual pipeline, deal tracking, email sync, and AI-powered sales assistant.

  • Pros: Intuitive interface; highly customizable; affordable for startups.
  • Cons: Not real-estate-specific (no IDX, no property tracking); limited automation.

Best For: Agents who want a clean, visual sales pipeline and don't need real estate-specific features.

How to Choose the Right Real Estate Software for You

With so many options, picking the best CRM for real estate comes down to three key factors:

1. Your Budget

If you're a solo agent just starting out, LionDesk ($39/month) or Wise Agent ($29/month) offer excellent value. For teams, Follow Up Boss ($299/month) is a proven winner. If you have a larger budget and want an all-in-one solution, kvCORE ($199+/month) is hard to beat.

2. Your Lead Sources

Do you get most of your leads from Zillow, Realtor.com, or Facebook? Follow Up Boss integrates with 50+ lead sources and routes them instantly. If you prefer to generate leads in-house, BoomTown or kvCORE have built-in ad tools.

3. Your Workflow

Do you need a simple contact manager, or a full marketing automation suite? If you're a hands-on agent who loves video, LionDesk is a gem. If you're a data-driven team that wants AI predictions, kvCORE shines.

Practical Tips for Implementing Your CRM

  • Start with data cleanup: Before migrating, remove duplicates and outdated contacts. Garbage in = garbage out.
  • Set up automation immediately: Create a welcome email sequence for new leads. Even a 3-email drip can increase response rates by 40%.
  • Use tags and custom fields: Tag leads by source (e.g., "Zillow," "Open House"), status (e.g., "Hot," "Nurture"), and property type. This makes segmentation easy.
  • Integrate your calendar: Sync your CRM with Google Calendar or Outlook to automatically log calls and meetings.
  • Train your team: The best real estate software is useless if no one uses it. Schedule weekly check-ins for the first month.
