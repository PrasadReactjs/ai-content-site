The Best CRM for Real Estate: Top Real Estate Software to Boost Your Sales in 2024

In the fast-paced world of real estate, managing leads, nurturing client relationships, and closing deals efficiently can make or break your business. That’s where the right real estate software comes in. A powerful Customer Relationship Management (CRM) tool tailored for agents, brokers, and teams can automate follow-ups, organize contacts, and provide valuable insights—saving you hours each week.

But with dozens of options on the market, how do you choose the best CRM for real estate? We’ve researched, tested, and compared the top platforms to help you find the perfect fit. In this comprehensive guide, we break down pricing, features, pros/cons, and give you actionable recommendations—all optimized for your success.

Why You Need a Dedicated Real Estate CRM

Generic CRMs like Salesforce or HubSpot are powerful, but they often lack the specific workflows real estate professionals need—like property tracking, MLS integration, and automated drip campaigns for home buyers and sellers. A purpose-built real estate software solution streamlines your entire pipeline, from first contact to closing.

Key benefits include:

  • Lead management: Capture leads from your website, Zillow, Realtor.com, and social media in one place.
  • Automated follow-ups: Set email and text sequences to stay top-of-mind without manual effort.
  • Transaction management: Track offers, contracts, and deadlines in one dashboard.
  • Reporting & analytics: See which sources generate the most leads and conversions.
  • Integration: Connect with MLS, IDX, and your favorite tools.

Top 5 Best CRM for Real Estate in 2024

1. Follow Up Boss – Best for Lead Routing and Speed

Best for: Real estate teams and brokerages that need fast lead distribution and multi-channel communication.

Pricing: Starts at $31/month for 1 user (Starter plan); Team plan at $83/user/month; Enterprise custom pricing.

Key Features:

  • Real-time lead routing (round-robin or rules-based)
  • Two-way text, email, and call logging
  • Smart lists and automated workflows
  • Integration with over 100 lead sources (Zillow, Realtor.com, Facebook, etc.)
  • Mobile app with full functionality

Pros:

  • Lightning-fast lead assignment—ideal for high-volume teams
  • Excellent mobile experience
  • Simple, intuitive interface

Cons:

  • Limited built-in marketing automation (no full email campaigns)
  • No integrated transaction management
  • Can get pricey for larger teams

2. LionDesk – Best All-in-One Value

Best for: Solo agents and small teams wanting marketing automation, video, and CRM in one affordable package.

Pricing: $25/month for 1 user; $49/month for up to 3 users; $99/month for 10 users.

Key Features:

  • Drip email and text campaigns
  • Video email and text (record and send directly)
  • Automated property alerts for buyers
  • Transaction pipeline with checklists
  • Integration with MLS, Zillow, and 100+ apps

Pros:

  • Very affordable for the feature set
  • Built-in video messaging (unique differentiator)
  • Great for nurturing long-term leads

Cons:

  • Interface can feel dated
  • Reporting is basic compared to premium tools
  • No advanced lead routing for teams

3. Wise Agent – Best for Transaction Management

Best for: Agents who want a CRM with robust deal management and document storage.

Pricing: $29/month for 1 user; $49/month for 3 users; $99/month for 10 users.

Key Features:

  • Full transaction pipeline with milestones and documents
  • Email marketing with templates and auto-responders
  • Contact management with custom fields
  • IDX website integration
  • Mobile app for iOS and Android

Pros:

  • Excellent transaction management—track every step from offer to close
  • Unlimited storage for documents and contracts
  • Affordable for small teams

Cons:

  • Email marketing features are less sophisticated than dedicated tools
  • No video messaging
  • UI could be more modern

4. Real Geeks – Best for Lead Generation and IDX

Best for: Agents who need a CRM tightly integrated with a powerful IDX website to generate leads.

Pricing: $299/month (includes IDX website, CRM, and lead generation tools).

Key Features:

  • Built-in IDX website with property search
  • Automated lead capture and follow-up (email + text)
  • Smart lead scoring and behavior tracking
  • Facebook lead ad integration
  • Drip campaigns for buyers and sellers

Pros:

  • All-in-one website + CRM solution
  • Excellent lead capture (IDX search, landing pages)
  • Automated follow-up sequences save time

Cons:

  • Higher price point (but includes website)
  • Less flexible for agents who already have a website
  • Limited customization for advanced workflows

5. Salesforce Essentials (for Real Estate) – Best for Scalability

Best for: Large brokerages and teams that need enterprise-grade customization and reporting.

Pricing: $25/user/month (Essentials); Enterprise starts at $150/user/month. Real Estate add-ons cost extra.

Key Features:

  • Highly customizable fields, pipelines, and automations
  • Advanced reporting and dashboards
  • AI-powered lead scoring (Einstein)
  • Integration with thousands of apps (via AppExchange)
  • Mobile app with full CRM capabilities

Pros:

  • Unmatched customization and scalability
  • Powerful analytics for data-driven decisions
  • Trusted by large enterprises worldwide

Cons:

  • Steep learning curve—requires setup and training
  • Expensive when adding real estate-specific features
  • No built-in IDX or property management

How to Choose the Best Real Estate Software for Your Needs

Selecting the right real estate software isn’t just about features—it’s about fit. Here’s a practical checklist to guide your decision:

1. Define Your Budget

CRM pricing ranges from $25/month (LionDesk) to $300+/month (Real Geeks). Factor in your team size and whether you need a website included. Solo agents often find great value in LionDesk or Wise Agent, while teams may prefer Follow Up Boss or Real Geeks.

2. Consider Your Lead Volume

If you get 50+ leads per day, you need a CRM with automated lead routing (like Follow Up Boss). If you get 5-10 leads per week, a simpler tool like Wise Agent works fine.

3. Evaluate Integration Needs

Check if the CRM integrates with your MLS, IDX provider, website, and tools like Zap
