The Best Real Estate Software in 2025: Top CRM Solutions Compared

In the fast-paced world of real estate, managing leads, nurturing client relationships, and closing deals efficiently can make or break your business. The right real estate software doesn't just organize your contacts—it automates follow-ups, tracks transactions, and helps you scale your operations. Whether you're a solo agent, a growing team, or a brokerage, choosing the best CRM is critical.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll compare the top CRM platforms for real estate professionals, break down their pricing, features, pros/cons, and give you actionable recommendations. By the end, you'll know exactly which real estate software fits your needs and budget.

Why You Need a Real Estate CRM

A generic CRM won't cut it in real estate. You need a system designed for the unique workflows of buying, selling, and renting properties. The best real estate software helps you:

  • Automate lead capture from Zillow, Realtor.com, and your website
  • Track property searches and client preferences
  • Manage transaction documents and deadlines
  • Send personalized drip email campaigns
  • Integrate with MLS, IDX, and marketing tools

Without a purpose-built CRM, you risk losing leads, missing follow-ups, and wasting time on manual tasks.

Top 5 CRM Software for Real Estate in 2025

1. Salesforce for Real Estate

Best for: Large brokerages and teams needing enterprise-level customization.

Salesforce is a powerhouse in the CRM world, and its real estate software edition offers unparalleled flexibility. With the Financial Services Cloud (adaptable for real estate), you can manage complex deal structures and compliance.

  • Pricing: Starts at $80/user/month (Essentials) to $300+/user/month (Unlimited). Add-ons and customizations cost extra.
  • Key Features: AI-powered lead scoring (Einstein), automated workflows, custom dashboards, MLS integration via third-party apps, and robust mobile app.
  • Pros: Highly customizable; vast app marketplace; strong reporting; enterprise-grade security.
  • Cons: Steep learning curve; expensive for small teams; requires dedicated admin or consultant.

Recommendation: Choose Salesforce if you have a large team (10+ agents) and budget for setup and training. It's overkill for solo agents.

2. HubSpot CRM (Free & Paid)

Best for: Solo agents and small teams who want a free starter option and easy marketing automation.

HubSpot's free real estate software tier is surprisingly powerful. It includes contact management, deal tracking, and email integration. Paid plans add automation and advanced reporting.

  • Pricing: Free tier (limited); Starter at $20/month; Professional at $100/month; Enterprise at $150/month. Marketing Hub sold separately.
  • Key Features: Drag-and-drop email builder, meeting scheduler, live chat, pipeline management, and 1,000+ integrations.
  • Pros: Generous free plan; intuitive interface; excellent marketing tools; strong support community.
  • Cons: Limited real estate-specific features (no built-in MLS/IDX); paid tiers get expensive quickly; automation requires add-ons.

Recommendation: Ideal for agents just starting out or those who prioritize marketing automation over property-specific tools. Pair it with an IDX plugin.

3. Follow Up Boss

Best for: Teams focused on lead response speed and multi-channel communication.

Follow Up Boss is built from the ground up for real estate teams. It's known for its "speed to lead" features and seamless integration with lead generation sources.

  • Pricing: Starts at $69/month for 1 user; $129/month for 2-3 users; $199/month for 4-5 users; custom pricing for larger teams.
  • Key Features: Centralized lead inbox, automated assignment rules, call tracking, text messaging, and integration with 50+ lead sources (Zillow, Realtor.com, etc.).
  • Pros: Excellent lead routing; fast setup; great for team collaboration; strong mobile app.
  • Cons: Limited marketing automation (no email builder); no built-in transaction management; can be pricey per user.

Recommendation: Perfect for teams that generate high lead volume and need instant response. Combine with a separate marketing tool for drip campaigns.

4. LionDesk

Best for: Budget-conscious agents and small teams wanting all-in-one features.

LionDesk is an affordable real estate software that bundles CRM, email marketing, video messaging, and transaction management in one platform.

  • Pricing: $25/month for 1 user; $50/month for 2-3 users; $100/month for 4-10 users; enterprise plans available.
  • Key Features: Drip campaigns, video email, landing pages, transaction pipeline, e-signatures (via DocuSign integration), and lead generation forms.
  • Pros: Very affordable; includes video marketing; good transaction management; easy to learn.
  • Cons: Limited integrations compared to competitors; basic reporting; mobile app can be glitchy.

Recommendation: A solid choice for agents who want an all-in-one solution without breaking the bank. Great for video marketing enthusiasts.

5. Propertybase

Best for: Brokerages and teams needing a full-fledged system with IDX websites and MLS integration.

Propertybase is a purpose-built real estate software that combines CRM, IDX website, and transaction management in one platform. It's built on Salesforce, giving it enterprise-level power.

  • Pricing: Starts at $99/user/month for the CRM; $199/user/month for the full suite (includes IDX website). Setup fees apply.
  • Key Features: Built-in IDX website, MLS data sync, property search on site, automated listings to social media, and transaction management.
  • Pros: Deep real estate functionality; includes website; strong MLS integration; scalable.
  • Cons: Expensive; requires training; website customization can be limited; not ideal for solo agents.

Recommendation: Best for brokerages wanting an all-in-one solution including a website. Consider if you need MLS-integrated property search.

Pricing Comparison Table

Here's a quick overview of starting prices (per user, per month):

  • Salesforce: $80+
  • HubSpot: Free – $20+
  • Follow Up Boss: $69+
  • LionDesk: $25+
  • Propertybase: $99+

Remember that many platforms charge extra for add-ons like SMS, advanced automation, or additional users. Always check the final cost before committing.

How to Choose the Right Real Estate Software

Follow these practical steps to select the best real estate software for your business:

1. Define Your Needs

List your top 3 pain points. Is it lead management? Follow-up automation? Transaction tracking? Choose a CRM that solves your biggest problem first.

2. Check Integrations

Ensure the CRM integrates with your existing tools: MLS, IDX provider, email platform (Gmail/Outlook), e-signature (DocuSign), and lead sources (Zillow, Realtor.com).

3. Test the User Interface

Most platforms offer free trials. Test with real data. Is it intuitive? Can your team adopt it quickly? A complex CRM that nobody uses is wasted money.
