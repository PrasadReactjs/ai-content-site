Best CRM for Real Estate in 2025: Top Picks, Pricing & Features

In the fast-paced world of property sales and rentals, staying organized is everything. Whether you’re a solo agent, a growing team, or a large brokerage, the right real estate software can mean the difference between a chaotic inbox and a seamless closing process. A dedicated Customer Relationship Management (CRM) tool helps you track leads, automate follow-ups, manage transactions, and nurture client relationships for years to come.

But with dozens of options on the market, how do you choose the best one for your business? This comprehensive guide breaks down the top CRMs for real estate professionals, covering pricing, key features, pros and cons, and actionable recommendations. By the end, you’ll know exactly which platform fits your workflow and budget.

Why Real Estate Professionals Need Specialized CRM Software

Generic CRMs like Salesforce or HubSpot can work, but they often lack the specific tools that real estate agents rely on daily. A purpose-built real estate software solution includes features like:

  • Property tracking – Manage listings, showings, and property details directly within the CRM.
  • Automated drip campaigns – Send personalized emails to leads based on their search behavior.
  • Transaction management – Track contracts, deadlines, and commission splits.
  • IDX integration – Sync your website’s property search with your CRM.
  • Smart lead routing – Assign leads to the right agent or team member instantly.

Using the right real estate software can increase your conversion rates by 30% or more, simply by ensuring no lead falls through the cracks.

Top 5 Best CRM for Real Estate in 2025

We’ve evaluated the leading platforms based on features, ease of use, pricing, and real-world feedback from agents. Here are our top recommendations.

1. Follow Up Boss

Best for: Teams and brokerages that prioritize lead management and speed-to-lead.

Follow Up Boss is widely considered the gold standard for lead routing and response management. It integrates with over 50 lead sources (Zillow, Realtor.com, Facebook, etc.) and ensures every inquiry gets an immediate response.

  • Pricing: Starts at $69/month for 1 user; team plans from $299/month.
  • Key Features: Smart lead distribution, call tracking, automated texting, performance dashboards, and Zapier integrations.
  • Pros: Lightning-fast lead assignment, excellent mobile app, robust reporting.
  • Cons: Limited built-in transaction management; higher cost for larger teams.

Recommendation: If you’re a team lead or broker who needs to ensure every lead is contacted within 5 minutes, Follow Up Boss is your best bet. Pair it with a transaction management tool like Dotloop for a complete workflow.

2. LionDesk

Best for: Solo agents and small teams looking for an all-in-one, affordable solution.

LionDesk offers a robust feature set at a competitive price point. It includes video email, automated SMS campaigns, and a built-in dialer—all in one platform.

  • Pricing: Starts at $39/month for 1 user; team plans from $59/user/month.
  • Key Features: Two-way texting, video email, lead generation forms, pipeline management, and AI-powered coaching (LionDesk IQ).
  • Pros: Affordable, easy to set up, excellent customer support.
  • Cons: Limited third-party integrations compared to competitors; reporting could be more advanced.

Recommendation: LionDesk is perfect for agents who want a simple, powerful CRM without breaking the bank. The video email feature is a standout for building personal connections with leads.

3. kvCORE

Best for: Agents and teams that want an all-in-one platform with a website and marketing automation.

kvCORE is more than a CRM—it’s a complete marketing and sales platform. It includes a built-in IDX website, automated nurture campaigns, and a powerful AI engine that scores leads based on buying intent.

  • Pricing: Starts around $199/month for a single agent; custom pricing for teams.
  • Key Features: AI lead scoring, automated social media posting, property search on your site, transaction management, and broker oversight tools.
  • Pros: All-in-one solution, beautiful website templates, intelligent lead prioritization.
  • Cons: Higher price point; can be overwhelming for beginners.

Recommendation: If you’re willing to invest in a premium real estate software that handles everything from lead generation to closing, kvCORE is a powerhouse. It’s especially valuable for agents who want to automate their marketing and focus on high-intent buyers.

4. Real Geeks

Best for: Agents who want a CRM tightly integrated with a lead-generating website.

Real Geeks combines a powerful IDX website with a CRM designed to capture and convert online leads. It’s built around the idea that your website should be your top lead source.

  • Pricing: Starts at $199/month (includes website and CRM).
  • Key Features: IDX property search, automated email campaigns, lead alerts, and a mobile app.
  • Pros: Excellent website-to-CRM integration, easy to use, strong community support.
  • Cons: Limited customization for the website; no built-in transaction management.

Recommendation: Real Geeks is ideal for agents who rely heavily on their website for leads. The seamless integration means you never miss a visitor’s activity—every search, save, and inquiry is captured automatically.

5. BoomTown

Best for: Growth-minded teams and brokerages focused on aggressive lead generation.

BoomTown is a premium platform that includes a full suite of marketing tools, from paid ad management to automated follow-up sequences. It’s designed to help teams scale quickly.

  • Pricing: Custom pricing (typically $300–$500+/month).
  • Key Features: Predictive analytics, Facebook and Google ad integration, smart lists, and a team dashboard.
  • Pros: Powerful lead generation, advanced automation, dedicated onboarding support.
  • Cons: Expensive; steep learning curve; long-term contract required.

Recommendation: BoomTown is best for established teams with a budget for aggressive growth. If you’re ready to invest heavily in paid advertising and need a CRM that can keep up, BoomTown delivers.

How to Choose the Right Real Estate Software for Your Business

With so many options, here’s a step-by-step process to find your perfect match:

Step 1: Define Your Needs

  • Solo agent? Focus on affordability and ease of use (e.g., LionDesk).
  • Team lead? Prioritize lead routing and team performance tracking (e.g., Follow Up Boss).
  • Brokerage owner? Look for broker oversight and transaction management (e.g., kvCORE).

Step 2: Check Integrations

Ensure the CRM integrates with your existing tools—your website, MLS, email provider, and calendar. Most top CRMs offer Zapier connections for custom workflows.

Step 3: Test the User Experience

Every CRM has a learning curve. Take advantage of free trials (usually 7–14 days) to see if the interface feels intuitive. Ask your team for feedback if you’re buying for a group.

Step 4: Calculate Total Cost

Consider not just the monthly fee, but also setup costs, training, and any add-ons (like SMS credits or extra storage). A $39/month CRM might cost more if you need premium features.
