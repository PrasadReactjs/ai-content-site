Curated Job Listings for Climate Tech Positions: Your Gateway to a Sustainable Career

The global push toward net-zero emissions, renewable energy, and sustainable innovation has created a booming job market: climate tech positions. As of 2025, climate technology—encompassing everything from carbon accounting software to green hydrogen infrastructure—is one of the fastest-growing sectors in the world. For job seekers, this translates into a wealth of opportunities that combine purpose with professional growth. But with so many roles emerging, how do you find the right one? This curated guide will help you navigate the landscape of climate tech jobs, offering practical tips, key platforms, and actionable advice to land your dream role.

What Are Climate Tech Positions?

Climate tech positions refer to jobs within companies that develop or deploy technologies aimed at mitigating or adapting to climate change. Unlike the broader "green jobs" category (which includes traditional roles like solar panel installation), climate tech is heavily innovation-driven. It includes software engineers building carbon tracking platforms, data scientists modeling climate risks, hardware engineers designing next-generation batteries, and policy experts shaping carbon markets.

Key sub-sectors within climate tech include:

  • Renewable Energy – Solar, wind, geothermal, and advanced nuclear technologies.
  • Carbon Management – Direct air capture, carbon credits, and emissions monitoring.
  • Electric Mobility – EV infrastructure, battery recycling, and autonomous electric fleets.
  • AgriTech & Food Systems – Precision agriculture, alternative proteins, and food waste reduction.
  • Built Environment – Smart grids, green building materials, and energy-efficient HVAC systems.
  • Climate Analytics & Finance – ESG data platforms, climate risk modeling, and green investment tools.

Why Climate Tech Is a Smart Career Move

The demand for climate tech talent is soaring. According to recent industry reports, climate tech startups raised over $40 billion globally in 2024, and venture capital continues to flow into areas like carbon removal and sustainable aviation fuel. This growth translates into high job security, competitive salaries, and the chance to work on some of the most pressing challenges of our time. Moreover, many climate tech companies offer remote or hybrid work options, aligning with the values of a workforce that prioritizes flexibility and impact.

For professionals transitioning from other industries (e.g., software, finance, or manufacturing), climate tech offers a meaningful pivot without starting from scratch. Your existing skills in project management, data analysis, or engineering are highly transferable—you just need to apply them to a climate context.

Top Platforms for Curating Climate Tech Job Listings

To find the best climate tech positions, you need to know where to look. General job boards like LinkedIn or Indeed can be noisy, so niche platforms are often more effective. Here are the top curated sources:

1. Climate Tech-Specific Job Boards

  • Climatebase – The leading platform for climate tech jobs, with thousands of listings from startups to Fortune 500 companies. Features include company impact ratings and skill-based filters.
  • Work on Climate – A community-driven board that emphasizes transparency and mission alignment. Many roles include salary ranges and remote options.
  • Greentown Labs Job Board – Focused on hard-tech startups in energy and materials, often featuring early-stage roles.
  • Clean Energy Trust – Curates positions in the Midwest U.S., with a focus on clean energy innovation.

2. General Boards with Climate Filters

  • LinkedIn – Use the "Climate" or "Sustainability" filter under industries. Also follow companies like Tesla, Ørsted, and Climeworks for direct updates.
  • AngelList / Wellfound – Excellent for startup roles; search for keywords like "carbon," "renewable," or "climate."
  • Indeed – Set up alerts for "climate tech" or "clean tech" to receive daily curated listings.

3. Community & Newsletter-Based Curation

  • Climate Tech Weekly (by Climatebase) – A newsletter that highlights top new positions every week.
  • Climate People – A recruitment agency that matches candidates with vetted climate tech companies.
  • My Climate Journey – A podcast and community that shares job openings in their Slack channel.

Practical Tips for Applying to Climate Tech Positions

Securing a role in climate tech requires more than just a generic resume. Here are actionable strategies to stand out:

1. Tailor Your Resume to Climate Impact

Highlight projects or experiences that demonstrate your commitment to sustainability. For example, if you’re a software engineer, mention a side project where you built a carbon footprint calculator. Use metrics: "Reduced energy consumption by 15% in a previous role" or "Led a team that deployed 50 EV charging stations."

2. Learn the Lingo

Climate tech has its own vocabulary. Familiarize yourself with terms like "scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions," "additionality," "levelized cost of energy (LCOE)," and "carbon offset integrity." This shows you’re not just a generalist but someone who understands the sector’s nuances.

3. Network Strategically

Join climate tech communities on Slack (e.g., Climate Tech VC, Work on Climate) and attend virtual events like Climate Week or TechCrunch Sessions: Climate. Many jobs are filled through referrals, so building relationships is key. Don’t be afraid to send a polite LinkedIn message to a hiring manager expressing your interest in their company’s mission.

4. Showcase Transferable Skills

If you’re coming from a non-climate background, frame your experience in terms of problem-solving. For instance, a project manager in construction can pivot to green building roles by emphasizing expertise in LEED certifications or energy-efficient retrofits. A data analyst can highlight experience with environmental datasets or ESG reporting.

5. Prepare for Technical Interviews

Some climate tech roles require domain-specific knowledge. For example, a battery engineer might need to understand lithium-ion chemistry, while a policy analyst should know about the Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) or EU Green Deal. Use free resources like ClimateTech 101 courses from MIT or Coursera to bridge gaps.

Actionable Advice: How to Curate Your Own Job Search

Beyond using curated boards, you can create a personalized system to stay ahead. Here’s how:

  • Set Google Alerts – For keywords like "climate tech hiring" or "sustainability engineer jobs." This pulls in listings from lesser-known sources.
  • Follow Investors – Venture capital firms like Lowercarbon Capital, Breakthrough Energy Ventures, and Planet A Ventures often share portfolio company openings on their blogs or Twitter.
  • Use Boolean Search – On LinkedIn or Indeed, combine terms: ("climate tech" OR "clean tech" OR "carbon removal") AND ("engineer" OR "analyst" OR "manager"). This narrows results to high-relevance roles.
  • Track Company News – Sign up for newsletters from companies like Climeworks, Northvolt, or Redwood Materials. They often post jobs before they hit major boards.

Common Challenges and How to Overcome Them

The climate tech job market isn’t without hurdles. Here’s how to address them:

  • Overwhelming competition – Many roles attract hundreds of applicants. Differentiate yourself by completing a climate-specific certification (e.g., Carbon Trust or GRI).
  • Geographic constraints – If you’re not in a hub like San Francisco, London, or Berlin, focus on remote-first companies. Platforms like Remote Climate Jobs can help.
  • Lack of experience – Consider internships, fellowships (e.g., Climatebase Fellowship), or volunteer projects with non-profits like Environmental Defense Fund to build a portfolio.
