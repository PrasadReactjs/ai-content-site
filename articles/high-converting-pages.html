Best Landing Page Examples: 7 High Converting Pages That Drive Sales

In the digital marketing world, a landing page is often the make-or-break point between a casual browser and a paying customer. The difference between a mediocre page and a high converting page can be thousands of dollars in lost or gained revenue. Whether you are launching a new product, building an email list, or promoting an affiliate offer, the structure and psychology of your landing page matter immensely.

In this guide, we analyze seven of the best landing page examples across different industries. We break down their pricing (where applicable), core features, pros and cons, and provide actionable recommendations so you can apply these principles to your own high converting pages. By the end, you'll have a blueprint for creating pages that turn visitors into loyal customers.

1. Unbounce – The Godfather of Landing Page Builders

Unbounce is one of the most well-known platforms for creating high converting pages without needing a developer. Their own landing pages are a masterclass in clarity and conversion optimization.

Pricing

  • Launch Plan: $99/month (up to 500 visitors)
  • Optimize Plan: $145/month (up to 1,000 visitors)
  • Accelerate Plan: $195/month (up to 2,500 visitors)
  • Scale Plan: Custom pricing for high-volume needs

Key Features

  • Drag-and-drop builder with 100+ templates
  • Smart Traffic AI (automatically sends visitors to the best variant)
  • A/B testing and analytics built-in
  • Integrates with major email marketing and CRM tools
  • Dynamic text replacement for PPC campaigns

Pros

  • Extremely user-friendly, even for beginners
  • Excellent template library designed for conversions
  • AI-driven optimization saves time on manual testing

Cons

  • Higher price point compared to some competitors
  • Some advanced customizations require CSS knowledge

Recommendation

Unbounce is ideal for serious marketers and small-to-medium businesses that want a reliable, professional tool. If you run multiple ad campaigns and need a high converting page for each, Unbounce’s Smart Traffic feature alone can boost your conversion rate by 20–30%.

2. Leadpages – Best for Lead Generation on a Budget

Leadpages is a close competitor to Unbounce but often appeals to entrepreneurs and solopreneurs who need a high converting page without the premium price tag.

Pricing

  • Standard Plan: $49/month (up to 500 visitors)
  • Pro Plan: $99/month (up to 1,000 visitors)
  • Advanced Plan: $199/month (up to 3,000 visitors)

Key Features

  • Over 200 conversion-optimized templates
  • Lead generation forms with built-in analytics
  • Facebook and Instagram ad integration
  • Pop-up and alert bar builder for additional conversions
  • Simple checkout for selling products or services

Pros

  • Affordable entry point for new businesses
  • Quick setup – can launch a page in under 30 minutes
  • Excellent for simple lead capture and list building

Cons

  • Less flexible design options compared to Unbounce
  • A/B testing only available on higher plans

Recommendation

If your primary goal is list building or promoting a low-cost offer, Leadpages delivers exceptional value. The templates are designed specifically for high converting pages in the lead generation space.

3. ClickFunnels – The All-in-One Sales Funnel Builder

ClickFunnels is not just a landing page builder; it’s a complete sales funnel platform. Their own landing pages are legendary in the marketing world for driving massive revenue.

Pricing

  • Basic Plan: $147/month (up to 20 funnels, 100 pages)
  • Pro Plan: $197/month (unlimited funnels and pages)
  • Funnel Hacker Plan: $297/month (includes advanced features)

Key Features

  • Funnel building, not just single pages
  • Order bumps, upsells, and downsells built-in
  • Email autoresponder (Actionetics) included
  • Membership site and webinar integration
  • One-click upsells for higher average order value

Pros

  • All-in-one solution – no third-party integrations needed
  • Designed for high-ticket sales and complex funnels
  • Strong community and training resources

Cons

  • Steep learning curve for complete beginners
  • Can be overkill for simple landing pages

Recommendation

ClickFunnels is the best choice if you are selling high-ticket products or services and want a complete system. Their funnels are textbook examples of high converting pages that guide visitors from curiosity to purchase.

4. Instapage – Enterprise-Grade Conversion Optimization

Instapage is designed for teams and agencies that need advanced collaboration features and deep analytics for high converting pages.

Pricing

  • Core Plan: $199/month (up to 5,000 visitors)
  • Convert Plan: $299/month (up to 10,000 visitors)
  • Enterprise Plan: Custom pricing

Key Features

  • Heatmaps and session recording for user behavior analysis
  • Collaboration tools with real-time editing and commenting
  • AMP (Accelerated Mobile Pages) support
  • AdMap – visual planning of ad-to-page alignment
  • Advanced A/B testing with statistical significance

Pros

  • Best-in-class analytics and optimization features
  • Excellent for large teams and agencies
  • Mobile-first design ensures fast loading on all devices

Cons

  • Higher price point may not suit small businesses
  • Overwhelming number of features for solo marketers

Recommendation

If you have a dedicated marketing team and run high-volume ad campaigns, Instapage’s analytics and collaboration tools make it a powerhouse for creating high converting pages that are data-driven.

5. HubSpot Landing Pages – Free and Powerful

HubSpot offers a free landing page builder as part of its Marketing Hub. Their own pages are clean, simple, and highly effective at converting visitors into leads.

Pricing

  • Free Plan: $0 (basic landing pages with HubSpot branding)
  • Starter Plan: $45/month (remove branding, more templates)
  • Professional Plan: $800/month (advanced features)

Key Features

  • Drag-and-drop builder with responsive templates
  • Built-in CRM integration (free)
  • Personalized content based on contact data
  • A/B testing and analytics on paid plans
  • Smart forms that adapt to user behavior

Pros

  • Free tier is excellent for startups and side projects
  • Seamless integration with HubSpot CRM and email tools
  • Smart content personalization boosts conversion rates

Cons

  • Free pages have HubSpot branding
